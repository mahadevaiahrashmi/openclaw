# Vision Narrative

## Purpose
Craft a compelling vision story that paints the future state, explains why it matters, charts the path to get there, and articulates what's at stake if we don't act. Written in narrative format — not bullet points — designed to inspire, align, and create urgency. This is the document people remember and rally around.

## Auto-Trigger Patterns
- "Write a vision narrative" / "product vision story"
- "Help me articulate our vision"
- "Create a compelling vision for [product/initiative]"
- "Paint the picture of where we're going"

## Inputs

**Zero-setup:** Only the user prompt is required. If context files are empty, use `context/_defaults.md` and label assumptions. See `skills/_GLOBAL-BEHAVIOR.md`.

- **Required:** Product/company context, target future state, key customer insights, strategic direction
- **Optional:** Existing mission/vision statements, competitive context, founder's intent, cultural values
- **Reference:** Narrative memo framework, `context/initiatives/` for strategic direction

## Process
1. **Start with the world, not the product** — What's happening in the market, industry, or society that creates the opening? Why now?
2. **Name the problem vividly** — Make the reader feel the pain of the current state. Use specific customer stories or scenarios.
3. **Paint the future** — Describe the world after your vision is realized. Be specific: what does a customer's day look like? What's different?
4. **Articulate the stakes** — What happens if we don't pursue this? What do we lose? What do competitors gain?
5. **Chart the path** — High-level phases from here to there. Not a roadmap — a journey narrative with milestones.
6. **Ground it** — Connect the vision to current capabilities, early proof points, and the team's unique ability to execute.
7. **Close with a call** — What does this vision demand of the team? What mindset, commitment, or change is required?
8. **Read it aloud** — Vision narratives must sound inspiring when spoken. Edit for rhythm, energy, and clarity.

## Output Format
```
## [Vision Title]

### The World Is Changing
[2-3 paragraphs: What's shifting in the market/industry/society that creates
this opportunity. Why this moment matters. Set the stage.]

### The Problem We See
[1-2 paragraphs: Vivid description of the current pain. Specific customer
scenarios. Make the reader feel it.]

### The Future We're Building
[2-3 paragraphs: Paint the future state. What does the customer's world look
like? What becomes possible? Be specific and human.]

### What's at Stake
[1 paragraph: The cost of inaction. What we lose. What competitors gain.
The urgency.]

### How We Get There
[2-3 paragraphs: The journey — phases, milestones, key unlocks. Not a
detailed roadmap but a narrative path.]

### Why Us, Why Now
[1-2 paragraphs: What makes this team uniquely positioned. Early proof
points. Capabilities that matter.]

### The Call
[1 paragraph: What this demands. The commitment. The inspiration.]
```

## Quality Standards
- Written in narrative prose — no bullet points, no tables. This is a story.
- Future state is specific enough to be vivid: "A customer can..." not "We will enable..."
- Grounded in reality — connected to current capabilities and evidence, not pure fantasy.
- Creates genuine urgency — the reader should feel that not acting is a choice with consequences.
- **Anti-patterns:** Bullet-point lists disguised as vision, generic aspirations ("be the leader in..."), no customer perspective, all aspiration with no grounding, vision that sounds like a feature list.

## Framework References
- Amazon narrative memo format — write-up first, discuss second
- Simon Sinek's Start With Why — purpose before product
- Narrative arc — setup, tension, resolution, call to action

## Formatting Guidelines
- Pure prose — paragraphs, not bullets. This is a narrative document.
- 2-4 pages. Long enough to be compelling, short enough to be read.
- Use headers as chapter titles, not labels. They should flow as a story.
- Write for reading aloud — short sentences mixed with longer ones. Rhythm matters.

## Example
> **The World Is Changing:** Every B2B company is becoming a platform company. The ones that open their data and workflows to developers will compound growth. The ones that don't will watch their customers build on top of more open competitors.
>
> **The Future We're Building:** Imagine a product manager at a Series B startup. She connects our platform to her existing tools in an afternoon — not weeks. She configures her onboarding flow without writing a ticket. Her developers extend it with three API calls. By the end of the quarter, her entire growth stack runs through us. Not because we replaced everything — but because we connected everything.
>
> **What's at Stake:** Two competitors are already building toward this future. If we wait for perfect, we'll arrive at a market that's already been claimed. The window is 18 months.
