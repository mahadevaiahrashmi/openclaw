# Executive Coaching Prep

## Purpose
Prepare the PM for executive coaching conversations — whether with a formal coach, a mentor, or a skip-level leader. Reflect on recent performance, identify growth areas, prepare specific examples, set a focused coaching agenda, and define commitments. Maximize the value of limited coaching time through structured preparation.

## Auto-Trigger Patterns
- "Prep for my coaching session"
- "Executive coaching prep"
- "What should I discuss with my coach"
- "Help me reflect on my performance"
- "Coaching conversation prep"
- "Mentor meeting preparation"

## Inputs

**Zero-setup:** Only the user prompt is required. If context files are empty, use `context/_defaults.md` and label assumptions. See `skills/_GLOBAL-BEHAVIOR.md`.

- **Coach/mentor identity** (optional) — who the PM is meeting with and their style
- **Recent performance context** (required) — wins, challenges, feedback received, key decisions made
- **Growth goals** (optional) — areas the PM is developing, career aspirations
- **Previous coaching notes** (optional) — commitments from last session, themes being tracked
- **Organizational context** (optional) — current role dynamics, upcoming opportunities or challenges

## Process
1. **Reflect on recent period** — structured self-assessment:
   - Wins: what went well and why
   - Challenges: what was hard and why
   - Decisions: key choices made and outcomes
   - Feedback: what others have said (formal and informal)
   - Surprises: what caught the PM off guard
2. **Identify patterns** — recurring themes across wins, challenges, and feedback
3. **Assess against growth goals** — progress on stated development areas
4. **Select coaching focus** — 1-2 topics for this session (depth over breadth)
5. **Prepare specific examples** — concrete situations to discuss, not abstractions
6. **Draft coaching questions** — what the PM wants the coach's perspective on
7. **Review previous commitments** — what was promised last time, what happened
8. **Define desired outcomes** — what the PM wants to leave the session with

## Quality Standards
- Self-reflection is honest, not performative — includes genuine challenges
- Patterns analysis goes beyond listing events to identifying behavioral themes
- Coaching questions are specific and require coach's expertise, not validation
- Previous commitments are honestly assessed
- **Anti-patterns**: Using coaching time for venting without reflection; bringing too many topics; only discussing wins; generic questions that don't require a coach

## Framework References
- Reflective practice (Schön)
- Coaching GROW model for structuring discussions
- Johari Window for self-awareness development
- Leadership competency frameworks for growth areas

## Formatting Guidelines
- Self-reflection as structured prompts, not free-form journaling
- Growth goal progress as tracked table
- Session focus limited to 1-2 topics with prepared examples
- Post-session commitments as blank checklist to fill during/after

## Example
Coaching prep: "Pattern noticed: I keep over-indexing on stakeholder alignment at the expense of speed — I seek consensus when I have the authority to decide. Example: spent 2 weeks getting 5 people aligned on API design when my VP said 'just make the call.' Question for coach: 'How do I calibrate when to seek alignment vs when to decide and inform? What signals should I watch for?'"
