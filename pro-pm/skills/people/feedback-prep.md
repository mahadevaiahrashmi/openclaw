# Feedback Prep

## Purpose
Prepare the PM to give constructive feedback using the SBI (Situation-Behavior-Impact) framework, or prepare to receive feedback effectively. For giving feedback: structure the message for clarity and impact while preserving the relationship. For receiving: prepare an active listening approach and follow-up plan.

## Auto-Trigger Patterns
- "Help me give feedback to…"
- "Prepare feedback for…"
- "I need to have a difficult conversation with…"
- "How do I tell [name] that…"
- "I'm getting feedback from [name], help me prepare"
- "SBI feedback for…"

## Inputs

**Zero-setup:** Only the user prompt is required. If context files are empty, use `context/_defaults.md` and label assumptions. See `skills/_GLOBAL-BEHAVIOR.md`.

- **Direction** (required) — giving or receiving feedback
- **Person** (required) — who is the feedback about/from
- **Persona** (optional) — `context/people/[name]/persona.md`
- **Situation details** (required for giving) — what happened, when, where, observable behavior
- **Desired outcome** (optional) — what change the PM hopes to see

## Process
### Giving Feedback
1. **Clarify intent** — is this developmental, corrective, or reinforcing feedback?
2. **Apply SBI framework**:
   - **Situation**: specific time and place (not "you always…")
   - **Behavior**: observable action (not interpretation or motive)
   - **Impact**: effect on you, the team, the project, or the person themselves
3. **Adapt to persona** — adjust tone, setting, and framing based on how this person receives feedback
4. **Prepare the conversation**:
   - Opening: set context and intent
   - SBI delivery: clear and specific
   - Listen: space for their perspective
   - Collaborate: agree on path forward
   - Close: reaffirm relationship and support
5. **Anticipate reactions** — defensive, dismissive, emotional, grateful
6. **Plan follow-up** — how to reinforce the conversation

### Receiving Feedback
1. **Prepare mentally** — separate identity from behavior, assume positive intent
2. **Active listening plan** — questions to ask, how to avoid defensiveness
3. **Processing framework** — how to evaluate the feedback objectively
4. **Follow-up plan** — how to act on what's useful, how to address what's inaccurate

## Output Format
```markdown
# Feedback Prep: [Giving/Receiving] — [Person]
**Type**: [Developmental / Corrective / Reinforcing]
**Setting**: [Recommended time and place]

## SBI Statement (for giving)
### Situation
"In [specific meeting/context] on [date]…"

### Behavior
"I observed that you [specific observable action]…"

### Impact
"The effect was [impact on team/project/person]…"

## Conversation Script
1. **Open**: "[Set context and caring intent]"
2. **Deliver SBI**: [Statement above]
3. **Listen**: "I'd like to hear your perspective…"
4. **Collaborate**: "What could we do differently going forward?"
5. **Close**: "[Reaffirm support and next steps]"

## Persona Adaptation
- **Their feedback style**: [from persona — how they prefer to receive feedback]
- **Adjust**: [specific adaptations]

## Anticipated Reactions & Responses
| Reaction | Response |
|----------|----------|

## Do's and Don'ts
| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Be specific with examples | Generalize ("you always…") |
| Focus on behavior, not character | Attribute motives |
| Allow their perspective | Monologue |

## Follow-Up Plan
- Within 24 hours: …
- Within 1 week: …
- Ongoing: …

---

## Receiving Feedback Prep (if applicable)
### Active Listening Checklist
- [ ] Listen fully before responding
- [ ] Ask clarifying questions
- [ ] Thank them for the feedback
- [ ] Ask for specific examples

### Processing Framework
- What resonates and why?
- What surprises me and why?
- What do I disagree with and why?
- What will I do with this?

### Follow-Up Actions
- [ ] Reflect for 24 hours before acting
- [ ] Identify 1-2 concrete changes
- [ ] Schedule check-in to show progress
```

## Quality Standards
- SBI statement uses truly observable behavior, not interpretations
- Impact is stated in terms the recipient cares about
- Conversation script sounds natural, not robotic
- Persona adaptation is specific (not "be sensitive")
- **Anti-patterns**: Sandwich method (positive-negative-positive dilutes the message); vague behavior descriptions; making it about character not actions

## Framework References
- SBI (Situation-Behavior-Impact) — Center for Creative Leadership
- Radical Candor (Care Personally + Challenge Directly)
- Nonviolent Communication (observation, feeling, need, request)

## Formatting Guidelines
- SBI statement in clear, separated sections
- Conversation script as numbered steps for rehearsal
- Do/Don't table for quick guardrails
- Keep to 1 page for giving, half page for receiving

## Example
Giving feedback: "Situation: In Tuesday's sprint planning, Behavior: you committed the team to a deadline without consulting the engineers first, Impact: the team felt unheard and two engineers expressed frustration afterward, reducing their engagement. Let's discuss how to include the team in estimation going forward." Persona note: this person responds best to private, data-backed feedback — schedule a 1:1, not in group setting.
