# All-Hands Talking Points

## Purpose
Prepare structured speaker notes and key messages for company or team all-hands presentations. Focuses on narrative arc, supporting data, illustrative stories, transition phrases, and anticipated Q&A. Designed as speaker notes — not slide content — so the presenter can deliver with confidence and coherence.

## Auto-Trigger Patterns
- "Prep my all-hands talking points" / "all-hands notes"
- "What should I say at the all-hands?"
- "Help me prepare for the team meeting presentation"
- "Key messages for the company update"

## Inputs

**Zero-setup:** Only the user prompt is required. If context files are empty, use `context/_defaults.md` and label assumptions. See `skills/_GLOBAL-BEHAVIOR.md`.

- **Required:** Topic or theme, key messages to land, audience (company-wide, team, department)
- **Optional:** Recent wins/metrics, strategic context, sensitive topics to navigate, time allocation
- **Reference:** `context/initiatives/` for initiative status, `context/people/` for audience composition

## Process
1. **Define the narrative arc** — What's the one thing the audience should remember? Build everything around that throughline.
2. **Identify key themes** — Group messages into 3-4 themes max. More than 4 and nothing sticks.
3. **Gather data points** — 2-3 compelling numbers per theme. Choose metrics that tell a story, not just report status.
4. **Find stories** — One customer story, team story, or example per theme. Stories make data memorable.
5. **Write transitions** — Explicit phrases that connect sections. "That customer win leads me to..." not awkward jumps.
6. **Prep Q&A** — Anticipate the 5-7 hardest questions. Draft concise, honest answers. Identify questions to redirect.
7. **Add timing** — Allocate minutes per section. All-hands always run long — build in buffer.
8. **Tone check** — Match the moment: celebratory, rallying, transparent about challenges, forward-looking.

## Output Format
```
## All-Hands Talking Points: [Topic/Theme]
**Date:** [Date] | **Audience:** [Who] | **Total Time:** [X min]

### Opening ([X] min)
- **Hook:** [Attention-grabbing opener — story, stat, or question]
- **Throughline:** [The one thing you want them to remember]
- **Agenda preview:** [Brief roadmap of what you'll cover]

### Theme 1: [Theme Name] ([X] min)
- **Key message:** [One sentence]
- **Data point:** [Metric with context]
- **Story/Example:** [Brief narrative]
- **Transition to next:** "[Transition phrase]"

### Theme 2: [Theme Name] ([X] min)
[Same structure]

### Theme 3: [Theme Name] ([X] min)
[Same structure]

### Close ([X] min)
- **Callback:** [Connect back to opening]
- **Forward look:** [What's next, what to be excited about]
- **Call to action:** [What you want people to do]

### Q&A Prep
| Likely Question | Answer | If Pressed |
|----------------|--------|------------|
| ...            | ...    | ...        |
```

## Quality Standards
- One clear throughline that the audience can repeat back. If there's no throughline, it's a status update, not a talk.
- Stories are specific and human, not generic. "A customer told us..." beats "customers are happy."
- Data points are contextualized — "42% activation, up from 28% when we started this initiative."
- Transitions are explicit — the audience should never wonder "why are we talking about this now?"
- **Anti-patterns:** Reading slides aloud, cramming too many themes, no stories (all data), skipping Q&A prep, no clear ask or forward look.

## Framework References
- Narrative arc (Setup → Conflict → Resolution)
- Rule of Three — three themes maximum for retention
- Assertion-Evidence structure for data points

## Formatting Guidelines
- Write as speaker notes — conversational language, not formal prose.
- Use bold for key phrases the speaker should emphasize.
- Include timing for each section. Talks always run over — plan for it.
- Q&A table format for quick reference during live Q&A.

## Example
> **Hook:** "Three months ago, we sat in this room and I asked — can we hit 10K active users by summer? Today I can tell you: we hit it last Tuesday."
>
> **Theme 1 — Growth is working:** DAU crossed 10K (↑47% QoQ). The activation redesign was the unlock — Day-1 retention went from 23% to 38%.
>
> **Story:** "Let me tell you about a customer named Priya at Meridian Health..."
>
> **Transition:** "That growth is exciting, but it's also surfacing our next challenge..."
