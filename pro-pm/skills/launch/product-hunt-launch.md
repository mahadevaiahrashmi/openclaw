# Product Hunt Launch

## Purpose
Plan and execute a Product Hunt launch — covering timing strategy, crafting the tagline and description, writing the first comment, selecting the right hunter, coordinating the team for launch day, planning community engagement, and designing the follow-up strategy. Maximizes visibility and converts attention into users.

## Process
1. **Choose launch timing** — Best days: Tuesday-Thursday. Best time: 12:01 AM PT (full day of visibility). Avoid major holidays, big tech announcements, and days with known major launches.
2. **Select or prepare the hunter** — High-follower hunter increases visibility. Options: self-submit, ask a known hunter, or leverage community connections. Outreach 2-3 weeks early.
3. **Craft the tagline** — Max 60 characters. Must be instantly clear and compelling. Formula: [What it does] + [for whom / why it's different].
4. **Write the description** — Concise product description with key features and differentiators. Include media: product screenshots, demo GIF/video, maker story.
5. **Write the first comment** — The maker's story: why you built this, who it's for, what makes it special, what you're looking for (feedback, users, collaborators). Authentic tone.
6. **Prepare visual assets** — Gallery images, logo, demo video/GIF. First image is the thumbnail — make it count.
7. **Plan team coordination** — Who's online at launch (12 AM PT), response schedule, who handles comments, who shares on social. Stagger coverage across time zones.
8. **Design engagement strategy** — Respond to every comment. Share on social, communities, newsletters. Engage authentically — no vote manipulation.
9. **Plan follow-up** — Convert PH traffic: onboarding flow, special offer for PH users, follow-up email sequence, retarget engaged visitors.

## Quality Standards
- Tagline is under 60 characters and immediately communicates value.
- First comment is authentic and personal — not corporate marketing speak.
- Team coverage ensures every comment gets a response within 30 minutes.
- Follow-up strategy converts attention into lasting users, not just one-day traffic.

**Anti-patterns:** Vote manipulation (against PH rules), generic marketing copy, no team coordination, launching on Friday/weekend, no follow-up plan.
