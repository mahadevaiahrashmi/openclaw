# Early Adopter Program

## Purpose
Design an early adopter program that creates a virtuous cycle — recruiting the right users, engaging them meaningfully, collecting actionable feedback, providing incentives that align interests, and graduating participants into advocates. Builds a foundation of engaged users and evidence before broader launch.

## Auto-Trigger Patterns
- "early adopter program for [product]"
- "design an early access program"
- "early adopter recruitment"
- "build a user community for [product]"
- "founder program design"
- "early access plan for [feature]"

## Process
1. **Define program goals** — What do you need from early adopters? Product feedback, usage data, testimonials, case studies, word-of-mouth, product-market fit signal? Goals shape the entire program.
2. **Set recruitment criteria** — Who's the ideal early adopter? Balance: sophistication (can articulate needs), representativeness (reflects target market), influence (can amplify), commitment (will actually engage).
3. **Design engagement model** — How do early adopters interact with you? Community (Slack/Discord), 1:1 calls, surveys, office hours, early feature previews. Match to goals and scale.
4. **Build feedback loops** — Structured methods: in-app surveys, interview cadence, usage analytics, feature request voting. Close the loop — show adopters their feedback is heard.
5. **Create incentive structure** — What early adopters get: free/discounted access, exclusive features, direct product team access, recognition, influence over roadmap. Align incentives with engagement goals.
6. **Design graduation path** — How early adopters transition: to paying customers, to advocates, to advisory board members. Don't leave them in limbo.
7. **Plan communication cadence** — Regular updates: what's changed based on feedback, upcoming features, community highlights. Keep engagement high without being noisy.
8. **Set success metrics** — How to measure program effectiveness: activation, retention, NPS, feedback quality, testimonials generated, referrals.

## Quality Standards
- Recruitment criteria ensure participants represent the target market, not just enthusiasts.
- Feedback loops are closed — adopters see their input reflected in the product.
- Incentives align with engagement goals — don't pay for passive participation.
- Graduation path is defined — no one is left wondering what happens next.

**Anti-patterns:** Recruiting too many (can't engage meaningfully), no feedback structure, one-way communication, indefinite programs without milestones, losing early adopters at GA transition.

This week we shipped a redesigned export with step-by-step guidance and format preview. Your feedback directly shaped this — thank you. Here's what's next on our list based on your input...'"
