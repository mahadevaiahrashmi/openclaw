# Social Media — Product Hunt

## Purpose
Creates a complete Product Hunt launch package: tagline, product description, maker's first comment (telling the origin story), community engagement plan, and post-launch follow-up strategy. Optimized for Product Hunt's unique community norms and voting dynamics.

## Auto-Trigger Patterns
- "Prepare a Product Hunt launch for [product]"
- "Write Product Hunt copy"
- "I need a Product Hunt launch plan"
- "Help me launch on Product Hunt"

## Inputs

**Zero-setup:** Only the user prompt is required. If context files are empty, use `context/_defaults.md` and label assumptions. See `skills/_GLOBAL-BEHAVIOR.md`.

- **Required:** Product name, one-line description, key features (3-5), origin story, what makes it unique
- **Optional:** Existing user count, beta results, pricing, launch timing strategy, hunter contacts
- **Reference files:** product-brief.md, landing-page-copy.md, blog-post-announcement.md

## Process
1. **Craft the tagline** — Under 60 characters. Describes the value, not the product category.
2. **Write the description** — First paragraph hooks. Features as benefits. Transparent about stage.
3. **Write the maker comment** — Personal, authentic origin story. Why you built this. What you learned.
4. **Plan engagement** — Respond to every comment within 2 hours. Prepare answers to likely questions.
5. **Design follow-up** — Thank supporters. Share results. Offer launch-day deals.

## Output Format
```
# Product Hunt Launch: [Product Name]

## Tagline (60 chars max)
[Benefit-focused, clear, intriguing]

## Description
### Paragraph 1: Hook
[What it does. Why it matters. One surprising detail.]

### Paragraph 2: Key Features
🔹 [Feature 1 as benefit]
🔹 [Feature 2 as benefit]
🔹 [Feature 3 as benefit]

### Paragraph 3: Who It's For
[Target user. Use case. Why now.]

### Paragraph 4: Offer
[Launch-day pricing, free tier, or special offer.]

## First Comment (Maker's Story)
Hey Product Hunt! 👋

I'm [Name], [role] at [Company].

[Why I built this — personal story, 2-3 paragraphs.
The problem I experienced. What I tried first.
The moment I decided to build this.]

[What makes this different. Honest about what's great and what's coming.]

[Ask: What would you want to see next? Happy to answer questions.]

## Engagement Plan
- **Pre-launch:** [Community seeding, teaser posts, hunter outreach]
- **Launch day:** [Response cadence, team roles, social amplification]
- **Common Q&A:** [Prepared answers for expected questions]
- **Post-launch:** [Thank you post, results sharing, offer follow-through]
```

## Quality Standards
- Tagline is instantly understandable—no jargon, no buzzwords.
- Maker's comment feels authentic and personal, not corporate.
- Every community comment gets a thoughtful response, not a canned reply.
- **Anti-patterns:** Corporate-speak, asking for upvotes, ignoring comments, no personal story, fake urgency.

## Audience
Product Hunt community: early adopters, indie hackers, developers, designers, and investors who value authenticity, innovation, and maker stories. They'll support genuine products but are allergic to corporate marketing.

## Formatting Guidelines
- Tagline: under 60 characters. Description: 3-4 short paragraphs.
- Use bullet emojis for features. Keep paragraphs short.
- Maker comment: 200-400 words. First person, conversational.
- Include product screenshots/GIF in the listing.

## Example
**Tagline:** "Find anything across all your work tools in 2 seconds"
**Maker comment opening:** "Hey PH! I'm Sarah, and I built this because I was spending 45 minutes a day just searching for things across Notion, Slack, Google Drive, and Jira. I tried building a personal script to solve it, then my teammates wanted it, then their teammates wanted it... and here we are."
