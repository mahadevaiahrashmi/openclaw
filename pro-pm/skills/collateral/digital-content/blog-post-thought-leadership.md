# Blog Post — Thought Leadership

## Purpose
Creates a 1000-1500 word industry perspective blog post built around a provocative thesis, supported by evidence, with counterarguments addressed and a clear call to action. Positions the author and company as credible voices in the industry.

## Auto-Trigger Patterns
- "Write a thought leadership post on [topic]"
- "Create an opinion piece about [industry trend]"
- "I need a perspective blog post on [subject]"
- "Help me write a contrarian take on [topic]"

## Inputs

**Zero-setup:** Only the user prompt is required. If context files are empty, use `context/_defaults.md` and label assumptions. See `skills/_GLOBAL-BEHAVIOR.md`.

- **Required:** Topic, thesis/perspective, 2-3 supporting evidence points, author voice/expertise
- **Optional:** Counterarguments to address, industry data, personal stories, related company work
- **Reference files:** product-brief.md, whitepaper.md, market-research-report.md

## Process
1. **Sharpen the thesis** — What do you believe that most people don't? What's your contrarian or insightful take?
2. **Build the evidence** — Support with data, examples, and reasoning. Not opinion alone.
3. **Address counterarguments** — Steelman the opposing view, then explain why your position holds.
4. **Connect to the reader** — Why should they care? What should they do differently?
5. **End with conviction** — Don't hedge. Take a clear position.
6. **Optimize for sharing** — Title and opening should make someone want to share on LinkedIn.

## Output Format
```
# [Provocative, Shareable Title]

**Meta description:** [155 chars, captures the thesis]

[Opening: Story, surprising data point, or bold statement that hooks]

## [The Conventional Wisdom]
[What most people believe. Why it's understandable. Set up the tension.]

## [The Problem with That View]
[Evidence that the conventional wisdom is wrong or incomplete.
Data points. Examples. Industry trends.]

## [A Better Way to Think About This]
[Your thesis, fully articulated. Framework or mental model.
Why this matters now.]

## [The Counterargument (and Why It Falls Short)]
[Steelman the objection. Address it honestly. Explain why your view holds.]

## [What This Means for You]
[Practical implications. What should the reader do differently?]

## [Closing: Strong final statement]
[Restate thesis with conviction. CTA: share, discuss, explore.]
```

## Quality Standards
- The thesis is genuinely interesting—not a restatement of conventional wisdom.
- Evidence is specific and cited, not "studies show" hand-waving.
- The counterargument section demonstrates intellectual honesty.
- **Anti-patterns:** Thesis that nobody would disagree with, product pitch disguised as thought leadership, no evidence, hedging the conclusion.

## Audience
Industry practitioners, leaders, and peers who follow the topic. They want to be challenged, learn something new, or find language for something they've felt but couldn't articulate. They'll share content that makes them look thoughtful.

## Formatting Guidelines
- 1000-1500 words. Strong opening, clear structure, conviction ending.
- Headers frame the argument progression.
- Pull quotes for the most shareable lines.
- Author byline with credentials that establish authority on the topic.

## Example
**Opening:** "The security industry has spent twenty years telling companies to 'shift left.' Here's the uncomfortable truth: most organizations that shifted left just moved the same problems to a different stage of the pipeline. The tools changed. The culture didn't. And until we address that gap, shifting left is shifting deck chairs."
