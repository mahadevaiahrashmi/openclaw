# Investor Pitch Deck (Product Section)

## Purpose
Crafts the 5-8 slide product section of an investor pitch deck, covering market opportunity, product vision, traction, competitive moats, and growth strategy. Designed to build investor confidence in the product as the engine of company value creation.

## Auto-Trigger Patterns
- "Create the product section for our investor deck"
- "Build investor-facing product slides"
- "Help me present our product to investors/VCs"
- "I need product slides for a fundraise"

## Inputs

**Zero-setup:** Only the user prompt is required. If context files are empty, use `context/_defaults.md` and label assumptions. See `skills/_GLOBAL-BEHAVIOR.md`.

- **Required:** Product description, market size (TAM/SAM/SOM), key metrics (ARR, growth rate, retention), competitive landscape, growth levers
- **Optional:** Product demo or screenshots, customer logos, unit economics, technology architecture, team bios
- **Reference files:** product-brief.md, strategy-document.md, business-case.md

## Process
1. **Frame the opportunity** — Quantify the market with credible sources. Show why now.
2. **Show, don't tell** — Use product visuals and metrics over claims. Every assertion backed by data.
3. **Articulate moats** — Identify defensible advantages: network effects, data advantages, switching costs, technology, brand.
4. **Map the growth path** — Show how product drives revenue expansion: new users, expansion, new segments.
5. **Pressure-test** — Ask "would I invest?" after each slide. Remove anything that doesn't build conviction.

## Output Format
```
# [Company] — Product Overview for Investors

## Slide 1: Market Opportunity
TAM/SAM/SOM with sources. Market growth rate. Why this market, why now.

## Slide 2: Product Vision
One-sentence vision. Product screenshot/visual. Core value proposition.

## Slide 3: How It Works
Simplified product flow. Key user experience moments. "Magic" differentiator.

## Slide 4: Traction
Key metrics: users, revenue, growth rate, retention, NPS.
Milestone timeline. Customer logos.

## Slide 5: Competitive Moats
Positioning vs alternatives. Defensibility framework.
Why hard to replicate.

## Slide 6: Growth Strategy
Current → near-term → long-term expansion path.
Growth levers with projected impact.

## Slide 7: Product Roadmap (High Level)
Next 12-18 months. Key unlocks and their revenue impact.
```

## Quality Standards
- Market sizing uses bottom-up methodology, not just top-down TAM assertions.
- Metrics are specific and verifiable—no vague "strong growth."
- Moats are articulated honestly; investors see through fluff.
- **Anti-patterns:** Feature lists, unsubstantiated TAM claims, missing retention data, no competitive acknowledgment.

## Audience
Investors (VC partners, board members, potential acquirers) evaluating product-market fit, scalability, and defensibility. They care about market size, growth trajectory, unit economics, and whether the product creates durable competitive advantage.

## Formatting Guidelines
- 5-8 slides maximum. High information density per slide.
- Metrics front and center with clear trend indicators.
- One powerful product visual or screenshot per relevant slide.
- Minimal text; let data and visuals carry the narrative.

## Example
**Slide 4: Traction**
Visual: Growth curve chart (MRR over 18 months, hockey stick).
Key metrics in bold: "$2.4M ARR | 3.2x YoY Growth | 95% Net Retention | 62 NPS"
Customer logos row: [6-8 recognizable logos]
Caption: "500+ teams across financial services, healthcare, and technology."
