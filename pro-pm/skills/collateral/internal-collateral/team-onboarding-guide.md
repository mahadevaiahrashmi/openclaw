# Team Onboarding Guide

## Purpose
Creates a new PM team member onboarding plan covering first day, first week, first month, and first quarter activities. Includes key resources to review, people to meet, product areas to learn, and milestone expectations that help new PMs reach full productivity quickly.

## Auto-Trigger Patterns
- "Create a PM onboarding guide"
- "Build an onboarding plan for a new PM"
- "I need to onboard a new product manager"
- "Help me design a new hire PM experience"

## Inputs

**Zero-setup:** Only the user prompt is required. If context files are empty, use `context/_defaults.md` and label assumptions. See `skills/_GLOBAL-BEHAVIOR.md`.

- **Required:** Team structure, product areas, key stakeholders, essential tools and systems
- **Optional:** Existing onboarding materials, new hire feedback from past onboarding, buddy system, learning resources
- **Reference files:** product-charter.md, process-documentation.md, knowledge-base-article.md

## Process
1. **Pre-arrival preparation** — Access, equipment, buddy assignment, welcome message.
2. **Day 1 experience** — Warm welcome, logistics, team introductions, first orientation.
3. **Week 1 immersion** — Product walkthrough, key stakeholder meetings, context reading.
4. **Month 1 contribution** — First deliverable, attend key ceremonies, shadow experienced PMs.
5. **Quarter 1 independence** — Own an area, deliver a meaningful output, receive feedback.
6. **Gather feedback** — Ask every new hire to improve the onboarding for the next person.

## Output Format
```
# Welcome to the [Team Name] Product Team!

## Before Day 1 (Manager's Prep)
- [ ] Access to tools: [list of tools and systems]
- [ ] Buddy assigned: [Name]
- [ ] Welcome message sent
- [ ] First week calendar pre-populated
- [ ] Onboarding reading list sent

## Day 1: Welcome & Orientation
- [ ] Team welcome (coffee/lunch)
- [ ] Workspace and tools setup
- [ ] Buddy introduction and expectations
- [ ] Product walkthrough (30 min guided tour)
- [ ] Review: team charter and operating principles

## Week 1: Immersion
- [ ] Meet your key stakeholders (1:1s scheduled)
  | Person | Role | Why They Matter |
- [ ] Product deep-dive sessions (2-3 hours total)
- [ ] Read key documents: [list of 5-7 essential docs]
- [ ] Attend team standup and planning meetings (observe)
- [ ] Complete product onboarding as a new user

## Week 2-4: Context Building
- [ ] Shadow 2-3 customer calls or support sessions
- [ ] Review last quarter's roadmap and OKRs
- [ ] Attend a sprint review and retrospective
- [ ] First small deliverable: [specific, achievable task]
- [ ] 1:1 with manager: Week 2 check-in

## Month 2-3: Contributing
- [ ] Own a defined product area or initiative
- [ ] Lead a meeting (planning, design review, or stakeholder update)
- [ ] Complete first significant deliverable: [type]
- [ ] Monthly 1:1s with skip-level and key cross-functional partners
- [ ] 30-day feedback session with manager

## Quarter 1 Milestone
- [ ] Full ownership of [area/initiative]
- [ ] Delivered [specific output]
- [ ] 90-day review with manager

## Essential Reading List
| Document | Why It Matters | Priority |
|----------|---------------|----------|

## People to Meet (First 30 Days)
| Name | Role | Context to Discuss |
|------|------|-------------------|

## Tools & Access
| Tool | Purpose | Access Request |
|------|---------|---------------|
```

## Quality Standards
- A new PM should feel productive by end of Week 1 and genuinely contributing by end of Month 1.
- The plan is specific—not "learn the product" but "complete these 3 tutorials and present findings."
- People-to-meet list includes context on what to discuss with each person.
- **Anti-patterns:** Information dump without structure, no buddy system, no first deliverable, unclear expectations, no feedback loops.

## Audience
New PM team members and their managers. The onboarding guide should make the new hire feel supported and the manager feel confident the process is comprehensive.

## Formatting Guidelines
- Checklist-driven with clear timelines.
- Organized by time period: Day 1, Week 1, Month 1, Quarter 1.
- Include links to all referenced documents and tools.
- Living document updated after each new hire's feedback.

## Example
**Week 1 meeting:** "Meet Alex Chen (Engineering Lead, Platform) — Context: Alex leads the platform team you'll collaborate with most. Discuss: current sprint priorities, how product and engineering work together on this team, their perspective on the biggest technical constraints. Ask: 'What do you wish PMs on this team understood better about the platform?'"
