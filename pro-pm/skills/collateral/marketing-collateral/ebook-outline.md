# Ebook Outline

## Purpose
Creates a structured multi-chapter educational content plan with chapter structure, key messages per chapter, visual needs, CTAs per chapter, and distribution strategy. Serves as the blueprint for producing a high-quality ebook that generates leads and establishes thought leadership.

## Auto-Trigger Patterns
- "Create an ebook outline on [topic]"
- "Plan an ebook for [audience/topic]"
- "I need a content plan for a multi-chapter ebook"
- "Help me structure an educational ebook"

## Inputs

**Zero-setup:** Only the user prompt is required. If context files are empty, use `context/_defaults.md` and label assumptions. See `skills/_GLOBAL-BEHAVIOR.md`.

- **Required:** Topic, target audience, key thesis or perspective, desired length (chapters)
- **Optional:** Existing content to repurpose, subject matter experts, competitive ebooks, design preferences
- **Reference files:** product-brief.md, whitepaper.md, blog-post-thought-leadership.md

## Process
1. **Define the reader's journey** — What does the reader believe before? What should they believe after?
2. **Structure chapters as a narrative** — Each chapter builds on the previous, leading to a conclusion.
3. **Assign key messages** — Each chapter has 1-2 key takeaways that support the overall thesis.
4. **Plan visuals** — Identify diagrams, charts, and illustrations needed per chapter.
5. **Design CTAs** — Progressive CTAs: early chapters educate, later chapters convert.
6. **Plan distribution** — How the ebook will be promoted, gated, and measured.

## Output Format
```
# Ebook Outline: [Title]

## Overview
- **Target audience:** [Who and what they care about]
- **Thesis:** [The central argument or perspective]
- **Length:** [X chapters, ~Y pages]
- **Tone:** [Educational, provocative, practical, etc.]

## Chapter Structure

### Chapter 1: [Title]
- **Key message:** [What the reader should take away]
- **Content outline:** [3-5 section descriptions]
- **Visual needs:** [Diagrams, charts, images described]
- **CTA:** [Chapter-appropriate next step]

### Chapter 2-N: [Same structure]

### Conclusion: [Title]
- **Key message:** [Thesis reinforced with evidence summary]
- **CTA:** [Primary conversion CTA]

## Production Plan
- **Content sources:** [Existing content to repurpose, SME interviews needed]
- **Design requirements:** [Style guide, template, illustration needs]
- **Timeline:** [Draft → review → design → publish dates]

## Distribution Plan
- **Gating strategy:** [Full gate, partial gate, ungated]
- **Promotion channels:** [Email, social, paid, partnerships]
- **Measurement:** [Downloads, reads, conversions, pipeline attributed]
```

## Quality Standards
- Each chapter delivers standalone value—readable independently.
- The narrative arc across chapters builds a compelling argument.
- Visual needs are specific enough for a designer to execute.
- **Anti-patterns:** Chapters that are thinly veiled product pitches, no narrative arc, vague visual needs, no distribution plan.

## Audience
Marketing and content teams who will produce the ebook, and the end reader who is a practitioner or leader seeking education on the topic. The outline should be specific enough for a writer to draft from.

## Formatting Guidelines
- 5-8 chapters recommended. Each chapter 4-8 pages in final form.
- Include a "reader's journey" diagram showing the progression.
- CTAs escalate: awareness → consideration → decision.
- Plan for both digital (PDF) and web-based versions.

## Example
**Chapter 3 outline:** "Chapter 3: 'Why Traditional Approaches Are Failing' — Key message: Legacy tools were built for a world that no longer exists. Content: (1) The three assumptions legacy tools make that are now false, (2) Real-world failure examples with anonymized data, (3) The cost of maintaining legacy approaches, (4) Signs it's time to evolve. Visual needs: Timeline showing technology evolution vs tool evolution gap. CTA: Self-assessment checklist—'Is your current approach at risk?'"
