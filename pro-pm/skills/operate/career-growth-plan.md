# Career Growth Plan

## Purpose
Build a structured PM career growth plan: assess current level, define target level, identify skill gaps, plan development activities, find mentorship, create visibility opportunities, and set milestone checkpoints. Transform vague career aspirations into a concrete, trackable development roadmap.

## Auto-Trigger Patterns
- "Help me grow my career"
- "Career development plan"
- "How do I get to [next level]"
- "PM growth plan"
- "What skills should I develop"
- "Career progression for PM"

## Inputs

**Zero-setup:** Only the user prompt is required. If context files are empty, use `context/_defaults.md` and label assumptions. See `skills/_GLOBAL-BEHAVIOR.md`.

- **Current level** (required) — title, scope, tenure at level
- **Target level** (optional) — desired next role or level
- **Self-assessment** (required) — PM's view of their strengths and development areas
- **Feedback received** (optional) — performance reviews, peer feedback, manager input
- **Career aspirations** (optional) — long-term direction (IC track, management, founder, etc.)
- **Company ladder** (optional) — leveling framework if available

## Process
1. **Assess current level** — map PM's competencies against level expectations:
   - Product sense, strategic thinking, execution, analytical, technical, leadership, communication
2. **Define target level** — what competencies and scope define the next level
3. **Gap analysis** — where current level falls short of target, with specificity
4. **Design development plan** per gap:
   - Learning activities (reading, courses, mentorship)
   - Practice activities (stretch projects, new scope)
   - Visibility activities (presentations, cross-functional initiatives)
5. **Identify mentorship opportunities** — who can help with specific gaps
6. **Plan visibility and evidence** — how to demonstrate growth to decision-makers
7. **Set milestones and checkpoints** — quarterly assessment points

## Quality Standards
- Assessment uses specific evidence, not self-perception alone
- Gaps are prioritized by impact on reaching target level
- Development activities are actionable (not "get better at strategy")
- Visibility plan ensures growth is seen by decision-makers
- **Anti-patterns**: Generic advice applicable to any PM; development plan without practice opportunities; focusing only on weaknesses (strengths matter too); no visibility planning

## Framework References
- PM competency frameworks (Reforge, Lenny's Newsletter, company-specific ladders)
- 70-20-10 development model (70% on-the-job, 20% mentorship, 10% formal learning)
- Skill-will matrix for prioritization

## Formatting Guidelines
- Gap analysis as comparison table
- Development plan grouped by gap with learning/practice/visibility for each
- Milestones as quarterly checkpoints
- Promotion readiness as a concrete checklist

## Example
"Gap: Strategic thinking — currently operates at feature-level strategy, needs to demonstrate product-level strategy. Learning: Study Blue Ocean Strategy and Jobs-to-be-Done frameworks. Practice: Volunteer to lead next quarterly planning for your product area. Visibility: Present competitive landscape analysis to leadership team. Mentorship: Monthly coffee with [VP Product] focused on strategic framing. Milestone Q2: Have independently authored a product strategy doc reviewed by VP."
