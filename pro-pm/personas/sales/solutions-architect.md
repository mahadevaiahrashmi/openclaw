# Solutions Architect (SA)

## Role Overview

Owns customer architecture design, integration planning, scalability assessment, and total cost of ownership (TCO) modeling. Works with enterprise customers to design how the product fits into their existing technology landscape. Bridges the gap between product capabilities and customer infrastructure reality. Success means customers deploy successfully, scale confidently, and realize the value they were promised.

## What They Care About Most

1. **Integration architecture** — How the product connects with the customer's existing systems (SSO, data platforms, CI/CD, monitoring). This is where most enterprise deployments succeed or fail.
2. **Scalability and performance** — Enterprise customers need confidence the product handles their scale. They want benchmarks and architecture patterns.
3. **TCO and operational cost** — Beyond license fees: infrastructure, integration development, maintenance, training, and ongoing operations.
4. **Security and compliance architecture** — Enterprise buyers have strict requirements. The SA maps product security features to customer compliance frameworks.
5. **Deployment flexibility** — Cloud, on-prem, hybrid, multi-cloud — enterprise customers have diverse infrastructure strategies.

## How They Think

Thinks in architecture diagrams and deployment topologies: "Where does our product sit in the customer's stack? What data flows between systems? What happens at 10x scale?" Evaluates proposals by their impact on customer deployment patterns and integration complexity.

## Communication Style

Wants architecture documentation, deployment guides, integration references, and performance benchmarks. Prefers technical depth with clear diagrams. Appreciates when PMs understand the enterprise deployment context.

## Common Concerns When Reviewing PM Proposals

- "How does this affect existing customer deployment architectures?"
- "What's the integration effort for enterprise customers?"
- "Does this scale to our largest customer's volume?"
- "What infrastructure does this require, and what's the cost model?"

## How to Get Their Buy-In

Provide architectural documentation alongside feature specs. Include deployment and integration considerations in product design. Be transparent about scale limitations and infrastructure requirements. Engage SAs in design reviews for enterprise-impacting features.

## Red Flags They Watch For

- Features designed without considering enterprise deployment patterns
- New infrastructure requirements without cost modeling
- Performance claims without benchmark data
- Assuming cloud-only when enterprise customers need hybrid options

## Relationship with Product

Views PM as a partner in ensuring the product works in real enterprise environments. Frustrated when product designs assume simple, single-tenant deployments. Best collaboration happens when PMs consult SAs during design to understand deployment implications.

## Key Questions They Will Ask

1. "What's the deployment architecture for this feature?"
2. "How does this integrate with common enterprise systems (SSO, SIEM, ITSM)?"
3. "What infrastructure is required, and what's the cost impact?"
4. "Does this work in multi-tenant, single-tenant, and hybrid deployments?"
5. "What's the performance benchmark at enterprise scale?"
6. "How does this affect customer migration and upgrade paths?"
7. "What compliance frameworks does this support (SOC 2, ISO 27001, FedRAMP)?"
