# Sales Engineer (SE) / Solutions Consultant

## Role Overview

Owns technical demos, proof of concept (POC) management, technical fit assessments, and bridging the gap between customer technical requirements and product capabilities. The SE validates that the product can actually solve the customer's technical problem — not just in theory, but in practice. Success means POCs that prove value, demos that create "aha" moments, and technical evaluations that customers trust.

## What They Care About Most

1. **Technical accuracy** — They demo the product to technical buyers. Anything that doesn't work as described destroys credibility.
2. **POC success rate** — Every failed POC is a lost deal. They need reliable features, good documentation, and responsive support.
3. **Integration capabilities** — Customers live in complex environments. The SE needs to know how the product integrates.
4. **Demo environment quality** — A broken demo environment means a lost impression. Demo infrastructure is sacred.
5. **Honest capability assessment** — They need to tell customers what the product can and can't do.

## How They Think

Thinks in technical fit: "Can we actually solve this customer's problem? What gaps exist? What workarounds are available?" Evaluates features by their demonstrability — can this be shown in a 30-minute demo? Deeply pragmatic about product limitations.

## Communication Style

Wants technical documentation, API references, architecture diagrams, and honest assessments of product limitations. Prefers to be told "it doesn't do that" over "it kind of does that" followed by a POC failure.

## Common Concerns When Reviewing PM Proposals

- "Can I demo this effectively?"
- "What integrations does this support out of the box?"
- "What are the known limitations I need to communicate?"
- "Does this work in our POC/trial environment?"

## How to Get Their Buy-In

Provide honest technical documentation including limitations. Ensure demo environments reflect new features. Include SEs in beta programs. Be transparent about what's coming and what's not.

## Red Flags They Watch For

- Features that work in demo but fail at customer scale
- Marketing claims that outpace product capabilities
- Poor documentation forcing SEs to reverse-engineer features
- Demo environments that lag behind production releases

## Relationship with Product

Views PM as the source of truth about product capabilities. Frustrated when marketing promises don't match reality. Best collaboration happens when PMs involve SEs in planning and treat SE feedback from customer evaluations as high-signal input.

## Key Questions They Will Ask

1. "Can I demo this reliably in under 30 minutes?"
2. "What are the known limitations and edge cases?"
3. "What integrations are supported, and what's the implementation effort?"
4. "Is this available in our POC and trial environments?"
5. "What's the customer implementation timeline for this feature?"
6. "How does this compare technically to the competitor's offering?"
7. "What technical prerequisites does a customer need?"
