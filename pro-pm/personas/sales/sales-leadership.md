# Sales Leadership (VP Sales / CRO)

## Role Overview

Owns revenue targets, pipeline health, win rates, market segment strategy, and sales team enablement. Accountable for predictable revenue growth and building a scalable sales organization. Makes strategic decisions about where to invest sales resources and which segments to pursue. Success means consistent, predictable revenue attainment and a sales organization that can scale.

## What They Care About Most

1. **Revenue predictability** — Forecast accuracy and pipeline coverage ratios. Surprises in either direction are bad.
2. **Win rates and deal velocity** — Product-market fit shows up in win rates. Low win rates signal positioning, product, or competitive problems.
3. **Market segment strategy** — Which segments to pursue, where to invest, how to allocate resources.
4. **Competitive position** — They know exactly where they're losing deals and why. Persistent competitive gaps are existential.
5. **Team enablement and scaling** — Can new reps ramp quickly? Are tools and content supporting the sales motion?

## How They Think

Thinks in revenue units: ARR, pipeline coverage, average deal size, sales cycle length, and win rate by segment. Evaluates the roadmap as a revenue lever. Plans on annual/quarterly cycles. Makes bets on market segments and holds the team accountable.

## Communication Style

Wants executive-level summaries with revenue impact. Prefers dashboards, pipeline reports, and competitive intelligence briefings. Appreciates when PMs speak the language of revenue impact and market position.

## Common Concerns When Reviewing PM Proposals

- "How does this affect our win rate in [target segment]?"
- "What's the revenue impact?"
- "Are we building for existing customers or to win new logos?"
- "Does this close competitive gaps?"

## How to Get Their Buy-In

Frame proposals in terms of revenue impact — deals influenced, segments enabled, competitive gaps closed. Provide data from win/loss analysis. Be clear about timelines and realistic about scope.

## Red Flags They Watch For

- Product roadmap disconnected from revenue-driving priorities
- Pricing and packaging changes that disrupt active deals
- Long development cycles for features with immediate competitive urgency

## Relationship with Product

Views Product as a strategic partner in revenue growth. Frustrated when product builds for long-term vision while sales loses deals on short-term gaps. Best collaboration happens when PM and Sales leadership share a common view of target segments and competitive positioning.

## Key Questions They Will Ask

1. "What's the revenue impact of this in the next 2-4 quarters?"
2. "Which competitive gaps does this close?"
3. "How does this affect our win rate in target segments?"
4. "What's the pricing and packaging strategy?"
5. "How many active deals are blocked on this feature?"
6. "Does this open a new market segment or accelerate an existing one?"
7. "What's the sales enablement plan?"
