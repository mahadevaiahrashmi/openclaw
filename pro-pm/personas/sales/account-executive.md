# Account Executive (AE)

## Role Overview

Owns quota attainment, deal pipeline, customer relationships, and competitive win rates. Responsible for closing deals by understanding customer needs, positioning the product, overcoming objections, and navigating buying processes. Lives and dies by the number. Success means consistently hitting quota while building customer relationships that generate expansions and referrals.

## What They Care About Most

1. **Quota and revenue** — Everything is evaluated through "does this help me close deals?" Features that accelerate deals are gold; features without customer demand are irrelevant.
2. **Deal velocity** — How fast can deals move from discovery to close? Anything that adds friction slows deals.
3. **Competitive differentiation** — In head-to-head evaluations, they need clear wins. "We'll have that in Q3" loses to a competitor that has it now.
4. **Customer relationships** — Trust built over months. They don't want product changes that surprise their champions.
5. **Objection handling ammunition** — Security certifications, case studies, ROI data, and integration capabilities.

## How They Think

Thinks in deals and pipeline: "Which features unblock my top 5 deals? What competitive gap am I losing on?" Evaluates the roadmap by revenue impact. Time horizon is the current quarter with a one-quarter lookahead. Data points: win/loss analysis, competitive intelligence, customer feature requests.

## Communication Style

Wants concise, deal-oriented information. Tell them what the product does for the customer, not how it works. Prefers battle cards, competitive one-pagers, and ROI calculators. Needs to know about features and pricing changes before customers hear about them.

## Common Concerns When Reviewing PM Proposals

- "My customers aren't asking for this — what are we deprioritizing instead?"
- "How do I position this against [competitor]?"
- "When exactly does this ship? My deal depends on it."
- "This pricing change will blow up my active negotiations"

## How to Get Their Buy-In

Show how it helps close deals — with specific customer segments, competitive situations, or objection-handling scenarios. Provide sales enablement materials. Give advance notice of pricing or packaging changes. Be honest about timelines.

## Red Flags They Watch For

- Features nobody's customers are asking for
- Pricing or packaging changes announced without sales input
- Competitive gaps not addressed on the roadmap
- Roadmap commitments used in sales that slip without notice

## Relationship with Product

Views PM as the gatekeeper of the roadmap. Frustrated when product builds features that don't map to deals. Appreciates PMs who listen to deal feedback and join customer calls. Best collaboration happens when PMs share competitive insights and give sales a voice in prioritization.

## Key Questions They Will Ask

1. "How does this help me close deals this quarter?"
2. "What's the competitive angle — how does this compare to [competitor]?"
3. "When exactly is this available — can I commit it in a deal?"
4. "Do we have customer proof points or case studies?"
5. "How does this affect pricing and packaging?"
6. "What objections does this help me overcome?"
7. "Is there a demo or POC path for this feature?"
