# CMO (Chief Marketing Officer)

## Role Overview

Owns brand, market awareness, demand generation strategy, analyst relations, and market share growth. Responsible for how the market perceives the company and product — shaping the narrative, building awareness, and generating demand. Operates at the intersection of product, sales, and the market. Success means strong brand recognition, growing market share, efficient demand generation, and a narrative that positions the company as a leader.

## What They Care About Most

1. **Brand and market narrative** — The company needs a clear, compelling story. Every product decision either reinforces or dilutes the brand.
2. **Market awareness and share of voice** — Are we being talked about? Are analysts recognizing us? Are we in the consideration set?
3. **Demand generation efficiency** — Pipeline generation, cost per lead, and marketing's contribution to revenue. Marketing must prove ROI.
4. **Competitive positioning** — Where do we sit in the market landscape? How do analysts categorize us? What's our differentiation story?
5. **Analyst and press relations** — Gartner, Forrester, and key media shape enterprise buying decisions. Product direction must support analyst narratives.

## How They Think

Thinks in market narratives and category dynamics: "What category are we in? Are we defining it or reacting to it? What's the story that makes us the obvious choice?" Evaluates product decisions by their market impact — is this a narrative-worthy moment or a quiet update? Plans campaigns and analyst engagement on quarterly cycles. Constantly monitors competitive moves and market sentiment.

## Communication Style

Wants product direction communicated in market-facing language — not internal jargon or technical specs. Prefers strategic briefings on product direction that can inform analyst conversations and press narratives. Appreciates when PMs can articulate the market category implications of product decisions. Communicates through brand guidelines, campaign briefs, and market positioning frameworks.

## Common Concerns When Reviewing PM Proposals

- "Does this reinforce or dilute our brand narrative?"
- "Is this significant enough for an analyst briefing or press announcement?"
- "How does this affect our position in the analyst quadrant/wave?"
- "What's the market category implication — are we redefining how we're categorized?"
- "Does this give us something competitors can't claim?"

## How to Get Their Buy-In

Frame product direction in terms of market narrative and category positioning. Highlight competitive differentiation. Provide the story — who the hero is (customer), what the problem is, and why the company is uniquely positioned to solve it. Give enough lead time for analyst briefings and PR. Coordinate launches for maximum market impact.

## Red Flags They Watch For

- Product direction that fragments the brand narrative
- Launching important features quietly when they deserve market attention
- Missing analyst briefing windows due to unclear product timelines
- Competitive parity features being positioned as innovation
- Product decisions made without market context
- Inconsistent messaging across product, sales, and marketing

## Relationship with Product

Views Product as the engine that creates the stories marketing tells. Frustrated when product direction isn't aligned with market positioning, when launches happen without marketing coordination, or when product features don't translate into marketable differentiation. Best collaboration happens when PM and CMO co-own the product narrative and coordinate on market moments.

## Key Questions They Will Ask

1. "What's the market narrative for this — how does it advance our category story?"
2. "Is this analyst-briefing-worthy? When can we brief them?"
3. "What's the competitive differentiation angle?"
4. "How does this affect our brand positioning?"
5. "What's the launch tier — event keynote, press release, blog post, or changelog?"
6. "Do we have customer stories that bring this to life?"
7. "How does this fit into the broader marketing campaign calendar?"
