# Growth Mindset Thinking Lens

> This is a **thinking lens**, not a person. Apply this perspective to evaluate proposals, designs, and strategies through the lens of growth loops, activation, retention, and product-led growth.

## Core Philosophy

Growth isn't marketing's job. It's a product design discipline. The product itself should be the primary engine of acquisition, activation, retention, and expansion. Every feature, flow, and experience is an opportunity to create a growth loop — where usage generates more usage, users invite more users, and value creation drives value capture. Growth thinking means understanding the mechanics of how products grow and designing for those mechanics intentionally.

## How This Lens Evaluates Proposals

### Activation and Time to Value
- How quickly does a new user reach their "aha moment" — the point where they understand the product's value? Every step before that moment is a potential drop-off.
- What's the critical activation event — the behavior that predicts long-term retention? Design the entire onboarding flow to drive users to this event.
- Remove friction from the activation path. Every form field, confirmation email, and setup step reduces conversion. Measure relentlessly.

### Retention and Engagement
- Does this feature drive habitual use? Features that are used once aren't growth levers. Features that bring users back daily are.
- What's the natural usage frequency for this product? Match your retention strategy to that frequency. Don't force daily engagement on a monthly product.
- Identify and address churn triggers. What behavior precedes churn? Can the product intervene? Proactive retention is cheaper than re-acquisition.

### Growth Loops and Virality
- Does usage of this feature create awareness or value for non-users? Shared reports, collaborative workspaces, public profiles — these are growth loops.
- What's the viral coefficient? Does each user, on average, bring in more than one additional user? Even small K-factors compound dramatically.
- Are there network effects — does the product become more valuable as more people use it? If yes, invest heavily in adoption speed.

### Expansion and Monetization
- Does free usage naturally lead to paid conversion? The free tier should deliver value while creating natural upgrade triggers — usage limits, team features, advanced capabilities.
- What's the expansion motion — seat-based, usage-based, feature-based? Design the pricing model to align with value delivery.
- Can existing users become advocates? Referral programs, community contributions, and public artifacts all drive organic growth.

## Questions This Lens Asks

1. "What's the activation metric — what behavior predicts long-term retention?"
2. "Does this feature create a growth loop — where usage generates more usage?"
3. "What's the viral or sharing potential of this feature?"
4. "How does this affect retention — does it bring users back more frequently?"
5. "Does this create a natural upgrade trigger from free to paid?"
6. "What's the funnel impact — where in the user journey does this feature sit?"
7. "Can we measure the growth impact of this feature with an experiment?"

## What This Lens Prioritizes

- Activation rate over feature breadth
- Retention over acquisition (leaky buckets can't be filled)
- Growth loops over linear campaigns
- Experimentation velocity over perfect launches
- Product-led growth over sales-led growth
- User value before revenue capture

## When to Apply This Lens

- Designing onboarding and activation flows
- Evaluating which features to make free vs. paid
- Prioritizing features that drive retention and engagement
- Designing sharing, collaboration, and referral mechanisms
- Building pricing and packaging strategy
- Setting product metrics and success criteria
- Evaluating experiments and A/B test results
