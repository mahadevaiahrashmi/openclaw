# Legal / Compliance

## Role Overview

Owns regulatory compliance, contracts, terms of service, intellectual property, data handling agreements, and liability management. Responsible for ensuring the company operates within legal boundaries and manages legal risk appropriately. Acts as a guardrail — not a blocker — ensuring product decisions don't create legal exposure. Success means the company avoids regulatory penalties, manages contractual obligations, and makes informed decisions about legal risk.

## What They Care About Most

1. **Regulatory compliance** — GDPR, CCPA, HIPAA, SOX, accessibility laws, and industry-specific regulations. Non-compliance means fines, lawsuits, and reputational damage.
2. **Data handling and privacy** — How user data is collected, stored, processed, and shared. Data handling mistakes have regulatory and reputational consequences.
3. **Contractual obligations** — Customer agreements, vendor contracts, and partner arrangements. Product changes that violate contractual terms create liability.
4. **Terms of service and user agreements** — Product changes may require ToS updates. Users need to consent to material changes.
5. **Intellectual property** — Ensuring the company owns what it builds and doesn't infringe on others' IP. Open source licensing compliance.

## How They Think

Thinks in risk assessment: "What's the legal exposure? What's the probability of enforcement? What's the potential penalty? Can we mitigate the risk through design, contracts, or disclosures?" Evaluates proposals by their legal surface area — does this touch regulated data, change contractual commitments, introduce IP risk, or require user consent updates? Conservative by training but pragmatic — they'd rather shape the approach to be compliant than block it entirely.

## Communication Style

Wants clear descriptions of what the product does, what data it handles, and who it affects. Prefers early consultation rather than last-minute review. Appreciates when PMs identify legal and compliance risks proactively. Communicates through legal opinions, risk assessments, and compliance checklists.

## Common Concerns When Reviewing PM Proposals

- "Does this involve personal data, and how is it handled under our privacy obligations?"
- "Do our current terms of service cover this, or do we need an update?"
- "Does this affect our compliance posture (SOC 2, GDPR, HIPAA, etc.)?"
- "Are there intellectual property considerations — open source licenses, third-party IP?"
- "Does this change what we've contractually committed to existing customers?"

## How to Get Their Buy-In

Involve legal early — before the design is finalized. Identify data handling and regulatory implications in your spec. Be willing to adjust product design to meet compliance requirements. Provide clear descriptions of data flows and user impact. Treat legal review time as part of the timeline, not as an obstacle.

## Red Flags They Watch For

- Products handling personal data without privacy impact assessment
- Features launched without legal review that touch regulated areas
- Open source components used without license compliance review
- Product changes that conflict with existing customer contracts
- "We'll update the terms later" — terms must be updated before the change ships
- AI/ML features without consideration for emerging AI regulations

## Relationship with Product

Views PM as a partner in building products that are both innovative and legally sound. Frustrated when legal is brought in at the last minute or when compliance is treated as a checkbox. Best collaboration happens when PMs understand the regulatory landscape, consult legal early, and design products that are compliant by default.

## Key Questions They Will Ask

1. "What personal data does this feature collect, process, or share?"
2. "Does this trigger any regulatory requirements (GDPR, CCPA, HIPAA)?"
3. "Do our terms of service and privacy policy need updating?"
4. "Are there third-party components with licensing implications?"
5. "Does this affect existing customer contractual commitments?"
6. "What's the data retention and deletion policy for this feature?"
7. "Has a legal or privacy impact assessment been conducted?"
