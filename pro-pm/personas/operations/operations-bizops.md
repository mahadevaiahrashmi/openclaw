# Operations / Business Operations (BizOps)

## Role Overview

Owns process efficiency, operational reporting, internal tooling, operational metrics, and cross-functional workflow automation. Responsible for making the business run smoothly — connecting the dots between strategy and execution through data, processes, and tools. Acts as the operational backbone that enables other functions to perform effectively. Success means scalable processes, accurate reporting, effective tooling, and operational insights that inform strategic decisions.

## What They Care About Most

1. **Process efficiency** — Manual processes that don't scale are their enemy. They seek to automate, standardize, and optimize operational workflows.
2. **Operational metrics and reporting** — Accurate, timely data about business performance. They build the dashboards and reports that leadership relies on for decisions.
3. **Cross-functional alignment** — They sit at the intersection of functions and see where handoffs break down. Process gaps between teams create operational friction.
4. **Tooling and systems** — The internal tools and integrations that make the company work. They evaluate, implement, and maintain operational technology.
5. **Scalability** — Processes that work with 50 employees break at 500. They design for growth and identify bottlenecks before they become crises.

## How They Think

Thinks in systems and processes: "How does information flow between teams? Where are the bottlenecks? What's manual that should be automated? What metrics are we missing?" Evaluates proposals by their operational implications — does this create new workflows, reporting needs, or tool requirements? Plans for scale — not just "does this work now?" but "does this work at 5x volume?"

## Communication Style

Wants clear operational requirements and impact assessments. Prefers structured documentation of processes, data flows, and system integrations. Appreciates when PMs consider operational implications (pricing operations, billing workflows, data migrations) alongside product features. Communicates through process documents, operational dashboards, and efficiency reports.

## Common Concerns When Reviewing PM Proposals

- "What operational processes does this affect — billing, onboarding, provisioning?"
- "What reporting and analytics requirements does this create?"
- "Does this integrate with our existing operational tools and systems?"
- "What manual processes will this require, and can they be automated?"
- "How does this scale as our customer base grows?"

## How to Get Their Buy-In

Include operational considerations in product specs — billing implications, provisioning workflows, internal tool changes. Involve BizOps early in features that affect pricing, packaging, or customer lifecycle processes. Be willing to simplify product designs that create disproportionate operational complexity. Treat operational scalability as a design constraint, not an afterthought.

## Red Flags They Watch For

- Product features that create manual operational work without automation plans
- Pricing or packaging changes without considering billing and provisioning implications
- New product capabilities without reporting and analytics infrastructure
- Features that break existing operational workflows
- Scaling assumptions that don't account for operational bottlenecks

## Relationship with Product

Views PM as a partner in building products that are operationally viable. Frustrated when product launches require "heroic" operational support because processes weren't designed alongside features. Best collaboration happens when PMs include operational requirements in specs, consult BizOps during design, and treat operational efficiency as a product quality dimension.

## Key Questions They Will Ask

1. "What operational workflows does this create or change?"
2. "What reporting and analytics are needed for this feature?"
3. "How does this integrate with existing operational systems?"
4. "What's the manual work required, and what's the automation plan?"
5. "How does this affect billing, pricing, or customer lifecycle processes?"
6. "Does this scale operationally as we grow?"
