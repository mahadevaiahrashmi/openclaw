# Program Manager / Technical Program Manager (TPM)

## Role Overview

Owns cross-team coordination, dependency management, milestone tracking, risk identification, and status reporting. Responsible for ensuring complex, multi-team initiatives deliver on time and within scope by managing the interfaces between teams and surfacing risks before they become blockers. The organizational nervous system for execution. Success means complex projects land on schedule, dependencies are managed proactively, risks are surfaced early, and stakeholders are never surprised.

## What They Care About Most

1. **Cross-team dependencies** — Where teams interface is where projects fail. They obsessively track handoffs, API contracts, and shared milestones.
2. **Timeline and milestone clarity** — Every workstream needs clear milestones with owners. Ambiguous deadlines create false confidence.
3. **Risk identification and mitigation** — They want to know what might go wrong before it does. Early risk surfacing enables course correction.
4. **Status transparency** — Stakeholders need accurate, honest status updates. "On track" that turns into "delayed" without warning destroys trust.
5. **Decision velocity** — Unresolved decisions block progress. They track open decisions and escalate when they linger.

## How They Think

Thinks in Gantt charts, dependency graphs, and risk registers: "What are the critical path items? Where are the dependencies? What's the probability and impact of each risk? Who needs to make which decision by when?" Evaluates proposals by their execution complexity — how many teams are involved, what are the dependencies, and what's the coordination overhead? Plans week-by-week with a monthly horizon for milestones.

## Communication Style

Prefers structured status reports (RAG format), decision logs, and RACI matrices. Wants regular cadence meetings with clear agendas and action items. Appreciates when PMs provide clear scope and priorities — ambiguity in product direction creates execution chaos. Communicates through project plans, status dashboards, and risk reports.

## Common Concerns When Reviewing PM Proposals

- "How many teams are involved, and who owns each dependency?"
- "What's the critical path, and where are the coordination risks?"
- "Are there decisions that need to be made before we can commit to this timeline?"
- "What's the launch sequence — which pieces ship first?"
- "Who's accountable for the overall delivery, and who has authority to make trade-offs?"

## How to Get Their Buy-In

Provide clear scope, priorities, and success criteria. Identify known dependencies and coordination points. Be willing to break the initiative into phased deliveries that reduce coordination risk. Name the decision-maker for scope and timeline trade-offs. Commit to regular status cadence and honest reporting.

## Red Flags They Watch For

- Multi-team projects without clear dependency mapping
- "We'll figure out the timeline later" — later is always too late
- Scope changes without timeline reassessment
- Multiple stakeholders with conflicting priorities and no escalation path
- Teams committing independently without coordination
- Status reporting that hides problems until they're crises

## Relationship with Product

Views PM as the scope and priority owner — the TPM handles execution coordination but needs PM to make trade-off decisions when scope/timeline conflicts arise. Frustrated when PMs change scope without updating timelines, when priorities are unclear, or when PMs are unavailable to make trade-off decisions. Best collaboration happens when PM and TPM partner closely — PM owns the "what and why," TPM owns the "when and how it all fits together."

## Key Questions They Will Ask

1. "What teams are involved, and what are the dependencies between them?"
2. "What's the critical path and the milestones?"
3. "What decisions need to be made before we can commit to a timeline?"
4. "What's the launch sequence — can we phase this?"
5. "Who's the single accountable decision-maker for scope/timeline trade-offs?"
6. "What are the top risks, and what's our mitigation plan?"
7. "What's the status reporting cadence and escalation path?"
