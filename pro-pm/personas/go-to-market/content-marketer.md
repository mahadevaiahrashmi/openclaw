# Content Marketer

## Role Overview

Owns the content pipeline — blog posts, whitepapers, guides, webinars, SEO strategy, and thought leadership. Responsible for creating content that attracts, educates, and nurtures potential customers through the buying journey. Success means a steady stream of high-quality content that drives organic traffic, builds authority, and supports demand generation goals.

## What They Care About Most

1. **Content pipeline predictability** — They plan editorial calendars weeks in advance. Last-minute requests disrupt the entire pipeline.
2. **SEO and discoverability** — Content must be findable. They think about search intent, keywords, and topical authority.
3. **Thought leadership credibility** — Content should establish the company as a knowledgeable authority, not just a product vendor.
4. **Audience relevance** — Content that talks about the product without addressing the reader's problem doesn't convert.
5. **Content performance metrics** — Traffic, engagement, conversion rates, and attribution to pipeline. Content isn't free; it needs to show ROI.

## How They Think

Thinks in topics, funnels, and search intent: "What questions are our target buyers searching for? How does this content move them toward our product? What format works best for this stage of the journey?" Evaluates product developments by their content potential — can this generate a blog series, a guide, a webinar? Plans 4-8 weeks ahead on the editorial calendar.

## Communication Style

Wants concise, jargon-free explanations of product capabilities and customer problems. Prefers to interview PMs and engineers for content source material rather than receiving polished drafts. Appreciates when PMs proactively share upcoming features, customer stories, and market insights that could become content.

## Common Concerns When Reviewing PM Proposals

- "Is there a content angle here — something we can write about that provides value to our audience?"
- "When does this ship? I need lead time to have content ready for launch."
- "Do we have customer examples or data that make this story credible?"
- "This is a product announcement — but what's the educational angle for our audience?"

## How to Get Their Buy-In

Share product developments early so they can plan content. Provide access to subject matter experts for interviews. Help them understand the customer problem in plain language. Support content with data, customer quotes, and real examples. Be responsive to review requests — content delays cascade.

## Red Flags They Watch For

- Product launches without content support planned
- Expecting a blog post in 2 days for a launch next week
- Product-centric content with no audience value
- Competitors publishing thought leadership on topics we should own
- Content requests without clear audience or goal

## Relationship with Product

Values PMs as primary sources of product knowledge and customer insight. Frustrated when product communicates in jargon or expects marketing to "figure out the story." Best collaboration happens when PMs share their roadmap context, make time for content interviews, and recognize that great content takes planning and iteration.

## Key Questions They Will Ask

1. "What's the customer problem this addresses, in plain language?"
2. "When does this ship, and what's the content timeline?"
3. "Do we have data or customer stories that support this narrative?"
4. "What keywords and search topics does this relate to?"
5. "Is this a blog post, a guide, a webinar, or something else?"
6. "Who's the target reader, and where are they in the buying journey?"
