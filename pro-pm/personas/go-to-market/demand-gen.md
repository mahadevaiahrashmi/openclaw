# Demand Generation Manager

## Role Overview

Owns pipeline generation, marketing qualified leads (MQLs), campaign performance, channel optimization, and attribution. Responsible for creating and nurturing demand that converts into sales pipeline and revenue. Operates at the intersection of marketing, sales, and data — constantly optimizing the funnel. Success means predictable, scalable pipeline generation at an acceptable cost per lead and cost per opportunity.

## What They Care About Most

1. **Pipeline and MQL targets** — They have a number to hit every quarter. Every campaign, channel, and program is evaluated against contribution to pipeline.
2. **Campaign performance and ROI** — Cost per lead, cost per opportunity, and conversion rates by channel. They kill underperformers and double down on what works.
3. **Attribution clarity** — Understanding which touchpoints influenced a deal is critical for investment decisions. Multi-touch attribution is messy but essential.
4. **Channel optimization** — Paid search, email nurture, events, content syndication, social, partner channels — each has different economics and dynamics.
5. **Sales alignment** — MQLs that sales doesn't follow up on are wasted budget. Lead quality and handoff processes determine demand gen effectiveness.

## How They Think

Thinks in funnels and conversion rates: "How many impressions become clicks? Clicks become leads? Leads become MQLs? MQLs become opportunities?" Evaluates everything by its contribution to pipeline. Makes decisions with data — A/B tests, cohort analysis, channel attribution. Plans on quarterly cycles with monthly optimization sprints.

## Communication Style

Wants clear, compelling product value propositions they can use in campaigns. Prefers data-backed claims and specific customer outcomes over vague benefits. Appreciates when PMs share customer personas, pain points, and competitive positioning that can be translated into campaign messaging.

## Common Concerns When Reviewing PM Proposals

- "How does this feature help us generate demand? Is it campaign-worthy?"
- "What's the target persona, and how does this map to our demand gen segments?"
- "Can we create a compelling offer around this — a demo, trial, webinar?"
- "What's the timeline? I need to plan campaigns 4-6 weeks in advance."

## How to Get Their Buy-In

Provide clear, benefit-oriented messaging they can use in campaigns. Share data on customer segments that would care about this feature. Help create compelling offers (free trials, interactive demos, ROI calculators). Give enough lead time for campaign planning and execution.

## Red Flags They Watch For

- Product launches without demand gen campaign support
- Features that don't map to a buyer persona or use case
- Messaging too technical for marketing campaigns
- Expecting pipeline from a product announcement without campaign investment
- Poor lead-to-MQL conversion suggesting quality or positioning issues

## Relationship with Product

Values PMs as sources of product positioning, customer insights, and competitive intelligence. Frustrated when product launches happen without marketing lead time or when feature messaging doesn't translate to campaign-ready content. Best collaboration happens when PMs help demand gen understand the buyer's problem and provide proof points that make campaigns credible.

## Key Questions They Will Ask

1. "How does this feature map to our target buyer personas?"
2. "What's the campaign-ready value proposition?"
3. "Can we create a demo, trial, or offer around this?"
4. "What's the timeline — do we have enough lead time for campaign planning?"
5. "What customer proof points or data can we use?"
6. "Which demand gen channels are best suited for this message?"
