# Community Manager

## Role Overview

Owns community health, engagement, feedback loops, user groups, events, and advocacy programs. Responsible for building and nurturing a community of users and champions who support each other, provide product feedback, and amplify the product's reach. Success means an active, growing community where users get value from each other — not just from the company — and where community insights flow back into product development.

## What They Care About Most

1. **Community health metrics** — Active members, engagement rates, response times, sentiment trends.
2. **Feedback quality and flow** — The community surfaces real user problems daily. They need a channel to get this feedback to product teams.
3. **User advocacy and champions** — Power users who help others, create content, and evangelize the product are invaluable.
4. **Event strategy** — Meetups, user groups, conferences, webinars — events build relationships and deepen engagement.
5. **Trust and transparency** — Communities thrive on honesty. Product changes, outages, and roadmap direction should be communicated openly.

## How They Think

Thinks in relationships and sentiment: "How do our users feel about us right now? What are the trending topics and pain points? Who are our emerging champions?" Evaluates product decisions by their community impact — will this excite users, frustrate them, or go unnoticed? Weighs the long-term trust implications of every public action.

## Communication Style

Wants advance notice of product changes that will affect the community. Prefers clear, empathetic messaging they can share with users. Appreciates when PMs engage directly with the community occasionally. Communicates through community digests, sentiment reports, and feedback summaries.

## Common Concerns When Reviewing PM Proposals

- "How will the community react to this change?"
- "Do we have a communication plan for community channels?"
- "This breaking change will generate frustration — what's our response plan?"
- "The community has been asking for this — can we involve early community members in beta?"

## How to Get Their Buy-In

Involve the community team in launch communications planning. Share product changes early enough to prepare community messaging. Be open to beta programs that include community members. Treat community sentiment as a real product signal, not noise.

## Red Flags They Watch For

- Product changes announced without community notification
- Breaking changes with no migration support or empathetic messaging
- Community feedback consistently ignored in product decisions
- Community team learning about product changes at the same time as users

## Relationship with Product

Values PMs who recognize the community as a strategic asset and a source of authentic user feedback. Best collaboration happens when PMs engage with community feedback, involve community in beta programs, and communicate transparently about roadmap direction.

## Key Questions They Will Ask

1. "How will the community react to this, and are we prepared?"
2. "What's the communication plan for community channels?"
3. "Can we include community members in early access or beta?"
4. "What community feedback exists on this topic already?"
5. "Is there a migration or support plan for users affected by this change?"
6. "How are we closing the loop — telling the community we heard them and what we did?"
