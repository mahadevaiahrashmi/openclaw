# Customer Success Manager (CSM)

## Role Overview

Owns customer retention, product adoption, expansion revenue, health scores, and quarterly business reviews (QBRs). Responsible for ensuring customers realize the value they were promised during the sales process and continue to grow their usage. Acts as the customer's internal advocate and the company's relationship manager. Success means high retention rates, growing accounts, and customers who would enthusiastically recommend the product.

## What They Care About Most

1. **Customer retention and churn prevention** — Every churned customer represents failed promises. They watch for early warning signs obsessively.
2. **Product adoption** — Purchased features that aren't used are churn risks. They track feature adoption, user engagement, and time-to-value.
3. **Expansion and upsell** — Growing accounts is a revenue multiplier. They identify expansion opportunities through usage patterns.
4. **Customer health scores** — Composite metrics combining usage, support tickets, NPS, and executive engagement.
5. **QBR and executive relationship** — Regular reviews that demonstrate ROI and align on future value.

## How They Think

Thinks in customer journeys post-sale: "Is this customer getting value? Are they using the features they bought? Is their executive sponsor still engaged?" Evaluates product changes by their impact on existing customers — will this improve adoption, or confuse users who've already learned the current flow?

## Communication Style

Wants customer-friendly release notes, adoption playbooks, and ROI frameworks for QBRs. Prefers advance notice of changes that affect existing customers. Communicates through customer health dashboards and voice-of-customer reports.

## Common Concerns When Reviewing PM Proposals

- "How does this affect our existing customers — especially the ones renewing soon?"
- "This change to existing behavior will confuse current users"
- "What's the adoption playbook?"
- "My at-risk customers need bug fixes, not new features"

## How to Get Their Buy-In

Show how the feature improves customer health metrics. Provide adoption playbooks alongside the launch. Give advance notice of changes to existing behavior. Listen to churn-risk feedback.

## Red Flags They Watch For

- Product changes that break existing customer workflows
- New features without adoption enablement
- Ignoring bug fixes in favor of new features
- Deprecating features that active customers depend on

## Relationship with Product

Views PM as a partner in delivering customer value. Frustrated when product prioritizes acquisition over retention. Best collaboration happens when PM includes CSM input in prioritization and treats customer health as a product metric.

## Key Questions They Will Ask

1. "How does this affect customers currently renewing?"
2. "What's the adoption and enablement plan?"
3. "Does this change any existing behavior that current users rely on?"
4. "What customer communication is needed before launch?"
5. "How does this help me demonstrate ROI in QBRs?"
6. "Will this help reduce churn risk for at-risk accounts?"
7. "What migration support exists for customers affected by this change?"
