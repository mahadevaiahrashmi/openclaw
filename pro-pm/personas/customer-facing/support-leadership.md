# Support Leadership (Director/VP of Support)

## Role Overview

Owns customer satisfaction (CSAT), SLA compliance, support cost per ticket, deflection rate, and team readiness for product changes. Responsible for building a scalable support operation that maintains quality while managing cost. Balances customer experience with operational efficiency. Success means high CSAT, met SLAs, predictable costs, and a team that's never surprised by product changes.

## What They Care About Most

1. **CSAT and customer experience** — Support interactions shape customer perception. Every interaction is a moment of truth.
2. **SLA compliance** — Contractual commitments to response and resolution times. SLA misses create escalations and churn.
3. **Support cost and efficiency** — Cost per ticket, deflection rate, self-service resolution rate. Linear scaling is unsustainable.
4. **Team readiness** — Product changes without support training create spikes in ticket volume and resolution time.
5. **Deflection and self-service** — Knowledge base, in-app guidance, and forums that help users solve problems without contacting support.

## How They Think

Thinks in operational metrics: "What's our ticket volume trend? Where are the spikes? What's our cost per resolution?" Evaluates product changes by their support impact — new features mean new ticket categories, training needs, and documentation updates. Plans staffing around product release schedules.

## Communication Style

Wants advance notice of product changes with support impact assessments. Prefers structured release readiness checklists. Appreciates when PMs quantify expected support impact. Communicates through operational dashboards and staffing models.

## Common Concerns When Reviewing PM Proposals

- "What's the support impact — expected ticket volume and complexity?"
- "When does this launch? I need 2-3 weeks for team training."
- "Is the documentation ready?"
- "What's the self-service path? Can we deflect these tickets?"

## How to Get Their Buy-In

Provide support impact assessments with product proposals. Include documentation and training in the launch timeline. Design self-service paths. Give support leadership a seat in launch readiness reviews. Treat support cost as a product design consideration.

## Red Flags They Watch For

- Product launches without support readiness
- Features designed without considering the support experience
- Increasing ticket volume without investment in self-service
- Breaking changes that spike escalations without warning

## Relationship with Product

Views PM as a partner in designing supportable products. Best collaboration happens when PMs include support readiness in launch criteria and treat ticket data as a product quality signal.

## Key Questions They Will Ask

1. "What's the expected support impact — ticket volume and complexity?"
2. "Is documentation and training material ready?"
3. "What's the self-service deflection strategy?"
4. "When does this launch — do we have enough lead time?"
5. "How does this affect our SLA compliance?"
6. "What's the escalation path for issues with this feature?"
7. "Are error messages and in-app guidance designed to reduce support contacts?"
