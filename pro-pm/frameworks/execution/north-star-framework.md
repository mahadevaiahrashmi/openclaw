# North Star Framework

## What It Is
The North Star Framework, developed by Amplitude, centers a product organization around a single metric (the North Star Metric) that captures the core value delivered to customers. This metric is supported by 3–5 Input Metrics that the team can directly influence. Together, they form a "constellation" that guides product decisions and measures progress.

## When to Use
- Aligning the entire product team around a common measure of success
- When teams are optimizing for different metrics that conflict with each other
- As a foundation for OKRs — the North Star informs what objectives matter
- When leadership asks "are we winning?" and there's no clear answer

## When NOT to Use
- Very early-stage products without enough data to identify the right metric
- Multi-product companies where a single metric can't capture all products' value
- As a replacement for financial metrics — the North Star complements revenue goals

## How to Apply
1. **Identify your North Star Metric**: Must satisfy three criteria:
   - Reflects value delivered to customers (not just business value)
   - Leads to revenue if it grows (but isn't revenue itself)
   - Measures progress — if this metric goes up, your product is winning
2. **Define Input Metrics** (3–5): Actionable metrics that teams can directly influence and that drive the North Star
3. **Map the constellation**: North Star at center, input metrics around it, with causal relationships
4. **Align teams**: Each team owns 1–2 input metrics. All input metrics ladder to the North Star
5. **Review weekly**: Track inputs and North Star. Investigate when inputs move but North Star doesn't (or vice versa)

## Example
**Product**: Collaboration tool for remote teams
- **North Star**: Weekly Active Collaborators (users who participate in at least one shared workspace per week)
- **Input Metrics**:
  1. New user activation rate (% completing first collaboration within 48 hours)
  2. Workspace creation rate (new workspaces per week)
  3. Cross-team workspace membership (users in 2+ workspaces)
  4. 7-day retention (% of users returning within 7 days)
- **Alignment**: Growth team → activation rate. Core product → workspace creation. Platform → cross-team membership. All ladder to Weekly Active Collaborators

## Key Pitfalls
- Choosing revenue as the North Star — it should reflect customer value, which *leads* to revenue
- Picking a vanity metric (total signups, page views) that doesn't reflect actual value delivery
- Too many input metrics — keep it to 3–5 or teams lose focus
- Not validating the causal chain — do inputs actually drive the North Star? Test the assumption
- Keeping the same North Star forever — it should evolve as your product and market mature

## Related Frameworks
- **OKRs** — North Star informs the "O"; input metrics become Key Results
- **Pirate Metrics (AARRR)** — input metrics often map to AARRR stages
- **Product-Led Growth** — North Star for PLG companies typically reflects product engagement
- **Growth Loops** — the mechanics that drive input metrics
