# Spotify Model

## What It Is
The Spotify Model is an organizational framework describing how Spotify structured its engineering teams (circa 2012–2014). It organizes people into Squads (small cross-functional teams), Tribes (collections of related squads), Chapters (functional discipline groups across squads), and Guilds (informal interest-based communities). It emphasizes autonomy with alignment.

## When to Use
- Scaling a product organization while maintaining team autonomy
- When you need both cross-functional product teams and functional skill development
- As inspiration for organizational design (but adapted to your context)
- Companies with 50–500 engineers seeking a balance of independence and coordination

## When NOT to Use
- Small teams (<20 people) — the structure overhead isn't worth it
- As a copy-paste template — even Spotify evolved beyond this model
- When the organization lacks the trust culture that makes autonomy work
- Regulated environments requiring more formal governance structures

## How to Apply
1. **Squads** (6–12 people): Autonomous, cross-functional teams with a specific mission. Each has a Product Owner and works like a mini-startup. Squads choose their own agile methodology
2. **Tribes** (multiple squads, <100 people): Collection of squads working in a related area. Led by a Tribe Lead. Squads within a tribe share a general business domain
3. **Chapters**: Functional groups (e.g., all backend engineers, all designers) across squads within a tribe. Led by a Chapter Lead (also a people manager). Provides career development and skill growth
4. **Guilds**: Informal communities of interest across the entire organization (e.g., "Web Performance Guild"). Open membership. Knowledge sharing and best practices
5. **Key principle**: Squads are aligned on strategy but autonomous on execution. "Be autonomous, but don't suboptimize"

## Example
**Tribe**: Growth
- **Squad: Activation** — Mission: Get new users to their first "aha moment" within 24 hours
- **Squad: Retention** — Mission: Reduce 30-day churn by improving engagement loops
- **Squad: Monetization** — Mission: Optimize trial-to-paid conversion
- **Chapter: Frontend** — All frontend engineers across Growth squads meet biweekly for code reviews and architecture discussions
- **Guild: Experimentation** — Cross-org community sharing A/B testing best practices, tools, and learnings

## Key Pitfalls
- Copying the model literally — Spotify itself said "this is a snapshot, not a prescription"
- Chapters without strong leads — skill development suffers; people feel disconnected from their discipline
- Squads that are "autonomous" but have no strategic alignment — autonomy without direction is chaos
- Ignoring the cultural prerequisites — the model assumes high trust, psychological safety, and engineering maturity
- Treating guilds as mandatory — they should be organic; forced participation kills engagement

## Related Frameworks
- **Team Topologies** — a more principled approach to team structure with clearer interaction modes
- **SAFe** — more formal, ceremony-heavy scaling framework
- **OKRs** — mechanism for alignment that enables squad autonomy
- **McKinsey 7-S** — broader organizational alignment beyond team structure
