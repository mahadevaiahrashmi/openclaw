# Working Backwards

## What It Is
Working Backwards is Amazon's product development methodology where teams start by writing a Press Release and FAQ (PR/FAQ) describing the finished product *before* building anything. The press release forces clarity on who the customer is, what the problem is, and why the solution matters. If you can't write a compelling press release, the idea isn't ready.

## When to Use
- Defining new products, features, or major initiatives
- When the team is excited about a solution but hasn't articulated the customer value clearly
- Aligning stakeholders on vision before investing in development
- As an alternative to PRDs for defining *what* and *why* before *how*

## When NOT to Use
- Small features or iterations that don't need this level of strategic framing
- When you need detailed technical specifications — Working Backwards defines the vision, not the implementation
- Rapid experimentation where speed matters more than upfront definition

## How to Apply
1. **Write the Press Release** (1 page):
   - Headline: One sentence naming the product and customer benefit
   - Subheading: Who is it for and what's the value?
   - Problem paragraph: Describe the current pain/frustration
   - Solution paragraph: Describe how the product solves it
   - Quote from a company leader: Why this matters
   - Customer quote: How it changed their experience
   - Call to action: How to get started
2. **Write the FAQ** (2–5 pages):
   - External FAQ: Questions customers would ask
   - Internal FAQ: Questions about feasibility, cost, timeline, risks
3. **Review and iterate**: Share the PR/FAQ with leadership. Iterate until the press release is genuinely exciting
4. **Only then**: Start building

## Template
**PRESS RELEASE**

**Headline**: [Product Name] Helps [Customer] [Achieve Benefit]

**Subheading**: [One sentence expanding on the benefit]

**Problem**: [2–3 sentences describing the current pain]

**Solution**: [2–3 sentences describing what the product does]

**Leader Quote**: "[Why this matters to us]"

**How It Works**: [2–3 sentences on the key experience]

**Customer Quote**: "[How this changed my experience]"

**Get Started**: [Call to action]

**FAQ**: [5–10 questions covering customer and internal concerns]

## Example
**Headline**: "Pulse Helps Engineering Managers Prevent Burnout Before It Happens"
**Problem**: Engineering managers learn about team burnout too late — usually when someone hands in their resignation. Current tools measure code output, not team health.
**Solution**: Pulse uses lightweight weekly check-ins and pattern analysis to surface early warning signs of burnout, giving managers actionable insights to intervene while it still matters.
**Customer Quote**: "Pulse flagged that two of my senior engineers were at risk three weeks before I would have noticed. That early warning saved my team." — VP Eng, Series B startup

## Key Pitfalls
- Writing a vague press release — if it could describe any product, it's not specific enough
- Using jargon or internal language — the press release should be understandable by a customer
- Falling in love with the press release and skipping validation — it's a hypothesis, not proof
- Writing the FAQ as an afterthought — the FAQ forces you to address hard questions honestly

## Related Frameworks
- **Narrative Memo** — Amazon's internal communication format that extends Working Backwards
- **Lean Canvas** — captures similar elements in a more structured format
- **Design Thinking** — Working Backwards emphasizes the Define phase
- **PRD** — traditional alternative; Working Backwards is more customer-centric
