# Land and Expand

## What It Is
Land and Expand is a go-to-market strategy where you "land" with a small initial deal (one team, one use case, or one product) and then "expand" within the account over time (more users, more products, more departments). It reduces initial sales friction, proves value before asking for larger commitments, and drives net revenue retention above 100%.

## When to Use
- Enterprise sales where large upfront deals face long procurement cycles
- Products with natural expansion mechanics (more users = more value)
- When your product benefits from organic internal adoption
- Building a PLG or product-led sales motion

## When NOT to Use
- Products that don't have natural expansion paths (single-user tools)
- Markets where the full solution must be deployed at once (security, compliance)
- When small deals are unprofitable and expansion isn't reliable

## How to Apply
1. **Define the wedge**: What's the smallest valuable entry point? One team, one use case, one department
2. **Make landing easy**: Low price, self-serve or low-touch sales, fast time-to-value
3. **Deliver undeniable value**: The land deal must succeed — no expansion without satisfaction
4. **Identify expansion triggers**:
   - Usage growth (hitting limits)
   - Adjacent teams discovering the product
   - New use cases emerging
   - Executive visibility creating top-down mandates
5. **Build expansion into the product**: In-app sharing, cross-team features, admin dashboards
6. **Measure**: Land deal size, time to first expansion, expansion rate, net revenue retention

## Example
**Product**: Cloud cost management
- **Land**: DevOps team, $1K/month, monitors AWS costs for one account
- **Expand path**: Add more cloud accounts → add Azure and GCP → finance team wants cost allocation → VP Eng wants org-wide visibility → enterprise contract at $15K/month
- **Expansion triggers**: Adding second cloud provider, finance requesting reports, exceeding usage tiers
- **Land-to-expand ratio**: Average 8x over 18 months
- **NRR**: 145%

## Key Pitfalls
- Landing too small — if the initial value is trivial, expansion never starts
- No systematic expansion playbook — leaving expansion to chance instead of designing for it
- Not tracking expansion metrics — you can't improve what you don't measure
- Over-investing in land at the expense of expand — the real revenue is in expansion

## Related Frameworks
- **Sales-Led Growth** — land and expand is the core SLG mechanic
- **Product-Led Growth** — PLG companies land through self-serve and expand through product adoption
- **Expansion Revenue** — the metrics framework for measuring expand success
- **Tiered Packaging** — tiers create the stepping stones for expansion
