# Freemium Model

## What It Is
Freemium offers a permanently free product tier alongside paid tiers. The free tier serves as a distribution mechanism and top-of-funnel acquisition tool. The key challenge: the free tier must deliver enough value to attract and retain users, but not so much that there's no reason to upgrade. Conversion rates typically range from 2–5% for consumer and 5–15% for B2B SaaS.

## When to Use
- Products with near-zero marginal cost per user (software, digital products)
- Products where usage creates data or network effects that increase value
- When your target users have low willingness to pay initially but high potential lifetime value
- Product-led growth strategies where product experience drives conversion

## When NOT to Use
- High marginal cost per user (custom onboarding, infrastructure-heavy products)
- Products where the free tier can't deliver meaningful value without the paid features
- Markets where "free" signals low quality or lack of seriousness
- When you can't afford to support a large non-paying user base

## How to Apply
1. **Define the free tier boundary**: Features available for free should solve a real problem but create natural upgrade pressure
2. **Choose a gating mechanism**:
   - **Feature-gated**: Core features free, advanced features paid (Slack, Notion)
   - **Usage-gated**: Limited volume/quantity free (Mailchimp, Zapier)
   - **Capacity-gated**: Limited users/projects free (Figma, Trello)
   - **Time-gated (reverse trial)**: Full features free temporarily, then downgrade (Ahrefs)
3. **Set conversion targets**: B2B SaaS: 5–15%, Consumer: 2–5%
4. **Design upgrade triggers**: In-app prompts when users hit limits, feature discovery moments, team growth
5. **Monitor**: Free-to-paid conversion rate, time to convert, cost to serve free users, viral coefficient from free users

## Example
**Product**: Design collaboration tool
- **Free**: 3 projects, unlimited collaborators, basic exports
- **Paid ($12/user/month)**: Unlimited projects, version history, design systems, advanced exports, admin controls
- **Gating**: Capacity (3 projects) + Feature (version history, design systems)
- **Upgrade trigger**: When designer creates 4th project → upgrade prompt. When team wants shared components → design systems is paid
- **Conversion**: 8% free-to-paid, average 45 days to convert
- **Viral**: Free collaborators invite 2.3 new users on average

## Key Pitfalls
- Free tier too generous — no upgrade pressure; conversion suffers
- Free tier too restrictive — users can't experience enough value; churn from free before upgrading
- Not accounting for support costs of free users — they still file tickets
- Assuming all free users should convert — some are your distribution; they bring paying users via virality
