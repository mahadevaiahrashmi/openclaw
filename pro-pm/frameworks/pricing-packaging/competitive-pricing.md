# Competitive Pricing

## What It Is
Competitive Pricing positions your price relative to competitors rather than based solely on cost or value. Three strategies: Premium (above competitors — justified by differentiation), Parity (match competitors — compete on other dimensions), and Penetration (below competitors — gain market share). Requires ongoing competitive intelligence and clear positioning.

## When to Use
- Entering a market with established players and known price benchmarks
- When customers actively compare prices before purchasing
- Setting a pricing floor (penetration) or ceiling (premium) based on the competitive landscape
- Complementing value-based pricing with market reality checks

## When NOT to Use
- Creating a new category with no direct competitors — use value-based pricing
- When your product is so differentiated that competitor prices are irrelevant
- As the sole pricing strategy — competitive pricing without understanding your own value is reactive

## How to Apply
1. **Map competitors**: List direct and indirect competitors. Identify their pricing models, tiers, and published prices
2. **Normalize for comparison**: Align on comparable units (per user/month, per 1K API calls, per feature set)
3. **Position your price**:
   - **Premium** (10–50% above): Requires clear differentiation. Justify with better features, support, brand, or reliability
   - **Parity** (within 10%): Compete on non-price factors (UX, integrations, support, ecosystem)
   - **Penetration** (10–30% below): Gain market share quickly. Must have a path to raise prices or reduce costs
4. **Monitor and adjust**: Track competitor price changes, new entrants, and market dynamics quarterly
5. **Communicate positioning**: Your price tells a story — premium = "we're better"; penetration = "we're accessible"

## Example
**Product**: Cloud monitoring tool
| Competitor | Per-host/month | Key Differentiator |
|-----------|---------------|-------------------|
| Datadog | $15 | Full-stack observability |
| New Relic | $12 | Developer-friendly |
| Our Product | **$10** | AI-powered alerts, simpler UX |

**Positioning**: Penetration pricing — 20–33% below incumbents to gain market share
**Justification**: Lower CAC through self-serve, lower infrastructure costs via efficient architecture
**Path to premium**: As market share grows and AI features mature, raise to parity ($12–15)

## Key Pitfalls
- Price wars — competing purely on price erodes margins for everyone
- Assuming lower price = more customers — pricing too low can signal low quality
- Not having a path from penetration to sustainable pricing
- Comparing apples to oranges — make sure you're normalizing correctly
- Ignoring indirect competitors and substitutes in your competitive set

## Related Frameworks
- **Value-Based Pricing** — determines what your product is worth independent of competitors
- **Penetration vs. Skimming** — strategic entry pricing decisions
- **Price Anchoring** — psychological positioning relative to competitors
- **Porter's Five Forces** — assesses competitive intensity that drives pricing dynamics
