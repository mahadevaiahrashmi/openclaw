# Van Westendorp Price Sensitivity Meter

## What It Is
The Van Westendorp Price Sensitivity Meter (PSM), developed by Dutch economist Peter van Westendorp, uses four pricing questions to identify an acceptable price range for a product. By plotting cumulative response curves, it reveals the Optimal Price Point, the Indifference Price Point, and the acceptable price range — all derived directly from customer perception.

## When to Use
- Setting initial pricing for a new product or feature
- Validating whether your current price is within the acceptable range
- Understanding price perception across customer segments
- Quick, low-cost pricing research (simpler than conjoint analysis)

## When NOT to Use
- Products where value is hard to conceptualize in a survey (very novel products)
- When you need to understand feature-price trade-offs — use conjoint analysis
- B2B enterprise with negotiated pricing — survey-based methods are less reliable

## How to Apply
1. **Ask four questions** (for a specific product/feature):
   - "At what price would this be **so cheap** you'd question its quality?" (Too Cheap)
   - "At what price would this be a **bargain** — great value for money?" (Cheap/Good Value)
   - "At what price would this be **getting expensive** but you'd still consider it?" (Expensive/High)
   - "At what price would this be **too expensive** to consider?" (Too Expensive)
2. **Plot cumulative distributions** for each question
3. **Find intersection points**:
   - **Point of Marginal Cheapness (PMC)**: Too Cheap = Expensive intersect
   - **Point of Marginal Expensiveness (PME)**: Too Expensive = Cheap intersect
   - **Optimal Price Point (OPP)**: Too Cheap = Too Expensive intersect
   - **Indifference Price Point (IDP)**: Cheap = Expensive intersect
4. **Acceptable range**: PMC to PME
5. **Set price** within the range based on positioning strategy

## Example
**Product**: AI writing assistant (monthly subscription, surveyed 200 content marketers)
- Too Cheap: Median $5/month — below this, they'd doubt quality
- Good Value: Median $15/month
- Getting Expensive: Median $35/month
- Too Expensive: Median $60/month
- **Acceptable Range**: $12–$42/month
- **Optimal Price**: $19/month
- **Decision**: Set at $25/month (premium positioning within acceptable range)

## Key Pitfalls
- Small sample sizes — need 100+ respondents for reliable curves
- Hypothetical bias — people say they'd pay more/less than they actually would
- Not segmenting — different customer types have different price sensitivity
- Using the Optimal Price Point as gospel — it's one input, not the final answer

## Related Frameworks
- **Conjoint Analysis** — more rigorous for feature-price trade-offs
- **Value-Based Pricing** — using value delivered as the price anchor
- **Competitive Pricing** — positioning relative to alternatives
- **Tiered Packaging** — structuring multiple price points within the acceptable range
