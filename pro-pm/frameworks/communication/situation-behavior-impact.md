# Situation-Behavior-Impact (SBI)

## What It Is
SBI is a feedback framework developed by the Center for Creative Leadership. It structures feedback into three components: the specific Situation where the behavior occurred, the observable Behavior (not interpretation), and the Impact it had. SBI removes subjectivity and judgment, making feedback concrete, actionable, and less likely to trigger defensiveness.

## When to Use
- Giving constructive feedback to direct reports, peers, or stakeholders
- Performance reviews and 1:1 conversations
- Reinforcing positive behavior (SBI works for praise, not just criticism)
- When previous feedback hasn't landed because it was too vague

## When NOT to Use
- Systemic issues that need process changes, not individual feedback
- When emotions are too high — wait until you can be factual, not reactive
- For feedback about traits ("you're lazy") — SBI focuses on behaviors, not character

## How to Apply
1. **Situation**: Describe the specific context. When and where did this happen?
   - "In yesterday's sprint review..." / "During the client call on Tuesday..."
2. **Behavior**: Describe what you observed — the specific, observable action. No interpretation
   - "You interrupted the designer three times during their presentation"
   - NOT: "You were disrespectful" (interpretation, not behavior)
3. **Impact**: Describe the effect of the behavior — on you, the team, the outcome
   - "The designer lost their train of thought, the presentation ran over, and the client didn't get the full picture"
4. **Optional — Ask**: "Can you help me understand what was happening?" or "How can we handle this differently next time?"

## Template
**Situation**: "During/In [specific context]..."

**Behavior**: "I observed that you [specific, observable action]..."

**Impact**: "The impact was [effect on people/outcome]..."

**Ask** (optional): "Can you help me understand...?" / "Going forward, could we...?"

## Example
**Constructive feedback**:
- **S**: "In yesterday's product review with the VP"
- **B**: "You presented the metrics without explaining the methodology changes we made last month"
- **I**: "The VP questioned our numbers and lost confidence in the data. We now need a follow-up meeting to rebuild trust"
- **Ask**: "Next time, can we add a brief methodology note to the deck so stakeholders understand the context?"

**Positive feedback**:
- **S**: "During the incident last Thursday"
- **B**: "You proactively pulled in the security team and documented a real-time timeline in Slack"
- **I**: "The team resolved the issue 2 hours faster than usual, and the post-mortem was the most thorough we've ever had"

## Key Pitfalls
- Using "you always" or "you never" — SBI requires a *specific* situation
- Describing interpretation as behavior — "you were rude" is interpretation; "you said X" is behavior
- Stacking multiple SBI points in one conversation — address one behavior at a time
- Skipping positive SBI — reinforcing good behavior is as important as correcting problems
- Delivering SBI publicly — do it privately for constructive feedback

## Related Frameworks
- **Nonviolent Communication** — deeper framework for difficult conversations
- **RACI/DACI** — role clarity can prevent the situations that need SBI feedback
- **Radical Candor** — the broader philosophy of caring personally while challenging directly
- **OKRs** — connects individual behavior to team outcomes
