# Lean Canvas

## What It Is
The Lean Canvas, created by Ash Maurya (Running Lean, 2012), is a startup-adapted version of the Business Model Canvas. It replaces Key Partners, Key Activities, Key Resources, and Customer Relationships with Problem, Solution, Key Metrics, and Unfair Advantage — focusing on what matters most in early-stage ventures: risk and validated learning.

## When to Use
- Early-stage products or startups where the business model is unvalidated
- Internal "new venture" projects exploring new markets or products
- Rapidly iterating on business hypotheses before committing resources
- Communicating a new idea to stakeholders in a structured, one-page format

## When NOT to Use
- Established products with proven business models — use Business Model Canvas
- Feature-level planning — too strategic for sprint backlogs
- When you need detailed financial projections — supplement with unit economics

## How to Apply
1. Fill in the nine blocks (start with the riskiest assumptions):
   - **Problem**: Top 3 problems your customers have. List existing alternatives
   - **Customer Segments**: Target customers. Identify early adopters specifically
   - **Unique Value Proposition**: Single, clear message that states why you're different and worth paying attention to
   - **Solution**: Top 3 features that address the top 3 problems
   - **Channels**: How you'll reach customers (acquisition paths)
   - **Revenue Streams**: How you'll make money. Pricing model
   - **Cost Structure**: Customer acquisition costs, distribution, hosting, people
   - **Key Metrics**: The 3–5 metrics that matter most right now (pirate metrics: AARRR)
   - **Unfair Advantage**: What can't be easily copied or bought (network effects, data, community, expertise)
2. **Identify riskiest assumptions** — mark blocks with highest uncertainty
3. **Design experiments** to test the riskiest assumptions first
4. **Update the canvas** as you learn — it's a living document

## Example
**Product**: AI meeting notes tool for remote teams
- **Problem**: 1) Important decisions get lost after meetings 2) Writing notes distracts from participation 3) Action items aren't tracked
- **Customer Segments**: Remote-first startups (50–200 employees)
- **Early Adopters**: Engineering managers running 10+ meetings/week
- **UVP**: "Never miss a decision. AI captures what matters so you can focus on the conversation."
- **Solution**: Auto-transcription, AI-extracted decisions and action items, Slack/Jira integration
- **Unfair Advantage**: Proprietary meeting-specific language model trained on customer data
- **Key Metrics**: Meetings processed/week, action item completion rate, NPS

## Key Pitfalls
- Spending too long on the canvas instead of testing — it should take 20 minutes, not 2 days
- Filling in "Unfair Advantage" with things that aren't actually hard to copy (e.g., "great team")
- Not listing existing alternatives — understanding current behavior is critical
- Treating the Lean Canvas as a plan rather than a set of hypotheses to test

## Related Frameworks
- **Business Model Canvas** — the original canvas for established business models
- **Lean Startup** — the methodology for testing Lean Canvas hypotheses
- **Value Proposition Canvas** — deep dive into Problem and UVP blocks
- **Assumption Mapping** — structured approach to testing the riskiest canvas blocks
