# Value Proposition Canvas

## What It Is
The Value Proposition Canvas, created by Alexander Osterwalder (Strategyzer), is a tool for designing and testing value propositions. It has two sides: the Customer Profile (jobs, pains, gains) and the Value Map (products/services, pain relievers, gain creators). The goal is achieving fit between what customers need and what you offer.

## When to Use
- Designing or refining your product's value proposition
- Validating whether your features actually address customer pains and gains
- Comparing your value proposition to competitors
- Customer discovery interviews — structuring what to learn

## When NOT to Use
- Full business model design — use the Business Model Canvas
- Feature prioritization — VPC defines *what matters*, then use RICE to prioritize *how*
- When you haven't talked to customers — VPC should be based on research, not assumptions

## How to Apply
1. **Build the Customer Profile** (right side):
   - **Customer Jobs**: What are they trying to accomplish? (functional, social, emotional)
   - **Pains**: What frustrates them? Risks, obstacles, negative outcomes
   - **Gains**: What do they want to achieve? Benefits, desires, positive outcomes
2. **Build the Value Map** (left side):
   - **Products & Services**: Your offering (features, services, support)
   - **Pain Relievers**: How do you eliminate or reduce pains?
   - **Gain Creators**: How do you create or increase gains?
3. **Check for fit**: Do your pain relievers address the most important pains? Do your gain creators deliver the most desired gains?
4. **Rank** pains and gains by importance — focus on the top ones
5. **Test** your assumptions with customer interviews and experiments

## Template
**Customer Profile**:
| Customer Jobs | Pains | Gains |
|--------------|-------|-------|
| 1. | 1. | 1. |
| 2. | 2. | 2. |
| 3. | 3. | 3. |

**Value Map**:
| Products/Services | Pain Relievers | Gain Creators |
|------------------|----------------|---------------|
| 1. | 1. | 1. |
| 2. | 2. | 2. |

**Fit Assessment**: Which pains/gains are addressed? Which are gaps?

## Example
**Product**: Team retrospective tool
**Customer Profile** (Engineering Manager):
- Jobs: Run effective retros, improve team processes, build trust
- Pains: Retros feel repetitive, quiet team members don't speak up, action items get forgotten
- Gains: Measurable team improvement, higher engagement, less meeting time

**Value Map**:
- Products: Anonymous input, AI-suggested discussion topics, action item tracking
- Pain Relievers: Anonymity addresses quiet members; templates prevent repetition
- Gain Creators: Trend dashboards show improvement over time; integrations with project tools

## Key Pitfalls
- Filling it in without customer research — the canvas is a hypothesis tool, not a creativity tool
- Listing every possible pain/gain instead of ranking by importance
- Building pain relievers for pains that don't matter much to customers
- Not iterating — your first canvas will be wrong; update it as you learn

## Related Frameworks
- **Business Model Canvas** — VPC is the detailed view of one BMC block (Value Propositions + Customer Segments)
- **Jobs to Be Done** — deep methodology for understanding customer jobs
- **Empathy Map** — complementary tool for understanding customer perspective
- **Lean Startup** — methodology for testing VPC hypotheses
