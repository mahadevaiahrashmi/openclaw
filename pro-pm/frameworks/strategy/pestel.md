# PESTEL Analysis

## What It Is
PESTEL is a macro-environmental analysis framework that examines six external factors affecting an organization: Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Environmental, and Legal. Originally PEST (without Environmental and Legal), it helps organizations understand the broader context in which they operate and anticipate external changes that could impact strategy.

## When to Use
- Strategic planning to identify external opportunities and threats
- Market entry analysis for new geographies or industries
- Risk assessment and scenario planning
- Complementing Porter's Five Forces with macro-level context

## When NOT to Use
- Product-level decisions — PESTEL is too macro for feature prioritization
- Internal analysis — PESTEL is purely external; pair with SWOT for internal factors
- Short-term tactical planning — these factors play out over months to years

## How to Apply
1. **Analyze each factor**:
   - **Political**: Government policies, trade restrictions, tax policy, political stability, regulation trends
   - **Economic**: GDP growth, interest rates, inflation, exchange rates, consumer confidence, unemployment
   - **Social**: Demographics, cultural trends, lifestyle changes, education levels, health consciousness
   - **Technological**: Innovation pace, R&D investment, automation, digital infrastructure, emerging tech
   - **Environmental**: Climate change, sustainability pressure, resource scarcity, carbon regulation, ESG requirements
   - **Legal**: Data privacy laws (GDPR, CCPA), employment law, IP protection, industry-specific regulation
2. **Rate impact** (High/Medium/Low) and **likelihood**
3. **Identify trends**: What's changing? What direction?
4. **Assess implications**: How does each factor affect your product/strategy?
5. **Prioritize**: Focus on high-impact, high-likelihood factors

## Example
**Product**: AI-powered HR software expanding to EU market
- **Political**: EU pushing AI regulation (AI Act) — High impact → Build compliance features
- **Economic**: Economic slowdown reducing HR tech budgets — Medium → Emphasize ROI and cost savings
- **Social**: Remote work normalization — High → Design for distributed workforce
- **Technological**: LLM capabilities advancing rapidly — High → Leverage but avoid vendor lock-in
- **Environmental**: Low direct impact for SaaS — Low → Monitor ESG reporting requirements
- **Legal**: GDPR and AI Act compliance mandatory — High → Privacy-by-design architecture required

## Key Pitfalls
- Making it too exhaustive — focus on factors that actually affect your product/market
- Treating it as a one-time exercise — macro factors change; reassess quarterly or semi-annually
- Analysis paralysis — PESTEL should inform decisions, not delay them
- Ignoring interconnections — factors interact (e.g., political changes drive legal changes)

## Related Frameworks
- **SWOT** — adds internal factors (Strengths, Weaknesses) to PESTEL's external analysis
- **Porter's Five Forces** — industry-level competitive analysis complementing macro PESTEL
- **Scenario Planning** — uses PESTEL factors to build multiple future scenarios
- **Wardley Mapping** — maps technological evolution that PESTEL identifies
