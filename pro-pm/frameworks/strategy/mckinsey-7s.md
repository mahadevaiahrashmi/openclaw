# McKinsey 7-S Framework

## What It Is
The McKinsey 7-S Framework (Tom Peters and Robert Waterman, 1980s) evaluates organizational effectiveness through seven interdependent elements: Strategy, Structure, Systems (hard elements) and Shared Values, Skills, Staff, Style (soft elements). All seven must be aligned for an organization to perform well. Change in one element requires adjustment in others.

## When to Use
- Organizational change management — ensuring all elements align with a new strategy
- Diagnosing why a good strategy isn't producing results
- Post-merger integration planning
- Assessing organizational readiness for a new product initiative or market entry

## When NOT to Use
- Product-level decisions — this is an organizational lens
- Quick tactical assessments — 7-S requires deep organizational analysis
- When you only control product/engineering — 7-S spans the entire organization

## How to Apply
1. **Audit each element**:
   - **Strategy**: The plan to build competitive advantage
   - **Structure**: How the org is organized (teams, reporting, hierarchy)
   - **Systems**: Processes, workflows, tools (IT systems, HR processes, planning cadences)
   - **Shared Values**: Core beliefs and culture that guide behavior (the center of the model)
   - **Skills**: Organizational capabilities and competencies
   - **Staff**: People, talent, roles, and development
   - **Style**: Leadership approach and organizational culture in practice
2. **Assess alignment**: Are all seven elements supporting the strategy?
3. **Identify gaps**: Where are elements in conflict or misaligned?
4. **Plan adjustments**: Changes needed in each element to achieve alignment
5. **Prioritize**: Start with Shared Values and Strategy, then align the rest

## Example
**Scenario**: Shifting from sales-led to product-led growth
- **Strategy**: ✅ PLG strategy defined
- **Structure**: ❌ No growth team; sales owns customer journey → Create growth squad
- **Systems**: ❌ No self-serve signup; CRM-centric tools → Build self-serve funnel
- **Shared Values**: ⚠️ Culture values big deals over user experience → Leadership messaging shift
- **Skills**: ❌ No product analytics expertise → Hire growth PM and data analyst
- **Staff**: ⚠️ Incentive structures reward sales, not product adoption → Adjust comp plans
- **Style**: ❌ Top-down, sales-driven decision making → Empower product teams with data

## Key Pitfalls
- Treating elements as independent — the power is in the interconnections
- Overweighting hard elements (Strategy, Structure, Systems) and ignoring soft ones
- Doing a 7-S analysis without involving people across the organization
- Using it as a one-time diagnostic instead of an ongoing alignment check

## Related Frameworks
- **Team Topologies** — detailed structure and interaction design for tech orgs
- **RACI/DACI** — role clarity that fits within the Structure and Systems elements
- **OKRs** — connects Strategy to measurable outcomes across the 7 elements
- **Spotify Model** — a specific structural model to evaluate against 7-S
