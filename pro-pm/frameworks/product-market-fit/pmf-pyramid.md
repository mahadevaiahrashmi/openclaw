# PMF Pyramid

## What It Is
The PMF Pyramid, created by Dan Olsen (The Lean Product Playbook, 2015), is a five-layer model for systematically achieving product-market fit. Each layer builds on the one below: Target Customer → Underserved Needs → Value Proposition → Feature Set → UX. The bottom two layers are the "market" and the top three are the "product." PMF exists when all five layers are aligned.

## When to Use
- Systematically building toward product-market fit for a new product
- Diagnosing why you don't have PMF — which layer is misaligned?
- Product strategy reviews to check alignment across all layers
- Pivoting — which layer needs to change?

## When NOT to Use
- Post-PMF growth optimization — use growth and retention frameworks
- Quick feature decisions — the pyramid is strategic, not tactical
- When you're already in scale mode with proven PMF

## How to Apply
1. **Target Customer** (bottom): Who is your ideal customer? Define with specificity — demographics, firmographics, and behavioral characteristics
2. **Underserved Needs**: What needs does this customer have that current solutions don't adequately address? Use JTBD or ODI to identify
3. **Value Proposition**: How does your product uniquely address these needs better than alternatives? What's your differentiation?
4. **Feature Set**: What specific features deliver on the value proposition? Prioritize ruthlessly — only features that directly serve the value prop
5. **UX** (top): How do users experience these features? Is the UX good enough that value is actually delivered?
6. **Iterate**: If PMF isn't achieved, work down the pyramid to find the misaligned layer. Don't change UX if the problem is in Target Customer

## Example
**Product**: AI writing assistant for marketing teams
- **Target Customer**: Content marketing managers at B2B SaaS companies (50–500 employees)
- **Underserved Needs**: "Produce high-quality blog posts faster without sounding generic" (current AI tools produce generic content)
- **Value Proposition**: Brand-trained AI that writes in your company's voice using your existing content as training data
- **Feature Set**: Brand voice training, content generation, editing suggestions, SEO optimization
- **UX**: Currently poor — training takes 2 hours and requires technical steps → **this is the misaligned layer**
- **Action**: Simplify the brand voice training to a 5-minute wizard. Don't change the value prop or feature set

## Key Pitfalls
- Changing the top layers (UX/features) when the problem is in the bottom layers (wrong customer/need)
- Skipping layers — you can't build a good UX for features that don't serve real needs
- Not validating each layer before building the next — use customer research at each step
- Assuming PMF is binary — it's a spectrum; you can have partial fit in one segment

## Related Frameworks
- **Sean Ellis Test** — quantitative measure of PMF that the pyramid helps you achieve
- **Superhuman Framework** — operational approach to improving PMF scores
- **Value Proposition Canvas** — detailed tool for the middle layers (needs + value prop)
- **Jobs to Be Done** — methodology for layer 2 (underserved needs)
