# Superhuman PMF Engine

## What It Is
The Superhuman Framework, developed by Rahul Vohra (CEO of Superhuman), is an operational system for systematically improving product-market fit. It extends the Sean Ellis Test with a structured process: survey users, segment by enthusiasm, understand what the "Very Disappointed" users love, build a roadmap from "Somewhat Disappointed" users' feedback, and track your PMF score over time. It turned PMF from a vague concept into a repeatable process.

## When to Use
- You've measured PMF (Sean Ellis Test) and scored below 40% — you need a system to improve
- You want to continuously increase PMF over time, not just measure it once
- Finding and deepening your product's appeal with the right customer segment
- Building a data-driven roadmap based on user enthusiasm

## When NOT to Use
- Pre-product with no users to survey
- Products that already have strong PMF and need growth frameworks instead
- When your sample size is too small for meaningful segmentation (<50 responses)

## How to Apply
1. **Survey**: Run the Sean Ellis Test with follow-up questions (see Sean Ellis Test framework)
2. **Segment**: Identify who the "Very Disappointed" users are — this is your high-expectation customer (HXC)
3. **Understand your strength**: From the "Very Disappointed" group, what is the main benefit they cite? This is your core value — never compromise it
4. **Build a roadmap from "Somewhat Disappointed"**: These users almost love you. Filter their improvement suggestions by: would this also appeal to "Very Disappointed" users? If yes, build it. If it would dilute what VD users love, skip it
5. **Ignore "Not Disappointed"**: Don't build for people who don't care about your product
6. **Track weekly**: Re-run the survey quarterly. Track your PMF score as a leading indicator
7. **Persona creation**: Build your ICP/persona from the "Very Disappointed" segment — demographics, use cases, motivations

## Example
**Product**: Email client (inspired by Superhuman's own journey)
- **PMF Score**: 33% (below 40%)
- **VD Segment**: Founders and execs who process 100+ emails/day and value speed
- **Main benefit**: "Fastest email experience I've ever used. I feel in control of my inbox"
- **SD Feedback**: "Need better calendar integration" and "Mobile app needs improvement"
- **Filter**: Calendar integration → Would VD users also want this? Yes — build it. Add a social feed? No — VD users want focused productivity → skip
- **Result**: After 3 quarters of building for the VD segment, PMF score rose from 33% to 58%

## Key Pitfalls
- Building for "Not Disappointed" users — they'll never love your product; don't chase them
- Losing sight of what "Very Disappointed" users love while chasing broader appeal
- Not re-surveying — PMF is dynamic; measure it regularly
- Treating the framework as a one-time exercise instead of an ongoing engine
- Small sample sizes in segments — ensure at least 20 responses per segment for directional insight
