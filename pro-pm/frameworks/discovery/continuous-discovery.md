# Continuous Discovery Habits

## What It Is
Continuous Discovery, defined by Teresa Torres (Continuous Discovery Habits, 2021), is a set of weekly practices that keep product teams connected to customers. The core habits: conduct at least one customer interview per week, map opportunities using an Opportunity Solution Tree, test assumptions with small experiments, and use these insights to make better product decisions continuously — not in batches.

## When to Use
- Any product team that wants to make more customer-informed decisions
- When discovery happens in quarterly "research phases" instead of continuously
- Teams transitioning from output-focused (features shipped) to outcome-focused (metrics moved)
- When the team is building things customers don't want or use

## When NOT to Use
- Crisis mode where you need to ship a fix immediately
- Pure infrastructure or platform work with no direct customer
- When the team is too small to sustain weekly cadence — do biweekly at minimum

## How to Apply
1. **Weekly customer touchpoint**: Interview at least one customer per week. Automate recruitment. Use story-based interviewing (not opinion-based)
2. **Map opportunities**: Maintain an Opportunity Solution Tree. Add opportunities discovered from interviews, support tickets, and analytics
3. **Prioritize opportunities**: Use the tree to compare and select which opportunities to pursue
4. **Generate multiple solutions**: For each opportunity, brainstorm at least 3 possible solutions before choosing
5. **Test assumptions**: Identify the riskiest assumption in your chosen solution. Design the smallest possible experiment to test it
6. **Iterate**: Use experiment results to refine or pivot. Update the tree weekly
7. **Involve the trio**: PM, Designer, and Tech Lead participate together in interviews and mapping

**Weekly Discovery Cadence**:

## Example
**Week 12 Discovery Log**:
- Interviewed 2 customers (mid-market ops managers)
- New opportunity discovered: "Teams can't tell which automations are actually saving time"
- Added to OST under "Improve automation ROI visibility"
- Generated 3 solutions: usage dashboard, weekly email digest, ROI calculator
- Riskiest assumption: "Users will engage with a dashboard" (they might not log in)
- Experiment: Send a weekly email summary to 50 users, measure open and click rates

## Key Pitfalls
- Treating interviews as usability tests — discovery interviews explore problems, not test solutions
- Asking leading or opinion-based questions — use "tell me about the last time you..." instead
- Not involving engineering in discovery — feasibility insights shape better solutions
- Doing discovery without connecting it to delivery — insights must feed the backlog
