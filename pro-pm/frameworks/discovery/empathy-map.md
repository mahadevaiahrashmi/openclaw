# Empathy Map

## What It Is
The Empathy Map is a collaborative visualization tool used to synthesize what you know about a user or customer. It organizes observations and insights into four quadrants — Think & Feel, See, Say & Do, Hear — plus their Pains and Gains. Created by Dave Gray (XPLANE), it's a quick, visual way to build shared understanding of a user segment.

## When to Use
- After user interviews to synthesize findings with your team
- Creating or refining personas based on research
- Workshop exercises to align on user understanding before ideation
- When starting a new project and need a quick user empathy baseline

## When NOT to Use
- As a replacement for actual user research — it organizes research, it doesn't replace it
- For quantitative analysis — this is a qualitative tool
- When you need detailed behavioral data — use analytics and cohort analysis

## How to Apply
1. **Choose the user**: Pick a specific user segment or persona to map
2. **Gather data**: Use interview notes, observation data, support tickets, survey responses
3. **Fill in quadrants** (best done as a team exercise with sticky notes):
   - **Think & Feel**: What are their worries, aspirations, and unspoken thoughts?
   - **See**: What is their environment? What do they see others doing? What are they exposed to?
   - **Say & Do**: What have they actually said in interviews? What behaviors have you observed?
   - **Hear**: What do colleagues, friends, influencers tell them? What media do they consume?
4. **Identify Pains**: Frustrations, obstacles, risks, fears
5. **Identify Gains**: Wants, needs, measures of success, desires
6. **Synthesize**: What patterns emerge? What surprises? What conflicts between quadrants?

## Template
```
         ┌───────────────────────────────────┐
         │         THINK & FEEL              │
         │  What matters? Worries? Dreams?   │
┌────────┼───────────────────────────────────┼────────┐
│  HEAR  │                                   │  SEE   │
│        │        [USER/PERSONA]             │        │
│ What   │                                   │ What's │
│ others │                                   │ in     │
│ say    │                                   │ their  │
│        │                                   │ world  │
└────────┼───────────────────────────────────┼────────┘
         │          SAY & DO                 │
         │  Actual quotes, observed behavior │
         └───────────────────────────────────┘
         PAINS: ___          GAINS: ___
```

## Example
**User**: Mid-level engineering manager (first-time manager)
- **Think & Feel**: "Am I doing this right? I miss coding. I worry about my team's morale"
- **See**: Senior managers doing skip-levels, Slack messages about burnout, team dashboards with red metrics
- **Say & Do**: Says "I'm fine" in 1:1s with their own manager. Does late-night Slack responses. Avoids difficult feedback conversations
- **Hear**: "You should be more strategic" from VP. "We need more headcount" from team. Podcasts about engineering leadership
- **Pains**: No training for management, unclear expectations, lonely role transition
- **Gains**: Team trust, career growth, seeing reports get promoted

## Key Pitfalls
- Filling it in from imagination instead of research data — every quadrant should be grounded in evidence
- Making it too generic — specific observations are more useful than broad generalizations
- Creating the map and never referencing it again — post it where the team can see it
- Conflating what users say with what they actually do — the Say & Do quadrant should capture both and note contradictions

## Related Frameworks
- **Value Proposition Canvas** — connects pains/gains to your offering
- **Jobs to Be Done** — deeper framework for understanding user motivation
- **Design Thinking** — empathy maps support the Empathize phase
- **Persona** — empathy maps can inform or validate personas
