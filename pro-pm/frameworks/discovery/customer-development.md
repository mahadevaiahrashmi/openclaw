# Customer Development

## What It Is
Customer Development, created by Steve Blank (The Four Steps to the Epiphany, 2005), is a four-step framework for building products customers actually want. It separates the "search" phase (finding a business model) from the "execution" phase (scaling it). The four steps: Customer Discovery, Customer Validation, Customer Creation, and Company Building.

## When to Use
- Building a new product or entering a new market
- When you're unsure whether your business model assumptions are correct
- Startup teams searching for product-market fit
- Intrapreneurial teams launching new products within larger organizations

## When NOT to Use
- Scaling a validated business model — you're past customer development
- Incremental feature improvements on a mature product — use continuous discovery
- When the market and business model are well-known (e.g., another CRM for SMBs)

## How to Apply
1. **Customer Discovery** (Search):
   - State your business model hypotheses (problem, customer, channel, revenue)
   - "Get out of the building" — interview 50–100 potential customers
   - Test problem hypotheses: Do customers have this problem? Is it painful?
   - Test solution hypotheses: Does your proposed solution resonate?
   - Pivot until you find problem-solution fit
2. **Customer Validation** (Search):
   - Build MVP and attempt to sell it
   - Develop a repeatable sales process
   - Validate that customers will pay and that the business model works
   - If not → return to Customer Discovery (pivot)
3. **Customer Creation** (Execution):
   - Scale demand creation (marketing, sales)
   - Choose a market type strategy: existing, resegmented, new, or clone
   - Build end-user demand and drive it into the sales channel
4. **Company Building** (Execution):
   - Transition from learning org to execution org
   - Build formal departments, processes, and management
   - Scale operations to match demand

## Template
| Phase | Key Questions | Activities | Exit Criteria |
|-------|--------------|-----------|---------------|
| Customer Discovery | Is the problem real? | 50+ interviews | Problem validated |
| Customer Validation | Will they pay? | Sell MVP, track metrics | Repeatable sales |
| Customer Creation | Can we scale demand? | Marketing campaigns | Predictable pipeline |
| Company Building | Can we scale operations? | Hire, process, structure | Org supports growth |

## Example
**Product**: Compliance automation for fintech startups
1. **Discovery**: Interviewed 60 fintech founders → validated that SOC 2 compliance is painful, expensive, and blocks enterprise deals
2. **Validation**: Built MVP (checklist + policy templates), sold to 10 companies at $500/month. 7 renewed. Repeatable sales pitch identified
3. **Creation**: Launched content marketing targeting "fintech + SOC 2" keywords, partnership with fintech accelerators
4. **Building**: Hired sales team, formalized onboarding process, built self-serve tier

## Key Pitfalls
- Skipping Discovery and going straight to building — the #1 startup mistake
- Treating Customer Validation as "demo to friends" — you need actual purchases
- Scaling (Creation/Building) before validating — premature scaling kills startups
- Not pivoting when Discovery shows your hypothesis is wrong — ego overrides data

## Related Frameworks
- **Lean Startup** — Eric Ries built on Blank's customer development model
- **Problem Interview** — the specific technique for Customer Discovery conversations
- **Lean Canvas** — captures the hypotheses you're testing in each phase
- **Crossing the Chasm** — addresses the market-type challenges in Customer Creation
