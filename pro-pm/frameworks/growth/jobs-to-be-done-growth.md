# JTBD for Growth

## What It Is
Applying Jobs to Be Done theory specifically for growth means identifying underserved jobs to expand your market, creating "switching moments" that trigger adoption, and understanding the forces that drive or resist change. This connects JTBD's customer motivation theory directly to acquisition, activation, and retention strategies.

## When to Use
- Identifying new growth opportunities beyond your current user base
- Understanding why users switch to or from your product (churn analysis)
- Creating marketing messages that resonate with customer motivation
- Designing onboarding that addresses the specific "hiring criteria" for your product

## When NOT to Use
- Optimizing existing funnels with A/B tests — JTBD for growth is strategic, not tactical
- When you need quantitative growth metrics — pair with analytics frameworks
- Products in mature markets where jobs are well-served — look for new jobs instead

## How to Apply
1. **Identify the job**: What job is the customer hiring your product to do?
2. **Map the Four Forces of switching**:
   - **Push**: Frustration with current solution (drives change)
   - **Pull**: Attraction of new solution (drives change)
   - **Anxiety**: Fear of the new solution (resists change)
   - **Habit**: Attachment to current solution (resists change)
3. **Find switching moments**: Life events, workflow changes, or frustrations that create openness to change
4. **Design for the forces**: Amplify push and pull in marketing. Reduce anxiety and habit friction in onboarding
5. **Identify underserved jobs** for market expansion: What related jobs do your best customers have that you don't address?

## Example
**Product**: Project management tool (targeting users switching from spreadsheets)
- **Push**: Spreadsheets can't handle 10+ people or cross-team visibility (frustration grows with team size)
- **Pull**: Real-time updates, automated notifications, visual dashboards
- **Anxiety**: "Will my team actually adopt this? What if we lose our current data?"
- **Habit**: Team has years of spreadsheet templates and muscle memory
- **Switching Moment**: New team member joins and says "Why aren't we using a real PM tool?"
- **Growth Action**: Target onboarding messaging at the "new team member" moment. Offer spreadsheet import to reduce habit friction. Free trial for entire team to reduce anxiety about adoption

## Key Pitfalls
- Focusing only on pull (how great your product is) and ignoring push (what makes them leave the current solution)
- Underestimating habit and anxiety — these forces often outweigh rational product comparisons
- Not identifying specific switching moments — vague "people will switch when ready" isn't actionable
- Assuming everyone has the same job — segment by job to find the most underserved group
