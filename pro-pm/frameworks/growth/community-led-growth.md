# Community-Led Growth (CLG)

## What It Is
Community-Led Growth is a go-to-market strategy where community becomes a primary driver of acquisition, engagement, and retention. Users gather around shared interests, help each other, create content, and advocate for the product. CLG builds a flywheel where community engagement drives product adoption, which attracts more community members.

## When to Use
- Developer tools, open-source products, or products with passionate user bases
- When word-of-mouth is a significant acquisition channel
- Products where users can help each other (reducing support costs)
- Building long-term brand loyalty and defensibility in competitive markets

## When NOT to Use
- Products without a natural community (users don't identify as a group)
- Early-stage products where you need faster, more direct growth
- When community management resources aren't available — communities need investment
- Products where users don't want to be identified publicly (sensitive use cases)

## How to Apply
1. **Identify the community type**: User forum, Slack/Discord, meetups, open-source contributors, content creators, certification programs
2. **Define the value exchange**: Why would someone join? What do they get? (Learning, networking, status, early access, influence on product)
3. **Seed the community**: Start small with your most passionate users. Provide exceptional experiences to the first 50–100 members
4. **Create engagement loops**: Q&A → expertise recognition → more Q&A. Content creation → distribution → new members → more content
5. **Connect community to product**: Feature requests from community → shipped features → community appreciation → more engagement
6. **Measure**: Community size, engagement rate, community-attributed signups, support deflection, NPS of community members vs. non-members

## Example
**Product**: dbt (data transformation tool)
- **Community**: dbt Community Slack (50K+ members), annual conference, local meetups
- **Value exchange**: Learn analytics engineering, share best practices, career development, shape the tool
- **Growth loop**: New analyst struggles → asks in Slack → gets help → becomes expert → helps others → advocates at work → company adopts dbt
- **Community → Product**: Community contributions become packages; community feedback shapes roadmap
- **Impact**: Community members convert to paid at 3x the rate of non-community users

## Key Pitfalls
- Building a "community" that's actually just a support forum — community requires genuine two-way value
- Over-moderating or under-moderating — find the balance that maintains quality without stifling conversation
- Expecting immediate ROI — community is a long-term investment (6–12 months to see meaningful impact)
- Ignoring community feedback — the fastest way to kill a community is to make members feel unheard
