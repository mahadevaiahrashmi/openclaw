# Viral Loop Design

## What It Is
Viral Loop Design is the intentional engineering of product mechanics that cause users to invite or expose others to the product as a natural part of usage. A viral loop has three measurable properties: viral coefficient (K-factor — how many new users each existing user generates), cycle time (how long one loop takes), and the balance of payoff vs. friction at each step.

## When to Use
- Designing product features that inherently spread through usage
- Optimizing an existing referral or sharing mechanism
- When CAC is high and you need organic distribution
- Products with collaborative, social, or sharing-based use cases

## When NOT to Use
- Products used in isolation with no natural sharing moment
- When the product isn't good enough to recommend — viral mechanics amplify quality, good or bad
- As a substitute for product quality — no amount of viral engineering saves a bad product

## How to Apply
1. **Identify the natural sharing moment**: When does usage naturally involve or benefit another person?
2. **Design the loop**: User A does [action] → [sharing mechanism] → User B discovers product → User B signs up → User B does [action] → repeat
3. **Minimize friction at each step**: Reduce clicks, remove unnecessary fields, pre-fill content
4. **Maximize payoff**: Make sharing rewarding for both sender (recognition, utility) and receiver (value, not spam)
5. **Measure**:
   - **Viral Coefficient (K)**: K = invites per user × conversion rate per invite. K > 1 = exponential growth
   - **Cycle Time**: Shorter cycles compound faster (daily loops >> monthly loops)
6. **Optimize**: Increase K through better UX. Reduce cycle time through triggers and reminders
7. **Layer viral types**: Inherent virality (product requires sharing) > Collaborative virality (product improves with others) > Word-of-mouth > Artificial incentives

## Example
**Product**: Collaborative whiteboard tool
- **Natural moment**: User creates a board for a meeting and shares the link with attendees
- **Loop**: Create board → share link → attendees join (some sign up) → create their own boards → share with their teams
- **K-factor**: 3 invites/user × 25% conversion = 0.75 (sub-viral but meaningful organic growth)
- **Cycle time**: ~7 days (meeting cadence)
- **Optimization**: Make the shared board visually impressive (receiver payoff). Add "Powered by [Product]" watermark on free tier. Prompt board creators to share templates publicly

## Key Pitfalls
- Obsessing over K > 1 — very few products achieve true virality; K = 0.5 still means 50% organic growth
- Making sharing feel spammy — LinkedIn invitation syndrome kills goodwill
- Artificial incentives without inherent value — "Invite 5 friends for a free month" has limited shelf life
- Not measuring cycle time — a K of 2 with a 90-day cycle grows slower than K of 0.5 with a 1-day cycle

## Related Frameworks
- **Growth Loops** — viral loops are one type of growth loop
- **Network Effects** — viral loops can create network effects, but they're different concepts
- **Hook Model** — the investment step often creates sharing triggers
- **Product-Led Growth** — viral loops are a key PLG mechanic
