# Product-Led Growth (PLG)

## What It Is
Product-Led Growth is a go-to-market strategy where the product itself is the primary vehicle for customer acquisition, conversion, and expansion. Instead of relying on sales teams to demo and close deals, PLG companies offer free tiers or trials that let users experience value before purchasing. Key metrics include Product Qualified Leads (PQLs) and time-to-value.

## When to Use
- Products with low barriers to adoption and quick time-to-value
- Markets where end-users have purchasing power or strong influence
- When you want to reduce CAC and accelerate top-of-funnel growth
- Products that benefit from viral/collaborative usage patterns

## When NOT to Use
- Complex enterprise products requiring extensive customization or integration
- Products targeting executives who don't use the product themselves
- Markets where regulatory or security requirements mandate sales-led procurement
- Products with high marginal cost per user (PLG assumes near-zero marginal cost)

## How to Apply
1. **Design for self-serve**: Users must be able to sign up, set up, and experience value without human assistance
2. **Minimize time-to-value**: How quickly can a new user achieve their first meaningful outcome? Target: minutes, not days
3. **Create a free tier or trial**: Freemium (limited features), free trial (limited time), or reverse trial (full features, then downgrade)
4. **Define your PQL**: A Product Qualified Lead is a user who has experienced enough value that they're likely to convert. Define based on product usage signals
5. **Build expansion mechanics**: Usage-based pricing, seat expansion, team features that encourage organic growth within organizations
6. **Layer in sales**: PLG ≠ no sales. Use sales for enterprise deals, expansion, and high-touch segments (product-led sales)

## Example
**Product**: Figma
- **Self-serve**: Sign up with email, start designing immediately in browser
- **Time-to-value**: ~2 minutes (create first design, no install required)
- **Free tier**: Generous free plan (3 projects, unlimited collaborators)
- **PQL**: User who has created 3+ projects with 2+ collaborators
- **Expansion**: Team features (shared libraries, admin controls) → organization buys paid plan
- **Viral loop**: Designer shares a Figma link → collaborator signs up to view/edit → becomes a user

## Key Pitfalls
- "Build it and they will come" — PLG still requires distribution (content, community, partnerships)
- Free tier that's too generous (no conversion pressure) or too restrictive (no value experienced)
- Ignoring enterprise needs — PLG companies still need sales for large accounts
- Not measuring PQLs — without a PQL definition, you can't bridge product usage to revenue
