# Pirate Metrics (AARRR)

## What It Is
AARRR (Pirate Metrics), created by Dave McClure (500 Startups), is a framework for measuring and optimizing the customer lifecycle across five stages: Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Revenue, and Referral. Each stage has specific metrics that help teams identify where the biggest leaks are in the funnel and focus improvement efforts accordingly.

## When to Use
- Building a metrics dashboard for your product
- Diagnosing where users drop off in the lifecycle
- Prioritizing growth investments — fix the biggest leak first
- Structuring growth team OKRs around lifecycle stages

## When NOT to Use
- B2B enterprise with long, complex sales cycles — adapt the stages significantly
- Pre-product products with no users — there's nothing to measure yet
- As a replacement for a North Star Metric — AARRR is a diagnostic framework, not a goal-setting one

## How to Apply
1. **Acquisition**: How do users find you? Metrics: traffic, signups, CAC by channel
2. **Activation**: Do users have a great first experience? Metrics: setup completion, time-to-value, "aha moment" rate
3. **Retention**: Do users come back? Metrics: DAU/MAU, D7/D30 retention, churn rate
4. **Revenue**: Do users pay? Metrics: conversion rate, ARPU, LTV, MRR
5. **Referral**: Do users tell others? Metrics: viral coefficient, NPS, referral rate
6. **Identify the bottleneck**: Which stage has the biggest drop-off?
7. **Prioritize**: Fix the leakiest stage first — no point acquiring users who don't activate

## Example
**Product**: B2B SaaS productivity tool
- **Acquisition**: 10K visitors/month (organic + paid) → 1,200 signups (12% conversion)
- **Activation**: 1,200 signups → 360 complete onboarding (30%) ← **biggest leak**
- **Retention**: 360 activated → 252 return in week 2 (70%)
- **Revenue**: 252 retained → 76 convert to paid (30%)
- **Referral**: 76 paid → 8 refer a colleague (10.5%)
→ Focus: Fix activation — 70% of signups never complete onboarding

## Key Pitfalls
- Treating AARRR as sequential — referral can happen at any stage, not just after revenue
- Optimizing acquisition when activation is broken — you're filling a leaky bucket
- Using vanity metrics (total signups) instead of stage-specific conversion rates
- Not segmenting by cohort — aggregate metrics hide important trends
