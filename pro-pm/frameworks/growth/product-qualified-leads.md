# Product Qualified Leads (PQLs)

## What It Is
A Product Qualified Lead (PQL) is a user or account that has experienced meaningful product value and exhibits behaviors indicating readiness to convert to paid. Unlike Marketing Qualified Leads (MQLs) based on content engagement, PQLs are based on product usage data. PQLs have significantly higher conversion rates because the user has already demonstrated that the product solves their problem.

## When to Use
- Product-Led Growth motions with free tiers or trials
- Bridging product usage data with sales team outreach
- When MQL-based qualification isn't effective (low conversion, sales frustration)
- Designing the handoff from self-serve to sales-assisted conversion

## When NOT to Use
- Pure sales-led motions with no free or trial tier
- Products without usage tracking infrastructure
- When you don't have enough conversion data to identify PQL signals (need 100+ conversions to analyze)

## How to Apply
1. **Analyze converted users**: Look at users who became paying customers. What did they do in the product before converting?
2. **Identify leading indicators**: Correlate product actions with conversion. Common signals:
   - Feature usage depth (used 3+ core features)
   - Collaboration actions (invited 2+ team members)
   - Volume thresholds (created 10+ projects, processed 1000+ records)
   - Time-based engagement (active 5+ days in first 2 weeks)
3. **Define PQL criteria**: Combine signals into a scoring model or threshold-based definition
4. **Score and route**: Automatically flag PQLs in your CRM. Route to sales for high-value accounts, trigger self-serve upgrade prompts for smaller accounts
5. **Measure and iterate**: Track PQL → paid conversion rate. Refine criteria quarterly
6. **Feedback loop**: Sales team confirms or rejects PQL quality — adjust scoring

## Example
**Product**: Team collaboration tool
- **PQL Definition**: Account where 3+ users are active AND the team has created 5+ projects AND at least 1 user has used an advanced feature (automations, integrations) in the last 7 days
- **PQL → Paid Conversion**: 25% (vs. MQL → Paid: 3%)
- **Routing**: Accounts with >20 users → enterprise sales. Accounts with 3–20 users → in-app upgrade prompt + SDR follow-up email
- **Iteration**: Added "integration usage" to PQL criteria after analysis showed users with integrations convert at 40% vs. 15% without

## Key Pitfalls
- Defining PQLs based on assumptions instead of conversion data — analyze what actual converters did
- Too many PQL signals — keep it simple (3–5 criteria) and refine over time
- Sales team ignoring PQLs — they need to understand why PQLs are better leads than MQLs
- Static PQL definitions — user behavior and product evolve; revisit quarterly
- Not distinguishing individual PQLs from account-level PQLs — enterprise deals are account-level

## Related Frameworks
- **Product-Led Growth** — PQLs are the bridge between product usage and revenue
- **Activation Framework** — activation often precedes PQL status
- **Pirate Metrics (AARRR)** — PQLs sit between Activation and Revenue
- **Sales-Led Growth** — PQLs help SLG teams prioritize outreach
