# Sales-Led Growth (SLG)

## What It Is
Sales-Led Growth is a go-to-market strategy where the sales team is the primary engine for acquiring and expanding customers. It relies on consultative selling, relationship building, and solution selling — typically for complex, high-ACV products where buyers need guidance. SLG is the traditional enterprise model and remains dominant for products with long sales cycles and multiple stakeholders.

## When to Use
- Complex products requiring customization, integration, or deployment assistance
- High ACV deals ($50K+) where the economics justify dedicated sales resources
- Buyers who are executives or committees, not individual end-users
- Regulated industries where procurement processes are formal and lengthy
- Products that require organizational change management to adopt

## When NOT to Use
- Low-ACV products where the cost of sales exceeds the deal value
- Products with instant, self-serve value delivery — PLG is more efficient
- Markets where end-users drive purchasing decisions (bottom-up adoption)
- When your product isn't mature enough to sell consultatively (still finding PMF)

## How to Apply
1. **Define your ICP** (Ideal Customer Profile): Firmographic and technographic criteria for companies most likely to buy and succeed
2. **Build the sales motion**:
   - **Land**: Sell into one team or use case. Start with a wedge that solves an immediate, painful problem
   - **Expand**: Once established, grow within the account (more seats, more use cases, more departments)
3. **Design the sales process**: Prospecting → Discovery → Demo → Proof of Value (POC/pilot) → Proposal → Negotiation → Close
4. **Enable sales**: Product demos, battle cards, ROI calculators, case studies, objection handling
5. **Align product**: Build features that support the sales motion (SSO, audit logs, admin controls, APIs)
6. **Measure**: Pipeline velocity, win rate, average deal size, sales cycle length, net revenue retention

## Example
**Product**: Enterprise data governance platform
- **ICP**: Companies with 1000+ employees, in regulated industries, with a Chief Data Officer
- **Land**: Start with data catalog for one business unit ($80K/year)
- **Expand**: Add data quality monitoring ($50K), compliance reporting ($40K), expand to 3 more business units
- **Sales cycle**: 4–6 months including POC
- **Key enablement**: ROI calculator showing compliance fine avoidance, security architecture review document

## Key Pitfalls
- Over-relying on sales heroics instead of building a repeatable process
- Not investing in product experience because "sales will explain it" — even enterprise buyers expect good UX
- Land-and-expand without a clear expansion playbook — "we'll figure it out later" rarely works
- Ignoring product-led signals — even SLG companies benefit from usage data to guide expansion
