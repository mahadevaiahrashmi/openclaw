# Expansion Revenue

## What It Is
Expansion Revenue is the additional revenue generated from existing customers through upselling (higher tiers), cross-selling (new products), and usage expansion (more seats, more usage). It's measured through Net Revenue Retention (NRR), which captures expansion minus churn. NRR > 100% means you're growing even without new customers — the hallmark of healthy SaaS.

## When to Use
- When you have a solid customer base and want to grow revenue efficiently
- Building a land-and-expand motion within accounts
- When CAC is rising and you need more capital-efficient growth
- Designing pricing and packaging to encourage natural expansion

## When NOT to Use
- Products with only one tier and no expansion path — redesign pricing first
- When churn is high — fix retention before focusing on expansion
- Very early-stage products without enough customers to expand within

## How to Apply
1. **Define expansion paths**:
   - **Upsell**: Move customers to higher tiers (more features, higher limits)
   - **Cross-sell**: Sell additional products or modules
   - **Usage expansion**: Organic growth through more seats, usage, or storage
2. **Identify expansion triggers**: What signals that a customer is ready for more? (Hitting limits, adding team members, using advanced features)
3. **Build expansion into the product**: In-app upgrade prompts, usage dashboards, team management features
4. **Measure**:
   - **Net Revenue Retention (NRR)**: (Starting MRR + Expansion - Contraction - Churn) / Starting MRR
   - **Gross Revenue Retention (GRR)**: (Starting MRR - Contraction - Churn) / Starting MRR
   - **Expansion MRR**: Monthly recurring revenue from upsells, cross-sells, and seat additions
5. **Benchmark**: NRR > 120% = excellent. 100–120% = good. <100% = net revenue churn

## Example
**Product**: Cloud monitoring platform
- **Upsell trigger**: Customer hits 80% of host limit on current tier → prompt upgrade with comparison table
- **Cross-sell**: Customer uses infrastructure monitoring → offer APM (application performance monitoring) add-on
- **Usage expansion**: Customer adds new services → automatic seat/host expansion at contracted per-unit price
- **NRR**: 135% — strong expansion more than offsets churn
- **Key driver**: Auto-expanding host-based pricing means customers grow without needing to "buy" anything

## Key Pitfalls
- Aggressive expansion that feels like nickel-and-diming — expansion should deliver proportional value
- No clear upgrade path in your pricing — if customers can't expand, they can't expand
- Measuring NRR without segmenting — enterprise NRR may be 150% while SMB is 85%
- Ignoring contraction — expansion revenue means nothing if just as many customers are downgrading

## Related Frameworks
- **Land and Expand** — the strategic motion for driving expansion
- **Unit Economics** — LTV improves dramatically with strong expansion revenue
- **Tiered Packaging** — pricing structure that enables upselling
- **Product Qualified Leads** — identifying which users/accounts are expansion-ready
