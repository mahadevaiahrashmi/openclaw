# New Market Pricing

## Overview
Playbook for developing pricing and packaging strategy for entering a new market—whether a new geography, vertical, customer segment, or product line. Use when existing pricing doesn't fit the new market context.

## Prerequisites
- New market opportunity validated (use market-expansion playbook)
- Target customer segment defined with ICP
- Competitive landscape in the new market analyzed
- Finance team available for modeling
- Sales or GTM team for the new market identified or planned

## Phases

### Phase 1: Market Research
**Objective:** Understand pricing dynamics, willingness to pay, and competitive pricing in the target market
**Skills/Agents Used:** `competitive-intel`, `research-analyst`
**Activities:**
- Research competitive pricing in the target market (published and discovered)
- Conduct willingness-to-pay interviews with 15–20 target customers
- Understand local purchasing patterns: annual vs. monthly, currency, payment methods
- Analyze economic factors: GDP per capita, software spending norms, purchasing power
- Identify price-sensitive vs. value-sensitive segments
- Study regulatory considerations: tax, compliance, data residency that affect pricing
- Benchmark against local and global competitors
- Understand channel dynamics: direct, partner, reseller margins
**Human Checkpoint:** Leadership reviews market pricing research
**Output:** Market pricing research document with competitive analysis and WTP data
**Duration:** 2–3 weeks

### Phase 2: Pricing Model Design
**Objective:** Design pricing that fits the market while protecting global pricing integrity
**Skills/Agents Used:** `strategy-advisor`, `data-storyteller`
**Activities:**
- Design 2–3 pricing model options for the new market
- Consider: geographic pricing, segment pricing, or separate product tier
- Model revenue scenarios for each option
- Assess impact on existing pricing: arbitrage risk, customer perception, channel conflict
- Design packaging: which features in which tiers for this market
- Define the pricing metric: per-seat, usage-based, flat rate, or hybrid
- Model unit economics: CAC, LTV, and payback for the new market
- Design discounting framework and approval levels
**Human Checkpoint:** Finance and product leadership review pricing models
**Output:** Recommended pricing model with revenue projections
**Duration:** 1–2 weeks

### Phase 3: Packaging
**Objective:** Create the packaging structure that resonates with the target market
**Skills/Agents Used:** `strategy-advisor`, `comms-chameleon`
**Activities:**
- Define tier structure (e.g., Starter, Pro, Enterprise) for the new market
- Allocate features across tiers based on value perception in this market
- Create add-on or module structure for optional capabilities
- Design trial or freemium offering if appropriate for this market
- Build pricing page mockup and messaging
- Create comparison table showing value at each tier
- Prepare sales pricing tools: calculator, quoting process
- Document pricing rules: discounting limits, bundling options, negotiation playbook
**Human Checkpoint:** Sales and marketing review packaging and messaging
**Output:** Complete packaging structure with pricing page and sales tools
**Duration:** 1–2 weeks

### Phase 4: Market Test
**Objective:** Validate pricing with real customers before broad launch
**Skills/Agents Used:** `research-analyst`, `data-storyteller`
**Activities:**
- Test pricing with 5–10 early customers in the new market
- Run A/B tests on pricing page if self-serve model
- Negotiate pilot deals at proposed pricing to gauge reactions
- Collect feedback on pricing clarity, perceived value, and objections
- Compare conversion rates against benchmark
- Adjust pricing or packaging based on test results
- Validate billing and payment infrastructure for the new market
- Confirm tax and compliance handling for the market
**Human Checkpoint:** Review test results and approve final pricing
**Output:** Validated pricing with test results and adjustments
**Duration:** 2–4 weeks

### Phase 5: Launch & Monitor
**Objective:** Roll out new market pricing and optimize based on performance
**Skills/Agents Used:** `launch-coordinator`, `data-storyteller`
**Activities:**
- Update product: pricing page, checkout, billing system for the new market
- Enable sales team with new pricing materials and training
- Launch pricing to the market with supporting communications
- Monitor key metrics: conversion, ARPU, churn, deal velocity
- Track competitive reactions to your market entry pricing
- Collect ongoing customer feedback on pricing perception
- Optimize pricing quarterly based on market performance
- Review for pricing consistency across markets
**Human Checkpoint:** 90-day pricing performance review
**Output:** New market pricing live and performing against targets
**Duration:** 3–6 months (ongoing monitoring)

## Success Criteria
- Conversion rate in new market meets or exceeds target
- ARPU is sustainable and unit economics work
- No significant cannibalization of existing market pricing
- Sales team is confident and effective with new pricing
- Customer perception of value-for-price is positive

## Common Pitfalls
- **Copy-paste pricing:** What works in one market rarely translates directly
- **Race to the bottom:** Low pricing can trap you; price to value, not just to compete
- **Ignoring arbitrage:** If markets can access different pricing, some will game it
- **Over-discounting to enter:** Establishing a discount expectation is hard to undo
- **Pricing too complex:** If customers can't understand pricing, they won't buy

## Adaptation Guide
- **Geographic expansion:** Focus on purchasing power parity, currency, and local payment methods
- **Downmarket expansion:** Simpler packaging, lower touch, self-serve billing
- **Upmarket expansion:** Value-based pricing, custom packaging, negotiated contracts
- **Adjacent product line:** Consider bundling with existing product vs. standalone pricing
