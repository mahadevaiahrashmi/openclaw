# New PM Onboarding

## Overview
Structured 90-day onboarding playbook for new product managers joining the team. Covers context setup, product ramp, stakeholder relationships, and first deliverables. Use whenever a new PM joins—whether junior or senior.

## Prerequisites
- PM's product area and team assignment confirmed
- Manager has prepared initial context package
- IT access provisioned (tools, repos, dashboards, Slack channels)
- Buddy or onboarding partner assigned
- 30/60/90 day expectations discussed with hiring manager

## Phases

### Phase 1: Context Setup (Days 1–14)
**Objective:** Get the new PM oriented with company, product, and tools
**Skills/Agents Used:** `strategy-advisor`, `research-analyst`
**Activities:**
- Complete HR onboarding and IT setup
- Review company strategy, mission, values, and OKRs
- Read product strategy doc, vision doc, and current roadmap
- Set up all product tools (analytics, project tracker, design tools, CRM)
- Review the Pro PM: playbooks, templates, skills, and workflows
- Study the product: use it daily as a customer would
- Read last 3 months of customer feedback, NPS comments, and support themes
- Review competitive landscape and positioning
- Shadow 2–3 customer calls or support sessions
**Human Checkpoint:** Manager check-in at end of week 2—confirm context is solid
**Output:** New PM has working knowledge of product, market, and tools
**Duration:** 2 weeks

### Phase 2: Product Deep Dive (Days 15–30)
**Objective:** Develop deep product expertise and technical understanding
**Skills/Agents Used:** `codebase-navigator`, `research-analyst`
**Activities:**
- Walk through product architecture with engineering lead
- Review data model, key APIs, and system dependencies
- Analyze product metrics: funnel, retention, feature usage
- Study top 10 customer accounts and their use cases
- Review recent PRDs and shipped features
- Understand technical debt and known limitations
- Attend sprint ceremonies and observe team dynamics
- Complete 5–10 customer interviews focused on listening
**Human Checkpoint:** Manager check-in—PM presents product understanding
**Output:** PM can articulate product value prop, technical constraints, and user needs
**Duration:** 2 weeks

### Phase 3: Stakeholder Integration (Days 15–45)
**Objective:** Build relationships with key cross-functional partners
**Skills/Agents Used:** `document-builder`
**Activities:**
- Complete stakeholder mapping using stakeholder-onboarding playbook
- Schedule 1:1s with all key stakeholders: engineering leads, design, sales, marketing, CS, support
- Understand each stakeholder's goals, frustrations, and working style
- Attend key recurring meetings: team standup, design review, sales pipeline
- Build relationship with executive sponsor
- Identify informal influencers and decision-makers
- Document communication preferences for each stakeholder
**Human Checkpoint:** Manager reviews stakeholder relationship progress
**Output:** Stakeholder map complete; relationships initiated with all key partners
**Duration:** 3 weeks (overlaps with Phase 2)

### Phase 4: First 90-Day Delivery (Days 30–90)
**Objective:** Deliver tangible value and establish credibility
**Skills/Agents Used:** `spec-writer`, `quarterly-planner`, `data-storyteller`
**Activities:**
- Identify a quick win: small improvement that demonstrates impact
- Write first PRD (with manager review)
- Lead first sprint planning session
- Deliver first feature or improvement to production
- Set up personal metrics dashboard for your product area
- Present first quarterly roadmap proposal (even if for feedback only)
- Write and deliver first exec update
- Conduct first user research study independently
- Document 3 strategic observations about the product
**Human Checkpoint:** 90-day review with manager—formal feedback and goal-setting
**Output:** First deliverable shipped; PM operating independently on core PM activities
**Duration:** 8 weeks

## Success Criteria
- PM is independently running their product area by day 90
- Key stakeholder relationships established and productive
- At least one feature or improvement shipped
- PM can articulate product strategy, competitive position, and key metrics
- Team and cross-functional partners provide positive feedback
- PM has a perspective on strategic priorities (even if still developing)

## Common Pitfalls
- **Drinking from the firehose:** Pace the context dump; prioritize learning over reading everything
- **Staying in learning mode too long:** Start contributing by week 3, even in small ways
- **Neglecting relationships:** Technical understanding without stakeholder trust limits impact
- **Copying the predecessor's approach:** Bring your own perspective; that's why you were hired
- **Skipping the product:** Use your own product daily; nothing replaces firsthand experience

## Adaptation Guide
- **Senior PM/Director:** Compress Phase 1 to 1 week; add strategic assessment in Phase 4
- **Junior/Associate PM:** Extend Phase 2 and add more structured mentorship
- **Remote PM:** Over-invest in stakeholder meetings; schedule informal virtual coffees
- **Platform PM:** Add developer experience ramp—read API docs, try the SDK, review DX metrics
