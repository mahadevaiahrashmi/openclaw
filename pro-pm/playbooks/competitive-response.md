# Competitive Response

## Overview
Playbook for responding to competitive moves—new product launches, pricing changes, acquisitions, or messaging shifts. Use when a competitor action requires a coordinated product or GTM response.

## Prerequisites
- Competitive intelligence monitoring active (alerts, analyst reports, social listening)
- Competitive landscape baseline documented
- Sales team channels open for field intelligence
- GTM and product leadership available for decision-making

## Phases

### Phase 1: Detect
**Objective:** Identify and validate the competitive signal quickly
**Skills/Agents Used:** `competitive-intel`, `research-analyst`
**Activities:**
- Capture the signal: announcement, product launch, pricing change, acquisition
- Verify through multiple sources (press, product testing, customer feedback, analyst reports)
- Assess signal strength: rumor, confirmed, or already impacting deals
- Determine urgency: immediate threat, near-term, or long-term shift
- Notify relevant stakeholders (product, sales, marketing, exec)
**Human Checkpoint:** Confirm the signal is real and warrants a response
**Output:** Competitive signal brief with verified facts and initial urgency assessment
**Duration:** 1–3 days

### Phase 2: Analyze
**Objective:** Deeply understand the competitive move and its implications
**Skills/Agents Used:** `competitive-intel`, `strategy-advisor`
**Activities:**
- Hands-on evaluation: sign up, use the product, read docs
- Feature-by-feature comparison on affected areas
- Analyze pricing and packaging changes
- Assess impact on active deals and pipeline (input from sales)
- Identify segments most at risk (customer, vertical, use case)
- Evaluate competitor's strategic intent—one-time or sustained investment
**Human Checkpoint:** Leadership reviews competitive analysis
**Output:** Competitive analysis document with impact assessment
**Duration:** 3–7 days

### Phase 3: Options Development
**Objective:** Generate response options spanning product, GTM, and messaging
**Skills/Agents Used:** `strategy-advisor`, `comms-chameleon`
**Activities:**
- Generate response options across dimensions: product, pricing, messaging, enablement
- For each option, assess effort, timeline, and expected impact
- Consider defensive (protect current base) vs. offensive (attack their weakness) approaches
- Identify quick wins vs. strategic investments
- Evaluate do-nothing scenario and its cost
- Prepare recommendation with pros/cons for each option
**Human Checkpoint:** Cross-functional review of response options
**Output:** Options matrix with recommendations
**Duration:** 3–5 days

### Phase 4: Decide
**Objective:** Select the response strategy and secure commitment
**Skills/Agents Used:** `document-builder`
**Activities:**
- Present options to executive team
- Select response strategy with clear rationale
- Allocate resources and assign owners
- Set timeline and milestones
- Define success metrics for the response
- Communicate decision and rationale broadly
**Human Checkpoint:** Executive sign-off on response strategy
**Output:** Approved response plan with owners, timeline, and metrics
**Duration:** 1–3 days

### Phase 5: Execute
**Objective:** Implement the response and measure effectiveness
**Skills/Agents Used:** `launch-coordinator`, `comms-chameleon`, `data-storyteller`
**Activities:**
- Execute product changes (feature acceleration, packaging updates)
- Update sales battlecards and competitive positioning
- Brief sales team on new talk tracks and objection handling
- Update marketing materials and website positioning
- Monitor win/loss rates in affected segments
- Track competitive mentions in customer conversations
- Iterate based on field feedback
**Human Checkpoint:** 30-day review of response effectiveness
**Output:** Response executed with effectiveness metrics tracked
**Duration:** 2–8 weeks (ongoing monitoring)

## Success Criteria
- Win rate in affected segments stabilizes or improves within 60 days
- Sales team is confident handling competitive objections
- Customers in at-risk segments feel heard and supported
- Response delivered within planned timeline and budget
- Learnings captured for future competitive intelligence

## Common Pitfalls
- **Knee-jerk reaction:** Not every competitive move warrants a response; analyze first
- **Feature-matching trap:** Copying competitors leads to undifferentiated products
- **Ignoring the field:** Sales sees competitive dynamics before anyone else
- **One-and-done:** Competitive response requires sustained monitoring, not a single action
- **Over-rotating:** Spending too much time on competitors distracts from customer value

## Adaptation Guide
- **Market leader:** Focus on differentiation and customer lock-in; respond selectively
- **Challenger/underdog:** Use competitive moves as opportunities to differentiate on speed and innovation
- **Platform product:** Assess ecosystem impact; rally partners if needed
- **Regulated market:** Verify compliance implications of competitive moves before responding
