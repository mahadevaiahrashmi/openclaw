# PM Team Scaling

## Overview
Playbook for scaling a product management team from assessment through hiring, onboarding, development, and organizational design. Use when growing the PM function to match company growth.

## Prerequisites
- Company growth trajectory requires additional PM capacity
- Budget approved for PM hiring
- Current PM team structure and coverage documented
- Hiring manager and recruiting partner identified

## Phases

### Phase 1: Assess
**Objective:** Evaluate current PM capacity and define future-state needs
**Skills/Agents Used:** `strategy-advisor`
**Activities:**
- Audit current PM team: coverage, seniority mix, skill distribution
- Map product areas to PM coverage—identify gaps and overloaded PMs
- Assess PM-to-engineer ratio and compare to industry benchmarks
- Identify capability gaps: technical PM, growth PM, platform PM, etc.
- Forecast needs based on company roadmap and growth plans
- Define the PM organizational model: feature teams, platform teams, or hybrid
- Assess whether to hire, promote internally, or restructure
- Create a phased hiring plan with priority ranking
**Human Checkpoint:** CPO/VP Product approves team assessment and hiring plan
**Output:** PM team assessment with hiring plan and organizational design
**Duration:** 1–2 weeks

### Phase 2: Hire
**Objective:** Recruit and select excellent PMs efficiently
**Skills/Agents Used:** `document-builder`
**Activities:**
- Write compelling job descriptions tailored to each role
- Define the PM leveling framework (if not existing)
- Design the interview process: resume screen, phone screen, case study, cross-functional panel
- Create standardized evaluation rubrics for consistency
- Source candidates through multiple channels: recruiting, referrals, communities
- Build a diverse candidate pipeline
- Run interview loops with trained interviewers
- Make competitive offers with clear leveling and growth path
- Maintain candidate experience throughout the process
**Human Checkpoint:** Hiring committee makes final decisions on offers
**Output:** Offers accepted for planned PM hires
**Duration:** 4–12 weeks per hire

### Phase 3: Onboard
**Objective:** Ramp new PMs to full productivity (use new-pm-onboarding playbook for each)
**Skills/Agents Used:** `strategy-advisor`
**Activities:**
- Execute new-pm-onboarding playbook for each new hire
- Assign onboarding buddy or mentor from existing team
- Create 30/60/90 day plans tailored to each PM's product area
- Set up regular 1:1s with manager (weekly) and skip-level (monthly)
- Introduce to key stakeholders across the organization
- Provide Pro PM training: playbooks, templates, skills, and workflows
- Set clear first-quarter goals aligned with product priorities
- Gather onboarding feedback to improve the process
**Human Checkpoint:** 90-day review for each new PM
**Output:** New PMs onboarded and productive within their product areas
**Duration:** 90 days per PM

### Phase 4: Develop
**Objective:** Build PM team skills and career growth paths
**Skills/Agents Used:** `strategy-advisor`, `document-builder`
**Activities:**
- Establish PM career ladder with clear level expectations
- Create individual development plans for each PM
- Set up PM community of practice: regular PM team meetings, skill shares, book clubs
- Provide coaching and mentorship (manager, peer, external)
- Identify training needs: technical skills, communication, leadership, domain expertise
- Create opportunities for stretch assignments and rotation
- Establish PM craft standards: quality bar for PRDs, customer research, metrics
- Run regular calibration to ensure consistent leveling and feedback
**Human Checkpoint:** Bi-annual development plan review with each PM
**Output:** PM team with clear growth paths, skill development, and strong culture
**Duration:** Ongoing

### Phase 5: Scale the Function
**Objective:** Evolve the PM organization as the company grows
**Skills/Agents Used:** `strategy-advisor`
**Activities:**
- Evaluate organizational structure as team grows: functional, divisional, or matrix
- Introduce PM management layers when team exceeds 6–8 ICs
- Define and evolve PM operating model: how PMs work with each other and cross-functional teams
- Build PM tools and infrastructure: shared templates, research repositories, metrics dashboards
- Create PM hiring pipeline for ongoing growth
- Establish PM branding: conference talks, blog posts, recruiting content
- Benchmark PM practices against industry peers
- Plan for specialization: growth PM, platform PM, technical PM, data PM
**Human Checkpoint:** Annual PM organization review with CPO
**Output:** Scalable PM organization that grows with the company
**Duration:** Ongoing

## Success Criteria
- All product areas have appropriate PM coverage
- New PMs reach full productivity within 90 days
- PM team retention above company average
- Cross-functional partners rate PM collaboration highly
- PM career paths are clear and promotions happen on schedule
- PM team ships high-quality work consistently

## Common Pitfalls
- **Hiring too fast:** Bad PM hires are extremely costly; maintain quality bar
- **No career ladder:** PMs leave when they can't see a growth path
- **Cloning yourself:** Hire for diverse strengths, not copies of the current team
- **Neglecting onboarding:** A great hire with bad onboarding is a wasted investment
- **PM silos:** Build community and shared practices; isolated PMs develop bad habits

## Adaptation Guide
- **First PM hire:** Focus on generalist who can build the function from scratch
- **Series B/C scale-up:** Build the leveling framework; hire mix of senior and junior
- **Enterprise PM team:** Add domain-specific hiring criteria and longer ramp time
- **Remote/distributed team:** Over-invest in async communication, documentation, and community
