# Enterprise Deal Support

## Overview
Playbook for PM involvement in large enterprise deals—from discovery through close. Defines when PM should engage, what value PM brings, and how to support sales without derailing product priorities.

## Prerequisites
- Deal identified as requiring PM involvement (typically $100K+ ARR, strategic account, or custom requirements)
- Sales rep has qualified the opportunity and requested product support
- Account details and context available in CRM
- PM has bandwidth allocated for deal support (part of capacity planning)

## Phases

### Phase 1: Discovery
**Objective:** Understand the prospect's needs, environment, and decision criteria
**Skills/Agents Used:** `research-analyst`, `codebase-navigator`
**Activities:**
- Review account intel: company size, industry, tech stack, existing solutions
- Join discovery call with sales to understand prospect's pain points
- Ask deep product questions: workflows, scale, integrations, compliance needs
- Identify the economic buyer, technical evaluator, and champion
- Understand their evaluation criteria and competitive alternatives
- Map their requirements to current product capabilities
- Identify gaps between requirements and current product
- Assess strategic fit: does this customer represent a segment we want to win
**Human Checkpoint:** PM and sales align on whether to pursue and at what investment level
**Output:** Discovery summary with requirements mapping and gap analysis
**Duration:** 1–2 days

### Phase 2: Qualification
**Objective:** Determine if the deal is worth significant product investment
**Skills/Agents Used:** `strategy-advisor`
**Activities:**
- Score the opportunity: deal size, strategic value, and segment fit
- Assess gap closure: can we close gaps with roadmap items, configuration, or workarounds
- Evaluate custom work: is any bespoke development required and is it justifiable
- Check alignment with product strategy: does this deal pull us toward or away from our vision
- Estimate PM and engineering time investment needed to support the deal
- Determine if commitments would create precedent we don't want
- Make a recommend: full support, limited support, or decline product involvement
**Human Checkpoint:** Product leadership approves PM engagement level
**Output:** Deal qualification assessment with recommended engagement level
**Duration:** 1–2 days

### Phase 3: Solution Design
**Objective:** Design how the product meets the prospect's needs
**Skills/Agents Used:** `spec-writer`, `codebase-navigator`
**Activities:**
- Build a solution architecture showing how the product fits their environment
- Design integration approach for their tech stack
- Prepare demo environment tailored to their use case
- Document any configuration, customization, or workarounds needed
- Create proof-of-concept plan if a POC is required
- Write a technical brief or solution brief for the prospect
- Prepare answers for security questionnaire (coordinate with InfoSec)
- Address compliance requirements (SOC2, HIPAA, GDPR, etc.)
**Human Checkpoint:** Technical review of solution design with engineering
**Output:** Solution design document, tailored demo, and POC plan (if applicable)
**Duration:** 1–2 weeks

### Phase 4: Proposal & Negotiation
**Objective:** Support sales through proposal and negotiation with product expertise
**Skills/Agents Used:** `document-builder`, `comms-chameleon`
**Activities:**
- Review proposal for accuracy of product capabilities and timelines
- Help sales articulate product roadmap commitments (only what's committed)
- Participate in executive-level product discussions
- Handle technical objections and competitive comparisons
- Support POC execution if running one
- Negotiate product requirements: distinguish must-haves from nice-to-haves
- Manage expectations on custom development timelines
- Provide product references and case studies from similar customers
**Human Checkpoint:** Product leadership approves any roadmap commitments in the proposal
**Output:** Product-validated proposal with clear commitments and caveats
**Duration:** 1–4 weeks

### Phase 5: Close & Transition
**Objective:** Ensure smooth transition from sales process to customer success
**Skills/Agents Used:** `launch-coordinator`, `research-analyst`
**Activities:**
- Celebrate the win and document what influenced the decision
- Document all commitments made during the sales process
- Create implementation plan with CS and professional services
- Add committed features to the roadmap with appropriate priority
- Brief the engineering team on customer-specific requirements
- Set up customer feedback channel for the new account
- Schedule 30/60/90 day check-ins with the customer
- Conduct win analysis: what worked, what we'd do differently
- Update competitive intelligence based on the deal
**Human Checkpoint:** CS confirms implementation plan is solid and resourced
**Output:** Signed deal with clear commitments, implementation plan, and win analysis
**Duration:** 1–2 weeks

## Success Criteria
- Deal closes at expected or better terms
- No product commitments made that conflict with strategy
- Customer implementation succeeds within planned timeline
- Win/loss insights captured and shared
- PM time investment was proportional to deal value

## Common Pitfalls
- **Becoming a sales engineer:** PM adds strategic value; delegate demos to SE where possible
- **Roadmap hostage-taking:** Never commit unplanned features to close a deal without product leadership approval
- **Ignoring smaller deals:** Set clear criteria for PM involvement; scale support through materials
- **Custom development trap:** One-off features for one customer create maintenance burden
- **Forgetting the handoff:** Commitments made in sales must transfer to delivery teams

## Adaptation Guide
- **SMB/mid-market:** Lighter PM touch; create self-serve materials instead of 1:1 support
- **Platform/API deals:** Add technical integration support; involve developer relations
- **Competitive displacement:** Add migration planning and competitive tear-down
- **Multi-product deal:** Coordinate PMs across products; unified solution narrative
