# Pricing & Packaging Change

## Overview
End-to-end playbook for evaluating, designing, aligning on, rolling out, and monitoring changes to product pricing or packaging. Use for any pricing change—from minor tier adjustments to full model overhauls.

## Prerequisites
- Signal or hypothesis that current pricing/packaging needs change
- Access to revenue data, customer segmentation, and usage analytics
- Finance, sales, and legal teams available for collaboration
- Executive sponsor for the pricing initiative

## Phases

### Phase 1: Signal Detection
**Objective:** Identify and validate the pricing signal
**Skills/Agents Used:** `competitive-intel`, `research-analyst`, `data-storyteller`
**Activities:**
- Document the signal: competitive pressure, sales feedback, low conversion, churn at renewal, margin compression, or new market opportunity
- Quantify the problem: what metrics are underperforming and by how much
- Segment analysis: which customers are most affected
- Benchmark against competitors and market standards
- Gather sales field intelligence on pricing objections and lost deals
- Assess urgency: immediate fix needed or strategic evolution
**Human Checkpoint:** Leadership agrees pricing change is worth pursuing
**Output:** Pricing signal brief with data and initial hypothesis
**Duration:** 1–2 weeks

### Phase 2: Analysis & Research
**Objective:** Deep-dive into pricing dynamics and customer willingness to pay
**Skills/Agents Used:** `competitive-intel`, `research-analyst`
**Activities:**
- Conduct willingness-to-pay research (Van Westendorp, conjoint, or interview-based)
- Analyze usage patterns to identify natural value thresholds
- Map customer segments to value perception
- Study competitive pricing in detail (feature comparison at each tier)
- Analyze revenue impact scenarios: by segment, cohort, and deal size
- Review historical pricing changes and their impact
- Identify packaging gaps: features in wrong tier, missing tiers, or confusing bundles
**Human Checkpoint:** Review analysis findings with finance and sales leadership
**Output:** Pricing research document with willingness-to-pay data and segmentation
**Duration:** 2–4 weeks

### Phase 3: Model Design
**Objective:** Design the new pricing and packaging structure
**Skills/Agents Used:** `strategy-advisor`, `data-storyteller`
**Activities:**
- Design 2–3 pricing model options (e.g., per-seat, usage-based, hybrid)
- Define tier structure, feature allocation, and price points for each option
- Model revenue impact for each option across customer segments
- Stress-test edge cases: high-volume users, small teams, enterprise deals
- Calculate transition impact for existing customers (winners and losers)
- Design grandfathering or migration strategy for existing customers
- Build pricing calculator or tool for sales team
**Human Checkpoint:** Finance and executive review of pricing models
**Output:** Recommended pricing model with revenue projections and migration plan
**Duration:** 1–2 weeks

### Phase 4: Alignment
**Objective:** Get cross-functional buy-in on the chosen pricing approach
**Skills/Agents Used:** `document-builder`, `comms-chameleon`
**Activities:**
- Present pricing recommendation to executive team
- Review with sales leadership: will this help or hurt in the field
- Review with CS: how will existing customers react
- Legal review: contractual implications, notice requirements
- Finance sign-off on revenue projections and accounting treatment
- Prepare board communication if pricing change is material
- Document approved pricing with effective dates
**Human Checkpoint:** CEO/CFO approval of pricing change
**Output:** Approved pricing structure with cross-functional alignment
**Duration:** 1–2 weeks

### Phase 5: Rollout
**Objective:** Execute the pricing change with proper enablement and communication
**Skills/Agents Used:** `launch-coordinator`, `comms-chameleon`
**Activities:**
- Update product: pricing page, checkout flows, billing system, API
- Prepare sales enablement: new pricing deck, calculator, objection handling, FAQ
- Draft customer communications based on impact tier
- Brief support and CS teams on handling pricing questions
- Communicate to existing customers per the migration strategy
- Execute phased rollout: new customers first, then renewals, then active contracts
- Prepare press response if pricing change is public
**Human Checkpoint:** Final review of customer communications before sending
**Output:** New pricing live with all stakeholders enabled
**Duration:** 2–4 weeks

### Phase 6: Monitor & Optimize
**Objective:** Track pricing change effectiveness and iterate
**Skills/Agents Used:** `data-storyteller`, `research-analyst`
**Activities:**
- Monitor key metrics daily for first month: conversion rate, ARPU, churn, deal velocity
- Track customer sentiment via NPS, CSAT, and direct feedback
- Monitor support ticket volume related to pricing
- Track sales deal metrics: win rate, deal size, sales cycle length
- Compare actual revenue against projections
- Identify segments where pricing isn't working and adjust
- Run A/B tests on pricing page if applicable
- Quarterly review to assess whether pricing change achieved goals
**Human Checkpoint:** 90-day pricing review with leadership
**Output:** Pricing effectiveness report with optimization recommendations
**Duration:** 3–6 months (ongoing)

## Success Criteria
- Revenue per user or ARPU improves within 2 quarters
- Conversion rate maintains or improves for target segments
- Churn does not increase beyond acceptable threshold
- Sales team prefers new pricing in the field
- Customer NPS remains stable through the transition

## Common Pitfalls
- **Pricing in a vacuum:** Involve sales, CS, and finance from day one
- **Ignoring existing customers:** Migration strategy is as important as the new pricing
- **Too complex:** If you can't explain pricing in 30 seconds, simplify
- **Racing to the bottom:** Competing on price alone erodes value perception
- **One-size-fits-all:** Different segments may need different packaging

## Adaptation Guide
- **PLG/self-serve:** Focus on transparent, published pricing; test via pricing page experiments
- **Enterprise/sales-led:** Focus on deal flexibility and value-based pricing; published pricing less critical
- **Usage-based model:** Add spend alerts, cost calculators, and predictability tools for customers
- **Freemium to paid:** Use new-market-pricing playbook instead
