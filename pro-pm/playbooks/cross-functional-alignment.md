# Cross-Functional Alignment

## Overview
Playbook for identifying, resolving, and sustaining alignment across cross-functional teams. Use when teams are working at cross-purposes, handoffs are failing, or strategic initiatives require tight coordination across functions.

## Prerequisites
- Misalignment symptoms identified (duplicated work, conflicting priorities, slow handoffs)
- Stakeholders from affected functions willing to participate
- Executive sponsor to support alignment decisions
- Organizational chart and team charters available

## Phases

### Phase 1: Identify Gaps
**Objective:** Diagnose where and why misalignment exists
**Skills/Agents Used:** `strategy-advisor`, `research-analyst`
**Activities:**
- Survey or interview each function: what are your goals, blockers, and frustrations
- Map each team's current priorities and OKRs
- Identify overlaps, conflicts, and gaps between team goals
- Document handoff points that are failing (e.g., PM→Design, Sales→Product feedback)
- Assess communication patterns: who talks to whom, how often, and about what
- Quantify the cost of misalignment: delayed launches, rework, customer impact
**Human Checkpoint:** Stakeholders validate the gap analysis
**Output:** Alignment gap analysis with impact assessment
**Duration:** 1–2 weeks

### Phase 2: Map Dependencies
**Objective:** Create a clear picture of how teams depend on each other
**Skills/Agents Used:** `strategy-advisor`
**Activities:**
- Map team dependencies for current quarter's key initiatives
- Identify RACI for shared processes (who is Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed)
- Document decision rights: who decides what, and when
- Identify shared metrics that multiple teams influence
- Map information flows: where does context get lost between teams
- Highlight critical path dependencies that could block delivery
**Human Checkpoint:** All function leads validate the dependency map
**Output:** Cross-functional dependency map with RACI and decision rights
**Duration:** 1 week

### Phase 3: Alignment Plan
**Objective:** Design the operating model changes needed to fix alignment
**Skills/Agents Used:** `strategy-advisor`, `document-builder`
**Activities:**
- Propose changes to meeting cadences, communication channels, or reporting structure
- Define shared goals or OKRs that span functions
- Design improved handoff processes with clear inputs, outputs, and SLAs
- Propose escalation paths for cross-functional disagreements
- Define shared rituals: joint planning, cross-team demos, retrospectives
- Identify quick wins that can be implemented immediately
- Get buy-in from each function lead on proposed changes
**Human Checkpoint:** Executive sponsor approves alignment plan
**Output:** Alignment action plan with owners, timelines, and success metrics
**Duration:** 1 week

### Phase 4: Execute
**Objective:** Implement the alignment improvements
**Skills/Agents Used:** `quarterly-planner`, `comms-chameleon`
**Activities:**
- Implement quick wins first to build momentum
- Set up new meeting cadences and communication channels
- Roll out updated RACI and decision-rights documentation
- Train teams on new handoff processes
- Create shared dashboards for cross-functional metrics
- Assign alignment champions in each function
- Run first cycle of new rituals (joint planning, cross-team retro)
- Address resistance or confusion promptly
**Human Checkpoint:** 30-day check-in—are new processes being followed
**Output:** New operating model in place with teams actively using it
**Duration:** 2–4 weeks

### Phase 5: Sustain
**Objective:** Maintain alignment as teams evolve and new challenges emerge
**Skills/Agents Used:** `data-storyteller`, `strategy-advisor`
**Activities:**
- Monitor alignment health quarterly: survey or interview team leads
- Track shared metrics to ensure cross-functional outcomes are improving
- Run quarterly cross-functional retrospectives
- Adjust processes based on feedback—what's working, what's bureaucratic
- Celebrate cross-functional wins to reinforce collaboration culture
- Onboard new team members into cross-functional processes
- Evolve the model as the organization grows or reorganizes
**Human Checkpoint:** Quarterly alignment review with executive sponsor
**Output:** Sustained alignment with continuous improvement cadence
**Duration:** Ongoing

## Success Criteria
- Handoff failure rate decreases measurably
- Time-to-launch improves for cross-functional initiatives
- Teams report higher satisfaction with collaboration
- Shared metrics trend positively
- Escalations decrease as decision rights are clear

## Common Pitfalls
- **Process for process's sake:** Only add structure that solves real problems
- **Ignoring culture:** Process changes won't stick without cultural reinforcement
- **Top-down only:** Alignment requires buy-in at the working level, not just executive mandates
- **One-time effort:** Alignment is ongoing; it degrades without active maintenance
- **Too many meetings:** Solve alignment through better async communication, not more meetings

## Adaptation Guide
- **Small company (<50 people):** Lightweight; shared Slack channels and weekly syncs may suffice
- **Large org (500+ people):** Formal RACI, shared OKRs, and dedicated alignment roles
- **Remote/distributed:** Over-invest in async documentation and recorded cross-team demos
- **Post-reorg:** Run this playbook immediately; alignment breaks during every reorg
