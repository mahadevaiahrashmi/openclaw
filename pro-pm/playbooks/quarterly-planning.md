# Quarterly Planning

## Overview
Structured process for reviewing the prior quarter, setting OKRs, building the roadmap, aligning stakeholders, and committing to deliverables. Run this 2–4 weeks before each quarter begins.

## Prerequisites
- Prior quarter metrics and OKR results available
- Company-level strategy and annual goals defined
- Engineering capacity estimates for upcoming quarter
- Customer feedback and competitive intelligence current
- Leadership available for alignment sessions

## Phases

### Phase 1: Review
**Objective:** Assess prior quarter performance and extract learnings
**Skills/Agents Used:** `data-storyteller`, `comms-chameleon`
**Activities:**
- Pull final OKR scores and document outcomes vs. targets
- Review shipped features against planned roadmap
- Analyze key metrics: adoption, retention, revenue, NPS
- Gather retrospective input from cross-functional teams
- Identify what worked, what didn't, and systemic issues
- Document carry-over items and unfinished work
**Human Checkpoint:** Leadership review of prior quarter results
**Output:** Quarter-in-review document with metrics, learnings, and carry-overs
**Duration:** 3–5 days

### Phase 2: OKR Setting
**Objective:** Define measurable objectives that ladder to company strategy
**Skills/Agents Used:** `strategy-advisor`, `data-storyteller`
**Activities:**
- Review company-level OKRs and strategic priorities
- Draft 2–4 product objectives with 3–5 key results each
- Validate key results are measurable with existing instrumentation
- Pressure-test ambition level (70% confidence = good stretch)
- Align product OKRs with engineering, design, and GTM OKRs
- Get input from direct reports on team-level OKRs
**Human Checkpoint:** OKR approval from leadership
**Output:** Finalized product OKRs with ownership and measurement plan
**Duration:** 3–5 days

### Phase 3: Roadmap Construction
**Objective:** Translate OKRs into a sequenced plan of work
**Skills/Agents Used:** `quarterly-planner`, `spec-writer`
**Activities:**
- Map initiatives to OKRs—every roadmap item should connect to a key result
- Size initiatives with engineering (story points or time estimates)
- Prioritize using impact vs. effort framework
- Identify dependencies across teams and flag risks
- Allocate capacity: ~70% planned, ~20% support/maintenance, ~10% exploration
- Sequence initiatives across the quarter with milestones
**Human Checkpoint:** Roadmap review with engineering and design leads
**Output:** Quarterly roadmap with initiatives, owners, milestones, and dependencies
**Duration:** 3–5 days

### Phase 4: Stakeholder Alignment
**Objective:** Ensure all stakeholders understand and support the plan
**Skills/Agents Used:** `comms-chameleon`, `document-builder`
**Activities:**
- Present roadmap to executive team for feedback
- Share with sales, marketing, CS, and support for input
- Address concerns and negotiate trade-offs
- Document decisions and rationale for non-obvious choices
- Communicate what's NOT on the roadmap and why
- Publish final roadmap to shared workspace
**Human Checkpoint:** Executive sign-off on quarterly plan
**Output:** Aligned, published roadmap with stakeholder buy-in documented
**Duration:** 3–5 days

### Phase 5: Commit & Kickoff
**Objective:** Lock the plan and energize the team for execution
**Skills/Agents Used:** `quarterly-planner`, `comms-chameleon`
**Activities:**
- Create epics and milestones in project tracker
- Assign owners to every initiative and key result
- Set up tracking dashboards and reporting cadence
- Schedule recurring check-ins (weekly status, mid-quarter review)
- Run quarter kickoff with the team—share vision, goals, and roadmap
- Establish escalation paths for blockers
**Human Checkpoint:** Team confirms capacity and commitments
**Output:** Project tracker populated, dashboards live, team aligned and executing
**Duration:** 2–3 days

## Success Criteria
- OKRs directly ladder to company strategy
- Roadmap is achievable within estimated capacity (no >100% allocation)
- All stakeholders have reviewed and provided input
- Team understands priorities and trade-offs
- Tracking mechanisms are in place before quarter starts

## Common Pitfalls
- **Planning too much:** Leave buffer for unplanned work and exploration
- **Vanity OKRs:** Key results must be measurable and meaningful, not easy wins
- **Ignoring carry-overs:** Unfinished work from last quarter still costs capacity
- **Siloed planning:** Cross-functional input prevents misalignment mid-quarter
- **No mid-quarter check:** Schedule a mid-quarter review to course-correct

## Adaptation Guide
- **Startup (<20 people):** Compress to a 2-day planning sprint; 2–3 OKRs max
- **Large org:** Add a pre-planning phase for bottom-up input collection
- **Platform team:** Include API/SDK roadmap and developer experience goals
- **Regulated industry:** Add compliance review phase before commit
