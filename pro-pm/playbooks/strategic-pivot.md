# Strategic Pivot

## Overview
Playbook for evaluating signals that suggest a strategic change is needed, analyzing options, making the decision, and executing the transition. Use when market shifts, performance data, or new opportunities suggest the current strategy may no longer be optimal.

## Prerequisites
- Persistent signals indicating current strategy is underperforming or at risk
- Executive leadership open to evaluating strategic alternatives
- Access to market data, financials, and customer insights
- Board or investor awareness (if applicable)

## Phases

### Phase 1: Signal Recognition
**Objective:** Identify and validate signals that warrant strategic reconsideration
**Skills/Agents Used:** `competitive-intel`, `data-storyteller`, `research-analyst`
**Activities:**
- Document the signals: declining metrics, market shifts, competitive disruption, customer churn patterns
- Separate noise from signal—validate with multiple data sources
- Quantify the trend: how long, how severe, and is it accelerating
- Benchmark against market and peers
- Gather qualitative input from customers, sales, and partners
- Assess whether current strategy can course-correct without a pivot
**Human Checkpoint:** Leadership acknowledges the signals warrant investigation
**Output:** Signal assessment document with data, trends, and initial hypothesis
**Duration:** 1–2 weeks

### Phase 2: Deep Analysis
**Objective:** Understand root causes and the strategic landscape
**Skills/Agents Used:** `strategy-advisor`, `competitive-intel`
**Activities:**
- Root-cause analysis: why is current strategy underperforming
- Market analysis: what has changed and what's emerging
- Customer analysis: who are we losing and why; who are we winning and why
- Capability analysis: what assets, skills, and advantages do we have
- Financial analysis: runway, burn rate, and investment capacity for change
- Evaluate analogies: how have similar companies navigated similar situations
**Human Checkpoint:** Leadership review of analysis findings
**Output:** Strategic analysis document with root causes and landscape assessment
**Duration:** 2–3 weeks

### Phase 3: Options Development
**Objective:** Generate and evaluate strategic alternatives
**Skills/Agents Used:** `strategy-advisor`, `document-builder`
**Activities:**
- Generate 3–5 strategic options including "stay the course with adjustments"
- For each option: define the hypothesis, required investment, timeline, and expected outcome
- Model financial impact: revenue, costs, runway implications
- Assess organizational impact: team changes, skill gaps, culture shift
- Identify customer impact: who benefits, who is disrupted
- Evaluate reversibility: can we undo this if it doesn't work
- Rank options against criteria: market opportunity, execution feasibility, financial sustainability
**Human Checkpoint:** Leadership reviews and narrows to 2–3 options
**Output:** Options framework with financial models and recommendations
**Duration:** 1–2 weeks

### Phase 4: Decision
**Objective:** Make the pivot decision with clarity and commitment
**Skills/Agents Used:** `document-builder`, `comms-chameleon`
**Activities:**
- Present final options to board/executive team
- Facilitate structured decision-making (not consensus—clear decision-maker)
- Document the decision rationale and what would change the decision
- Define success metrics and failure criteria for the pivot
- Set check-in points for evaluation (30/60/90 days)
- Prepare transition communication plan
**Human Checkpoint:** CEO/board makes final pivot decision
**Output:** Pivot decision document with rationale, metrics, and communication plan
**Duration:** 1 week

### Phase 5: Transition Execution
**Objective:** Execute the pivot with speed while managing disruption
**Skills/Agents Used:** `launch-coordinator`, `comms-chameleon`, `quarterly-planner`
**Activities:**
- Communicate decision internally—be transparent about the why
- Restructure teams and priorities to align with new direction
- Wind down deprioritized initiatives gracefully
- Set up new OKRs and roadmap for the pivoted strategy
- Communicate to customers, investors, and partners as appropriate
- Reallocate resources and potentially adjust hiring plans
- Monitor team morale and address concerns
- Execute first milestone of the new strategy within 30 days
- Review progress at 30/60/90 days against pivot success criteria
**Human Checkpoint:** 90-day pivot review with leadership
**Output:** Organization executing on new strategy with early results measured
**Duration:** 3–6 months

## Success Criteria
- New strategy shows positive signal within 90 days
- Team is aligned and executing with conviction
- Key talent retained through the transition
- Customers (existing or new target) responding positively
- Financial trajectory improving or on-plan for the pivot model

## Common Pitfalls
- **Pivoting too late:** Don't wait for consensus; respond to data
- **Pivoting too often:** Multiple pivots in rapid succession destroys trust and momentum
- **Half-pivoting:** Commit fully; straddling old and new strategies wastes resources
- **Neglecting communication:** Teams need to understand the why or they won't execute with conviction
- **Ignoring culture:** A strategic pivot often requires a cultural shift too

## Adaptation Guide
- **Pre-PMF startup:** Pivots may happen every 3–6 months; compress all phases
- **Growth-stage company:** Pivots are more complex; invest more in change management
- **Public company:** Add investor relations and guidance impact analysis
- **Large enterprise:** Focus on business unit pivot; manage organizational change formally
