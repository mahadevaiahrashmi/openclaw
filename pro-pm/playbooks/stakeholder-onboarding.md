# Stakeholder Onboarding

## Overview
Playbook for systematically building relationships with key stakeholders—whether you're new to a role, starting a new initiative, or resetting relationships. Covers gathering context, building personas, planning engagement, initial meetings, and ongoing nurturing.

## Prerequisites
- Role or product area defined
- Organizational chart available
- Initial list of stakeholders identified by manager or predecessor
- Calendar time available for relationship building

## Phases

### Phase 1: Gather Context
**Objective:** Identify all relevant stakeholders and understand the landscape
**Skills/Agents Used:** `strategy-advisor`
**Activities:**
- List all potential stakeholders across functions: engineering, design, sales, marketing, CS, support, finance, legal, executive
- Research each stakeholder: role, tenure, goals, current projects
- Talk to your manager and peers about stakeholder dynamics and history
- Identify formal and informal power structures
- Understand past relationships between PM and each function
- Map decision-making processes that involve multiple stakeholders
- Identify any existing tensions or alignment issues
**Human Checkpoint:** Manager validates stakeholder list and provides context
**Output:** Comprehensive stakeholder list with initial context
**Duration:** 2–3 days

### Phase 2: Build Stakeholder Personas
**Objective:** Develop a working model for how to engage each stakeholder effectively
**Skills/Agents Used:** `strategy-advisor`
**Activities:**
- Classify stakeholders by influence and interest (2x2 matrix)
- For high-influence stakeholders, document: goals, success metrics, communication style, pet peeves, preferred meeting format
- Identify what each stakeholder needs from product: information, decisions, support, visibility
- Map stakeholders to product areas they care about most
- Identify potential allies, skeptics, and those who are neutral
- Determine appropriate engagement frequency for each stakeholder
- Note any political dynamics or sensitivities
**Human Checkpoint:** Review personas with a trusted peer for accuracy
**Output:** Stakeholder persona map with engagement strategy per person
**Duration:** 2–3 days

### Phase 3: Plan Engagement
**Objective:** Design a structured approach to initial stakeholder meetings
**Skills/Agents Used:** `comms-chameleon`
**Activities:**
- Prioritize stakeholders for initial meetings (highest influence first)
- Prepare for each meeting: research their recent work, prepare questions, define your ask
- Design a standard intro meeting agenda: listen first, then share context
- Prepare your elevator pitch: who you are, what you own, how you work
- Identify quick wins you can deliver for each stakeholder early on
- Plan meeting cadence: who needs weekly, biweekly, monthly, or ad-hoc
- Schedule initial meetings across a 2–3 week window
**Human Checkpoint:** Manager reviews engagement plan and prioritization
**Output:** Engagement plan with scheduled meetings and prepared agendas
**Duration:** 2–3 days

### Phase 4: Initial Meetings
**Objective:** Build rapport and establish productive working relationships
**Skills/Agents Used:** `research-analyst`
**Activities:**
- Listen more than you talk (80/20 rule in initial meetings)
- Ask about their goals, challenges, and how product can help
- Understand their communication preferences
- Share your working style and how you like to collaborate
- Ask about past PM relationships: what worked and what didn't
- Identify 1–2 things you can help them with right away
- Take detailed notes and follow up with a summary and any commitments
- Adapt your approach based on each person's style
**Human Checkpoint:** Reflect on initial meetings—adjust engagement plan as needed
**Output:** Initial meetings completed with notes and action items captured
**Duration:** 2–3 weeks

### Phase 5: Nurture & Maintain
**Objective:** Build relationships from transactional to trusted partnership
**Skills/Agents Used:** `comms-chameleon`, `comms-chameleon`
**Activities:**
- Deliver on any commitments made in initial meetings (quick wins)
- Establish regular communication cadence per the engagement plan
- Provide stakeholders with information before they have to ask
- Seek their input on decisions that affect their area
- Give credit publicly; handle disagreements privately
- Adapt communication style to each stakeholder's preferences
- Proactively share relevant customer insights, competitive intel, or data
- Periodically ask for feedback on how the relationship is working
- Update stakeholder map quarterly as people and dynamics change
**Human Checkpoint:** Quarterly stakeholder health check with manager
**Output:** Strong, productive stakeholder relationships with clear communication cadence
**Duration:** Ongoing

## Success Criteria
- All high-influence stakeholders have productive working relationships with PM
- Stakeholders proactively share information and involve PM in relevant decisions
- Cross-functional projects run smoothly without relationship-driven friction
- Stakeholder feedback on PM collaboration is positive
- PM has trusted advisors across multiple functions

## Common Pitfalls
- **All talk, no listen:** The most common mistake is talking too much in initial meetings
- **Ignoring informal influencers:** The org chart doesn't show all the power dynamics
- **Treating all stakeholders equally:** Prioritize based on influence and relevance
- **One-and-done:** Relationships require ongoing investment, not a single meeting
- **Avoiding difficult stakeholders:** The skeptics need the most attention, not the least

## Adaptation Guide
- **New PM at a company:** Phase 1–4 are critical; run the full playbook
- **Existing PM, new initiative:** Focus on Phase 1–3 for stakeholders new to the initiative
- **PM leadership role:** Add skip-level and executive relationship building
- **Remote/distributed:** Over-index on written communication and informal video chats
