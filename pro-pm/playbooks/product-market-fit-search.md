# Product-Market Fit Search

## Overview
Iterative playbook for finding product-market fit in a new product or segment. Covers hypothesis formation, rapid testing, measurement, and validation. Use for early-stage products, new product lines, or entering new markets where PMF is not yet established.

## Prerequisites
- Target market or customer segment identified
- Minimum viable team (product, engineering, design) assembled
- Runway sufficient for 3–6 iteration cycles
- Access to target customers for research and testing
- Leadership aligned that this is an exploration, not a committed roadmap

## Phases

### Phase 1: Hypothesize
**Objective:** Form a clear, testable hypothesis about who needs what and why
**Skills/Agents Used:** `strategy-advisor`, `research-analyst`
**Activities:**
- Define the target customer persona with specificity
- Articulate the core problem hypothesis: "We believe [persona] struggles with [problem] because [reason]"
- Define the value proposition hypothesis: "Our solution helps by [value] through [mechanism]"
- Identify key assumptions that must be true for the hypothesis to work
- Rank assumptions by risk: which, if wrong, would invalidate the entire thesis
- Define lightweight experiments to test the riskiest assumptions first
- Set clear success criteria: what would make you continue vs. pivot
**Human Checkpoint:** Team alignment on hypothesis and experiment plan
**Output:** Documented hypothesis with ranked assumptions and experiment design
**Duration:** 3–5 days

### Phase 2: Test
**Objective:** Run experiments to validate or invalidate key assumptions
**Skills/Agents Used:** `research-analyst`, `spec-writer`
**Activities:**
- Execute customer discovery interviews (minimum 20 for this phase)
- Build and deploy the minimum test: landing page, prototype, concierge MVP, or Wizard of Oz
- Run demand tests: sign-up rates, waitlist conversion, letter of intent
- Test willingness to pay: pricing conversations, pre-orders, pilot agreements
- Observe users interacting with prototype or early product
- Collect qualitative feedback alongside quantitative signals
- Track funnel metrics: awareness → interest → trial → activation → retention
**Human Checkpoint:** Experiment results review—pivot, persevere, or iterate
**Output:** Experiment results with data supporting or contradicting hypothesis
**Duration:** 2–4 weeks per cycle

### Phase 3: Measure
**Objective:** Quantify signals and determine if you're approaching PMF
**Skills/Agents Used:** `data-storyteller`
**Activities:**
- Apply the Sean Ellis test: what % of users would be "very disappointed" without the product
- Track retention curves: is there a flattening cohort or is everyone churning
- Measure engagement: are users coming back without being prompted
- Calculate unit economics: even rough CAC/LTV gives directional signal
- Assess organic growth: referrals, word-of-mouth, unsolicited inbound
- Compare metrics against benchmarks for your stage and market
- Identify your strongest user segment—who loves it most
**Human Checkpoint:** Leadership reviews PMF metrics and decides next step
**Output:** PMF scorecard with metrics, segment analysis, and recommendation
**Duration:** 1 week

### Phase 4: Iterate
**Objective:** Refine the product, positioning, or target based on learnings
**Skills/Agents Used:** `spec-writer`, `research-analyst`
**Activities:**
- If signal is weak: adjust the hypothesis (different persona, problem, or solution)
- If signal is moderate: double down on the strongest segment
- Refine the product based on user feedback and usage data
- Adjust positioning and messaging based on what resonates
- Simplify onboarding to reduce time-to-value
- Remove features that aren't contributing to core value
- Run next experiment cycle with refined hypothesis
- Repeat Phases 1–4 until clear PMF signal or decision to stop
**Human Checkpoint:** Pivot/persevere decision at each iteration boundary
**Output:** Iterated product with refined hypothesis and updated experiment plan
**Duration:** 2–4 weeks per iteration

### Phase 5: Validate
**Objective:** Confirm PMF and prepare for scaling
**Skills/Agents Used:** `strategy-advisor`, `data-storyteller`
**Activities:**
- Demonstrate consistent PMF signals across multiple cohorts
- Show retention curve that flattens (users stick around)
- Show organic growth signal (referrals, word-of-mouth, inbound)
- Confirm unit economics can work at scale (even if not yet optimized)
- Document the ICP, positioning, and core value proposition that's working
- Identify the repeatable acquisition channel
- Transition from exploration mode to execution mode
- Build the scaling plan (use market-expansion playbook for geographic/segment scaling)
**Human Checkpoint:** Leadership confirms PMF achieved and approves scaling investment
**Output:** PMF validation document with evidence, ICP definition, and scaling plan
**Duration:** 2–4 weeks

## Success Criteria
- Sean Ellis score > 40% ("very disappointed" without the product)
- Retention curve flattens with engaged cohort
- Organic growth emerging (referrals, inbound, word-of-mouth)
- Clear ICP identified with repeatable acquisition path
- Unit economics directionally viable
- Team has conviction backed by data

## Common Pitfalls
- **Premature scaling:** Don't scale before PMF is validated; it amplifies problems
- **Vanity metrics:** Sign-ups and downloads aren't PMF; retention and engagement are
- **Confirmation bias:** Actively seek disconfirming evidence; talk to churned users
- **Too many features:** PMF comes from doing one thing exceptionally, not many things adequately
- **Giving up too early:** PMF search is iterative; 3–5 cycles is normal
- **Not narrow enough:** PMF in a niche > no PMF in a broad market

## Adaptation Guide
- **B2B enterprise:** Longer cycles; pilot agreements and expansion revenue are PMF signals
- **B2C consumer:** Faster cycles; viral coefficients and daily engagement are key metrics
- **Platform/API:** Developer adoption and integration depth are PMF signals
- **Marketplace:** Both sides must show PMF signals; monitor liquidity metrics
