# Board Review Prep

## Overview
Playbook for preparing and delivering an effective board of directors product review. Covers metrics assembly, narrative construction, deck creation, rehearsal, and presentation. Use 3–4 weeks before each board meeting.

## Prerequisites
- Board meeting date and agenda confirmed
- Access to company and product metrics dashboards
- Prior board deck available for format continuity
- CEO/CFO expectations for product section clarified
- Key wins and challenges from the quarter identified

## Phases

### Phase 1: Metrics Assembly
**Objective:** Collect and validate all metrics needed for the board narrative
**Skills/Agents Used:** `data-storyteller`, `strategy-advisor`
**Activities:**
- Pull core metrics: revenue, growth rate, retention, churn, NRR, active users
- Compile product-specific metrics: feature adoption, engagement, NPS, time-to-value
- Compare against targets, prior quarter, and year-over-year
- Identify positive trends and areas of concern
- Validate data accuracy with finance and data teams
- Prepare metric visualizations with clear trend lines
**Human Checkpoint:** CFO validates financial metrics; product validates product metrics
**Output:** Validated metrics package with visualizations and comparisons
**Duration:** 3–5 days

### Phase 2: Narrative Construction
**Objective:** Build a compelling story that connects metrics to strategy
**Skills/Agents Used:** `document-builder`, `comms-chameleon`
**Activities:**
- Frame the quarter: what was the thesis, what happened, what we learned
- Highlight 2–3 key wins with metrics and customer proof points
- Address 1–2 challenges honestly with what's being done
- Connect product progress to strategic themes and annual plan
- Articulate forward-looking roadmap highlights for next quarter
- Identify 1–2 strategic questions or decisions for board input
- Draft executive summary (one page)
**Human Checkpoint:** CEO reviews narrative and strategic framing
**Output:** Board narrative document with executive summary
**Duration:** 3–5 days

### Phase 3: Deck Creation
**Objective:** Translate narrative into a clear, concise presentation
**Skills/Agents Used:** `comms-chameleon`
**Activities:**
- Follow board deck template: exec summary, metrics, wins, challenges, outlook
- Keep to 8–12 slides for the product section
- Every slide should answer: so what? why does this matter?
- Include appendix slides for detailed data (reference only if asked)
- Use consistent formatting with company board deck style
- Embed customer quotes or logos for proof points
- Include competitive context where relevant
**Human Checkpoint:** CEO and CFO review final deck
**Output:** Board-ready presentation deck
**Duration:** 3–5 days

### Phase 4: Rehearse
**Objective:** Practice delivery, anticipate questions, and refine
**Skills/Agents Used:** `document-builder`
**Activities:**
- Do a full run-through with the CEO or CPO
- Practice 15-minute delivery (boards hate long presentations)
- Prepare for likely questions: competitive threats, metric misses, resource asks
- Create a "hot seat" document with tough questions and prepared answers
- Refine slides based on rehearsal feedback
- Practice transitions between presenters if multiple speakers
- Time the presentation to ensure it fits the allotted slot
**Human Checkpoint:** CEO confirms presentation is board-ready
**Output:** Rehearsed, refined presentation with Q&A preparation
**Duration:** 2–3 days

### Phase 5: Present & Follow-up
**Objective:** Deliver confidently and capture board feedback
**Skills/Agents Used:** `document-builder`, `comms-chameleon`
**Activities:**
- Present with confidence; let data tell the story
- Take real-time notes on board questions and feedback
- Be honest about unknowns—commit to follow-up rather than guessing
- Capture action items assigned to product
- Distribute follow-up materials within 48 hours
- Track board action items to completion before next meeting
- Debrief with CEO on board reactions and feedback
**Human Checkpoint:** Post-meeting debrief with CEO
**Output:** Board presentation delivered with action items captured and tracked
**Duration:** 1 week (presentation + follow-up)

## Success Criteria
- Presentation completed within allotted time
- Board members engaged with strategic questions, not confused by data
- Key wins and challenges communicated clearly
- Action items captured and tracked
- CEO provides positive feedback on product section quality

## Common Pitfalls
- **Too much detail:** Boards want strategic insight, not feature demos
- **Only good news:** Address challenges proactively; boards respect honesty
- **No ask:** If you need something from the board (resources, decision, intro), ask for it
- **Reading slides:** Present conversationally; slides support the story
- **Ignoring board feedback:** Capture it and follow through, or explain why you chose differently

## Adaptation Guide
- **Series A/B board:** More narrative, less polish; boards expect raw data and learning speed
- **Public company board:** Add compliance, regulatory, and risk sections
- **Advisory board:** More interactive; prepare discussion topics rather than presentations
- **Investor update (non-board):** Written format preferred; use investor-update template
