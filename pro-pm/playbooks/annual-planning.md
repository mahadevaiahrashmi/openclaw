# Annual Planning

## Overview
Strategic planning process for setting the product direction, placing bets, allocating resources, and building the annual roadmap. Run this 6–8 weeks before the fiscal year begins.

## Prerequisites
- Company mission, vision, and multi-year strategy documented
- Current year performance data and OKR results
- Market research and competitive landscape analysis current
- Executive team aligned on company-level strategic priorities
- Finance team available for resource and budget discussions

## Phases

### Phase 1: Strategic Assessment
**Objective:** Evaluate current position and identify strategic opportunities
**Skills/Agents Used:** `strategy-advisor`, `competitive-intel`, `research-analyst`
**Activities:**
- Review annual performance against goals (revenue, growth, retention)
- Conduct SWOT analysis for the product portfolio
- Analyze market trends, TAM shifts, and emerging technologies
- Review competitive landscape and positioning changes
- Synthesize customer feedback themes from the past year
- Identify macro tailwinds and headwinds
**Human Checkpoint:** Leadership alignment on strategic assessment findings
**Output:** Strategic assessment document with market position analysis
**Duration:** 1–2 weeks

### Phase 2: Strategic Bets
**Objective:** Define the big bets and investment themes for the year
**Skills/Agents Used:** `strategy-advisor`, `document-builder`
**Activities:**
- Propose 3–5 strategic themes aligned with company direction
- For each theme, articulate the opportunity, risk, and expected impact
- Evaluate build vs. buy vs. partner options
- Assess resource requirements and capability gaps
- Model scenarios: aggressive, moderate, conservative
- Rank bets by strategic importance and confidence
**Human Checkpoint:** Executive approval of strategic bets and investment themes
**Output:** Strategic bets document with rationale, scenarios, and ranking
**Duration:** 1–2 weeks

### Phase 3: Resource Allocation
**Objective:** Match resources to strategic bets and operational needs
**Skills/Agents Used:** `strategy-advisor`, `quarterly-planner`
**Activities:**
- Inventory current team capacity and planned hiring
- Allocate headcount and budget across strategic themes
- Balance new investment vs. maintenance vs. technical debt
- Identify hiring needs and timeline for new roles
- Plan for infrastructure and tooling investments
- Model trade-offs between competing priorities
**Human Checkpoint:** CFO/CEO approval of resource allocation plan
**Output:** Resource allocation plan with headcount, budget, and hiring timeline
**Duration:** 1 week

### Phase 4: Annual Roadmap
**Objective:** Translate strategic bets into a phased annual roadmap
**Skills/Agents Used:** `spec-writer`, `quarterly-planner`
**Activities:**
- Break each strategic bet into quarterly milestones
- Identify key deliverables and success metrics per quarter
- Map dependencies across teams and external partners
- Define what "done" looks like for each major initiative
- Build in flexibility—detail Q1/Q2, directional Q3/Q4
- Create visual roadmap for communication
**Human Checkpoint:** Product leadership review of annual roadmap
**Output:** Annual roadmap with quarterly milestones and success metrics
**Duration:** 1–2 weeks

### Phase 5: Alignment & Communication
**Objective:** Align the entire organization and communicate the plan
**Skills/Agents Used:** `comms-chameleon`, `document-builder`
**Activities:**
- Present to executive team for final approval
- Share with board of directors (use board-review-prep playbook)
- Communicate to all-hands with vision narrative
- Brief sales, marketing, and CS on annual direction
- Publish internal strategy document
- Set up quarterly planning cadence for the year
**Human Checkpoint:** Board and executive final sign-off
**Output:** Communicated annual plan with organizational alignment
**Duration:** 1 week

## Success Criteria
- Strategy connects company mission to specific product investments
- Resource allocation matches stated priorities
- Roadmap is phased with increasing flexibility in later quarters
- All functions understand the annual direction and their role
- Q1 planning can begin immediately from this foundation

## Common Pitfalls
- **Over-planning Q3/Q4:** The world will change; keep later quarters directional
- **Peanut-buttering resources:** Spreading thin across too many bets dilutes impact
- **Ignoring technical debt:** Allocate explicit capacity or it will steal from features
- **Top-down only:** Combine top-down strategy with bottom-up input from teams
- **No kill criteria:** Define when you'd stop investing in a bet

## Adaptation Guide
- **Series A/B startup:** Focus on 1–2 bets maximum; annual plan may be 6-month horizon
- **Public company:** Add investor narrative and guidance considerations
- **Multi-product:** Run per-product then synthesize portfolio-level plan
- **Regulated industry:** Include compliance roadmap as a strategic theme
