# Market Expansion

## Overview
Playbook for entering a new market—whether a new geography, vertical, segment, or use case. Covers research through scaling. Use when expanding beyond your current served market.

## Prerequisites
- Core product has product-market fit in existing market
- Strategic rationale for expansion documented
- Executive sponsor for the expansion initiative
- Budget allocated for research and initial go-to-market

## Phases

### Phase 1: Market Research
**Objective:** Deeply understand the target market opportunity and requirements
**Skills/Agents Used:** `competitive-intel`, `research-analyst`
**Activities:**
- Size the market: TAM, SAM, SOM for the target segment
- Analyze market dynamics: growth rate, trends, regulatory environment
- Map competitive landscape in the target market
- Identify 10–20 potential customers for research interviews
- Conduct customer discovery interviews to understand needs and buying behavior
- Assess product-market gaps: what's missing for this market
- Evaluate go-to-market channels available in this market
- Identify local or segment-specific requirements (compliance, localization, integrations)
**Human Checkpoint:** Leadership review of market opportunity assessment
**Output:** Market research document with sizing, competitive map, and gap analysis
**Duration:** 2–4 weeks

### Phase 2: Entry Strategy
**Objective:** Define how to enter the market and with what offering
**Skills/Agents Used:** `strategy-advisor`, `document-builder`
**Activities:**
- Choose entry approach: direct, partnership, acquisition, or local team
- Define target ICP (ideal customer profile) for the new market
- Determine pricing and packaging for the market (use new-market-pricing playbook if complex)
- Plan required product adaptations (localization, compliance, integrations)
- Develop go-to-market plan: channels, messaging, and sales motion
- Build financial model: investment required, expected timeline to ROI
- Define success metrics and kill criteria
- Identify key hires or partners needed
**Human Checkpoint:** Executive approval of entry strategy and investment
**Output:** Market entry plan with go-to-market strategy and financial model
**Duration:** 2–3 weeks

### Phase 3: MVP Launch
**Objective:** Launch a minimum viable offering in the new market
**Skills/Agents Used:** `spec-writer`, `launch-coordinator`
**Activities:**
- Build required product adaptations (minimum viable for market entry)
- Recruit 3–5 design partners or early adopters from the target market
- Onboard design partners with white-glove support
- Create market-specific content: case studies, landing pages, documentation
- Set up market-specific support and feedback channels
- Launch to design partners and iterate based on feedback
- Track adoption, engagement, and satisfaction closely
**Human Checkpoint:** Design partner feedback review—ready to expand
**Output:** MVP live with design partners; initial feedback and iterations
**Duration:** 4–8 weeks

### Phase 4: Market Launch
**Objective:** Open the market for general availability
**Skills/Agents Used:** `launch-coordinator`, `comms-chameleon`
**Activities:**
- Incorporate design partner feedback into product
- Execute go-to-market plan: campaigns, events, partnerships, PR
- Hire or assign dedicated sales/CS capacity for the market
- Launch marketing campaigns targeted at the new segment
- Establish presence in market-specific channels (conferences, communities, publications)
- Set up market-specific metrics dashboard
- Build reference customers and case studies
**Human Checkpoint:** Market launch go/no-go based on design partner results
**Output:** Product generally available in new market with GTM in motion
**Duration:** 4–8 weeks

### Phase 5: Scale
**Objective:** Grow the market from initial traction to meaningful revenue
**Skills/Agents Used:** `data-storyteller`, `strategy-advisor`
**Activities:**
- Scale sales and marketing investment based on early results
- Optimize acquisition funnels for the market segment
- Expand product capabilities based on market-specific needs
- Build partnerships and channel relationships
- Develop market-specific expertise within the team
- Monitor unit economics: CAC, LTV, payback period for the new market
- Evaluate need for dedicated market team vs. integrated approach
- Set quarterly OKRs for market growth
**Human Checkpoint:** Quarterly market expansion review with leadership
**Output:** Scaling plan with demonstrated product-market fit in new market
**Duration:** 6–12 months

## Success Criteria
- Product-market fit signals from design partners within first 8 weeks
- Revenue from new market growing quarter-over-quarter
- Unit economics trending toward or better than core market
- Referenceable customers established in the new market
- Clear path to target market share within planned timeframe

## Common Pitfalls
- **Overbuilding for a new market:** Start with MVP adaptations, not a custom product
- **Ignoring local context:** What works in one market may not translate directly
- **Underfunding GTM:** Product readiness without go-to-market investment wastes effort
- **No kill criteria:** Define when you'd exit the market if it's not working
- **Neglecting core market:** Expansion shouldn't cannibalize core market investment

## Adaptation Guide
- **Geographic expansion:** Focus on localization, compliance, and local partnerships
- **Vertical expansion:** Focus on domain expertise, compliance, and industry-specific workflows
- **Segment expansion (e.g., upmarket):** Focus on enterprise features, security, support
- **Use case expansion:** Focus on product adaptations and new user personas
